## 3. Forward guidance

Management raised FY27 net-new-ARR guidance to $1.279B–$1.303B, representing 27–29% growth over FY26 — a ~520bps increase in YoY growth vs. prior guidance (per CFO Podbere, call summary). Q2'27 net-new-ARR guided to $284–286M. Seasonality framing was adjusted to reflect first-half strength, with management citing a "record Q2 pipeline" as the primary confidence anchor. FY27 free-cash-flow margin guided to at least 30%. The guidance philosophy here is notably ambitious rather than conservative — a guidance raise on the first print of the year, attributing the acceleration to an AI-driven structural demand shift (the "Mythos moment" inflection dated to RSA in March). Government/US-executive-order AI-security tailwinds were explicitly described as NOT yet reflected in guidance — a potential upward bias to the FY27 outlook.

## 4. Thesis read by theme

### enterprise_ai_adoption

- **Status:** Confirm
- **Evidence:** Management framed AI adoption as driving structural demand for cybersecurity; CRWD was selected by both Anthropic and OpenAI to secure new frontier AI models, and analysts (Essex/JPM, DiFucci/Guggenheim) probed the linkage between frontier-model adoption and incremental security budget, which Kurtz tied directly to "significant adoption of frontier models leading to incremental security funding" (Q&A, call summary).
- **Implication:** Enterprise AI adoption is acting as a demand multiplier for CRWD rather than a disruption risk — the call positions CRWD as a toll on the AI buildout, strengthening the thesis that AI adoption pulls security spend forward.

### ai_native_vertical

- **Status:** Confirm
- **Evidence:** AIDR (AI Detection & Response) ending ARR grew 250%+ sequentially and management asserts it will be a larger opportunity than EDR due to broader attack surfaces ("high number of agents per employee"); next-gen SIEM surpassed $600M ending ARR, described as "the operating system of the AI SOC"; agentic-identity demand (Falcon Shield ARR ~4x YoY, Signal acquisition) is an emerging pillar (prepared remarks + Boyd/UBS, Gallo/Jefferies Q&A, call summary).
- **Implication:** CRWD is building net-new AI-native product categories (AIDR, AI SOC, agentic identity) that did not exist in the prior thesis framing — this is the strongest evidence for an AI-native expansion vector and supports a potential ai_positioning drift-up.

### cybersecurity_competitive_landscape

- **Status:** Confirm
- **Evidence:** Gartner named CRWD an endpoint-security leader for the seventh consecutive year; management cited strong competitive displacements across next-gen SIEM and Falcon Flex (approaching $2B ending ARR, +99% YoY, 300+ Flex accounts added in Q1); Kurtz's AIDR>EDR TAM claim rests on CRWD's structural advantage in endpoint as the on-ramp to the agentic attack surface (prepared remarks + Boyd/UBS Q&A, call summary).
- **Implication:** CRWD is extending its endpoint moat into adjacent AI-security categories via platform consolidation rather than facing erosion — the competitive position is widening, not narrowing, reinforcing the overall competitive-advantage score.

## 8. Management Q&A flags

- **Government adoption is early / not in guidance (Moskowitz, Mizuho):** Kurtz was constructive on the US executive order on AI security but management explicitly excluded it from current guidance — a sandbag/conservatism flag that biases FY27 upside but also signals the federal ramp is not yet contracted revenue.
- **Agentic identity adoption still early (Gallo/Jefferies; analyst commentary):** Management is confident on positioning (Signal acquisition) but analysts noted companies are not yet fully deploying identity security for agentic workloads — runway acknowledged, but a reminder that AIDR/agentic-identity ARR is early-stage and the 250% sequential growth is off a small base.
- **Pricing-model evolution (Heath/KeyBanc):** On token-based AI pricing, Kurtz deflected disruption risk by asserting Falcon Flex already accommodates it — confident, but worth monitoring whether AI-consumption pricing pressures seat-based economics over time (ties to the software_seat_pricing_pressure theme vocabulary, not currently assigned to CRWD).
- **Net-new-ARR seasonality shift (Tilton/Wolfe):** Management revised seasonality to front-load H1 strength — a change in the shape of the year. Watch whether this pulls demand forward (creating a tougher H2 compare) vs. reflects durable acceleration.
