## 1. Headline read

The Q4'25 / FY25 print is a Confirm of the AI-cloud and Apollo Go growth pillars, with a notable upside Drift on AI Cloud Infrastructure magnitude (143% YoY growth in subscription-based AI accelerator infrastructure) and a positive structural Drift on capital allocation (first-ever dividend + USD5B buyback + Kunlunxin spin-off). Counterweight: full-year revenue declined 3% YoY on legacy ad-business drag, and search disruption pressure remains unresolved — management's narrative has effectively pivoted away from defending search toward building the AI-cloud-plus-Apollo-Go bull case. First earnings-reviewer note on BIDU — no prior thesis-conditioned note to anchor against.

## 3. Forward guidance

Management did not provide quantitative point-estimate guidance for FQ1'26 or FY26 — consistent with prior BIDU practice. Qualitative guide-up signals:
- **AI Cloud:** expected to grow "faster than industry average" in 2026; tied explicitly to "accelerating enterprise AI adoption."
- **AI-powered business share:** management expects the AI-powered business to become "the majority of Baidu General Business in the foreseeable future" (currently 43% of GB).
- **Apollo Go:** "more cities achieving positive unit economics this year" (Wuhan already at breakeven); continued international expansion in UK, Switzerland, Middle East, South Korea, Dubai, Hong Kong.
- **Capital allocation:** new USD 5B buyback program announced; first-ever dividend policy adopted; Kunlunxin spin-off and separate listing planned.
- **AI investment density:** management committed to maintaining the post-March-2023 investment rate (>RMB 100B cumulative); CFO He flagged "exploring alternate financing methods to support AI investments" — a signal worth tracking for off-balance-sheet vehicles.

Tone: confident on AI Cloud and Apollo Go; defensive on consumer-AI monetization (Luo acknowledged "highly competitive" AI2C market); pragmatic on legacy ad business (no defense attempted). The absence of revenue point estimates is normal for BIDU but means the FactSet FQ1'26 SALES consensus of ~RMB 30.5B implied (n=18, May-01 snapshot) is the operative Street model — Q1'26 will be reported on or around mid-May 2026, with no future earnings date currently in InsiderScore's queue.

FactSet sell-side consensus snapshot (2026-05-15): **OVERWEIGHT (1.28)** — 26 buy + 4 overweight + 5 hold + 1 underweight + 1 sell from 37 analysts. Ratings have been stable since the Q4 print (1.29 → 1.28 over ~30 days).

## 4. Thesis read by theme

### search_disruption

- **Status:** Drift (cautious — thesis intact but management has pivoted narrative)
- **Evidence:** Management did not defend the legacy search advertising business in any meaningful way; FY25 revenue down 3% YoY explicitly attributed to "decline in legacy business." Consumer-AI monetization framing (Luo Q&A) was forward-looking and aspirational — "prioritizing product excellence and user experience" with monetization "still at an early stage" despite >200M MAU on ERNIE Assistant + Baidu app. Search-as-a-revenue-pillar got no airtime; Baidu's response to search disruption is to substitute AI-cloud + Apollo Go, not to defend the core. (Prepared remarks; Zhuang BofA Q&A)
- **Implication:** The search_disruption thesis as a *pressure on BIDU's revenue base* is confirmed — search is being cannibalized, both by external AI competitors and by Baidu's own AI products that have not yet monetized. The Drift is on the *substitution path*: BIDU's bet is that AI Cloud + Apollo Go scale faster than legacy decline. The risk: the 200M MAU consumer-AI base remains pre-revenue, and management's tone suggests monetization is years not quarters away. Watch for any signal that AI-native ad load or agentic-commerce take rates inside ERNIE Assistant begin to compound.

### china_ai_infrastructure_demand

- **Status:** Confirm (strong)
- **Evidence:** AI Cloud Infra FY25 revenue RMB 30B (+34% YoY); subscription-based AI accelerator infrastructure +143% YoY in Q4 (highest-cited growth metric in the call). Management explicitly cited "Baidu's proprietary Kunlunxin AI chips deployed at scale" as the structural advantage. Cloud expected to grow "faster than industry average" in 2026. Yao (JPMorgan) Q&A confirmed enterprise AI adoption is the demand driver. (Prepared remarks; Yao Q&A; AI Cloud sentiment paragraph)
- **Implication:** The China-AI-infra demand thesis is unambiguously confirmed and accelerating. The 143% YoY subscription growth is a leading indicator that domestic enterprise adoption is in an early-S-curve phase. The Kunlunxin spin-off thesis derives directly from this confirmation: the chip business is now generating enough cloud-revenue signal to justify a standalone listing. Read-through to BABA and other China-cloud peers is positive (consistent with the BABA 4Q26 read in adjacent notes).

### autonomous_vehicle_competition

- **Status:** Confirm with positive Drift on geographic scope
- **Evidence:** Apollo Go: 3.4M fully-driverless rides in Q4 (+200% YoY); 10M+ rides FY25; cumulative 20M+ through Feb 2026; 26 cities globally; peak weekly rides >300K. New market entries: UK, Switzerland, Middle East, South Korea, Dubai, Hong Kong. Wuhan breakeven on unit economics; "more cities achieving positive unit economics this year." Strategic partnerships with Uber and Lyft for international expansion. Claims lowest cost per mile and industry-leading safety. Yu (Morgan Stanley) Q&A confirmed flexibility on "strategic options for Apollo Go to maximize long-term shareholder returns" (i.e. spin-off option live). (Prepared remarks; Yu Q&A; Apollo Go sentiment paragraph)
- **Implication:** Drift is positive on the geographic-scope axis — Apollo Go is now competing globally rather than as a China-only story, with partnership-led entry into Western markets (UK, Switzerland) that historically have been Waymo / Cruise territory. The Uber/Lyft partnerships are an asset-light scaling model that competitive-positions Apollo Go differently from Waymo (own-fleet) and Tesla (Robotaxi-on-FSD). The unit-economics signal (Wuhan breakeven, more cities targeted) is the gating item for a credible spin-off valuation. Competitive read-through: TSLA's Robotaxi narrative has a credible non-US challenger at scale; WAYMO (private) faces a global competitor for the first time.

### ai_re_architected_incumbent

- **Status:** Confirm
- **Evidence:** Baidu Core AI-powered business revenue Q4 >RMB 11B (43% of Baidu General Business), expected to become "the majority of Baidu General Business in the foreseeable future." AI applications FY25 revenue >RMB 10B. Integration of Baidu Wenku and Baidu Drive into the PSIG business group cited as a re-architecting initiative. ERNIE 5.0 released with organizational restructure (separate teams for frontier R&D vs. application-specific adaptation) — Li (Yap Q&A) emphasized "application-driven approach" as the operating philosophy. Full-stack end-to-end AI architecture with Kunlunxin chip integration cited as the competitive moat. (Prepared remarks; Yap Q&A; Kong Goldman Q&A)
- **Implication:** Confirm — BIDU has converted ~43% of its core revenue mix to AI-powered streams, ahead of where most legacy-internet peers sit. The "application-driven model strategy" framing is differentiated from the foundation-model-first approach of competitors and gives BIDU a credible monetization narrative even where ERNIE is not the technical leader. The re-architecting is real and revenue-visible, not just slideware. Trajectory: if AI-powered share crosses 50% in 2026, that is the formal inflection point where the incumbent transformation thesis "completes."

## 5. AI positioning signal

- **Current score:** (not populated in watchlist — BIDU's schema attributes are not populated)

A 4 captures the "strong, vertically integrated, but second-tier on raw model capability" reality. A 4- could be argued if the operator wants to weight model leadership more heavily.

## 6. Competitive advantage signal

- **Overall** (current: not populated)

But it is not 4+ or 5 because (a) the consumer-AI monetization remains unproven, (b) the cloud business, while growing fast, is still smaller than BABA's, and (c) the legacy search business is in clear decline.

## 8. Management Q&A flags

- **Yap (Citibank) — ERNIE 5.0 strategic rationale:** Li deflected the model-leadership question by emphasizing "applications over models." The phrase "applications over models" is itself the tell — BIDU's CEO is no longer arguing ERNIE will be the model winner, and is reframing the bull case around the application stack. Important narrative shift that the buy-side should price into model-leadership comparisons. Not a dodge, but a strategic concession.
- **Yao (JPMorgan) — Cloud growth drivers 2026:** Shen gave a detailed and confident answer on subscription accelerator growth (+143%) and the Kunlunxin integration — this is the most data-rich part of the call and is the segment that justifies the bull case. No sandbagging, no hedging.
- **Xiong (UBS) — Capital allocation and Kunlunxin spin-off:** He's response was uncharacteristically detailed for a Chinese-ADR CFO discussing capital return — explicit references to disclosures, buyback execution discipline, and Kunlunxin spin-off mechanics. Read as a deliberate signal to international institutional investors. Worth watching for follow-through in the next call.
- **Jiang (Macquarie) — AI CapEx, ROIC, and "alternate financing methods":** He's reference to "exploring alternate financing methods to support AI investments" is a flag. This phrasing typically precedes either off-balance-sheet vehicles, structured financing tied to specific data center assets, or external partner-funded capex. Operator should track for follow-up disclosures.
- **Zhuang (BofA) — Consumer AI monetization:** Luo's answer was the weakest defensive answer of the call. "Highly competitive," "prioritizing product excellence," "monetization at early stage" — three phrases that together signal management does not have a credible near-term monetization roadmap for the 200M MAU. The consumer-AI revenue pillar is therefore aspirational for 2026/2027.
