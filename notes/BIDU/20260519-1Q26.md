Versus the 4Q25 read (Confirm/Drift on the same pillars), this print escalates conviction on AI Cloud and accelerates the AI-revenue-share timeline.

## 3. Forward guidance

Management again did not provide quantitative point-estimate guidance for Q2'26 or FY26 — consistent with prior BIDU practice. Qualitative guide-up signals:

- **AI Cloud:** "Continued strong demand," management "confident in ability to capture opportunities as AI adoption accelerates"; explicit pivot toward GPU Cloud share of mix, which supports "durable margin improvement." No revenue magnitude commentary beyond Q1'26 actuals.
- **Apollo Go:** International expansion "expected to contribute to improved profitability" given "more favorable pricing environments" overseas vs. China; new launches in London, Switzerland, Dubai; rental partnerships in Hainan. Wuhan remains the only unit-economics-breakeven city explicitly cited, but management implied additional cities are approaching breakeven.
- **AI agents / monetization:** Li framed a forward narrative that monetization is shifting "from token-based pricing to more results-oriented approaches" — a strategic signal that BIDU expects to monetize agents on outcome-based pricing as agentic capability matures. Important framing for the agentic_commerce thematic adjacency.
- **CapEx (2026):** He (CFO) responded to Jefferies' Chong on CapEx with a "strategic investment approach" framing — no quantitative number disclosed, but the prior-call "alternate financing methods" hint was not retracted. Operator should continue tracking for off-balance-sheet vehicles.
- **Capital allocation:** Buyback program and dividend policy active; dual primary listing in Hong Kong "flexible," no concrete update — slight de-escalation vs. 4Q25 tone where Kunlunxin spin-off and HK listing were both more aggressively flagged.

Tone: strongly upbeat (InsiderScore sentiment: Slightly Positive, consistent with prior call; sentiment scoring note that the model is "more skeptical of generic optimism" implies the underlying tone was likely Positive). Zero challenging analyst exchanges flagged. The Q1'26 print is the operational follow-through on the 4Q25 narrative pivot — management is no longer defending search, they are executing the AI-substitution playbook with visible revenue numbers.

## 4. Thesis read by theme

### search_disruption

- **Status:** Drift (positive — substitution path now revenue-visible)
- **Evidence:** Baidu Core AI-powered business revenue RMB 13.6B (+49% YoY) crossed >50% of Baidu General Business for the first time — the inflection point flagged in the 4Q25 note as the formal "incumbent transformation completes" marker. Management again offered no defense of legacy search; the AI-substitution playbook is fully operational. ERNIE 5.1 launched with top Chinese rankings on text/search leaderboards (#4 globally) — BIDU is now competing in AI Search as a feature within ERNIE Assistant, not as a defended legacy product. Zhuang (BofA) Q&A on AI agent monetization: Li discussed multiple monetization models (subscription, token, project-based) and the prospective shift to "results-oriented" pricing — a real-time framing of how the search-revenue-replacement engine works. (Prepared remarks; Zhuang Q&A; Yap Q&A on ERNIE)
- **Implication:** The 4Q25 read flagged the risk that BIDU's 200M consumer-AI MAU base remained pre-revenue and that monetization was "years not quarters away." This print materially de-risks that concern at the *enterprise* and *AI applications* layer (RMB 13.6B AI-powered business revenue is large) but **does not** resolve it at the consumer-AI 2C layer — Li still framed consumer-AI monetization as evolving. Net: the search-disruption thesis as a pressure on BIDU's revenue base is reframed from "imminent risk" to "in-progress substitution" with the substitution path delivering. Watch Q2'26 for whether AI-powered share of BGB pushes further past 55%, and whether DuMate / Miaoda / Famou Agent revenue starts to surface as discrete disclosure items.

### china_ai_infrastructure_demand

- **Status:** Confirm (strong — accelerating)
- **Evidence:** AI Cloud Infrastructure revenue +79% YoY in Q1'26 (vs. +34% YoY in FY25 and the +143% YoY subscription accelerator metric cited in 4Q25 — the +79% is the consolidated AI Cloud Infra line, which is the more comparable apples-to-apples metric). GPU Cloud subsegment +184% YoY — the second derivative remains explosive. Shen (JPMorgan Q&A) described demand as "surging" and "broad-based" with wins across new industries and top-tier client expansion. Kunlunxin chips deployed at "clusters of over 30,000 accelerators" commercially — a discrete operational metric not previously disclosed at this magnitude. Kong (Goldman) Q&A reinforced that the domestic AI chip supply chain remains constrained — "demand growing faster than supply" — and that BIDU's full-stack integration is a competitive moat. (Prepared remarks; Shen Q&A; Kong Q&A; AI Cloud Infra sentiment paragraph)
- **Implication:** China AI infra demand thesis is unambiguously accelerating, not merely confirmed. The +79% AI Cloud Infra growth rate in Q1'26 is one of the strongest reported numbers in the China-internet group this earnings cycle and meaningfully exceeds the FY25 exit growth rate. The GPU Cloud +184% subsegment specifically signals that BIDU is winning enterprise compute share at the high end (inference workloads on Kunlunxin) — this is the highest-margin and most defensible part of the cloud stack. Read-through: positive for BABA cloud growth narrative (consistent with the BABA 4Q26 note in the repo); cautiously positive for TCEHY; and a signal that domestic AI chip share (Kunlunxin, Huawei Ascend, etc.) is gaining traction at the expense of NVDA's China-allowed SKUs given the export-control overlay.

### autonomous_vehicle_competition

- **Status:** Confirm (with operational milestone progression)
- **Evidence:** Apollo Go 3.2M fully-driverless rides in Q1'26 (vs. 3.4M in Q4'25 — slight QoQ softening but +triple-digit YoY); cumulative 22M+ rides through April (vs. 20M+ through Feb cited in 4Q25 — implying ~2M rides over Feb-April, indicating sustained ~600-700K/month run rate). New international launches: London, Switzerland, Dubai. Wuhan remains the unit-economics breakeven city; management anticipates "stronger profitability in overseas markets due to more favorable pricing environments" — a useful framing that suggests Apollo Go international economics may be structurally better than China at the unit level. Yu (Morgan Stanley) Q&A: Li indicated Apollo Go's role in the robotaxi ecosystem is "still evolving" — a phrase that maintains the spin-off optionality flagged in the 4Q25 note without committing. (Prepared remarks; Yu Q&A; Robotaxi sentiment paragraph)
- **Implication:** Confirm — the AV thesis trajectory remains intact. The 3.2M Q1 rides figure (vs. 3.4M Q4'25) is a slight sequential softening that the operator should note — could be seasonal, could be deliberate slowdown in low-margin geographies as BIDU shifts toward international markets with better unit economics, could be capacity-constrained as fleet redeploys. The international launches in London, Switzerland, and Dubai are the operational follow-through on the 4Q25 announcements; execution is on schedule. The "overseas more profitable" framing is new and worth tracking — if Apollo Go London / Switzerland unit economics actually print materially above Wuhan, the spin-off valuation case gets meaningfully stronger. Competitive read-through: TSLA Robotaxi narrative continues to face a credible non-US scale challenger; Waymo (private) still owns the US, but BIDU is now competing in markets historically considered Western incumbent turf.

### ai_re_architected_incumbent

- **Status:** Confirm (inflection crossed)
- **Evidence:** Baidu Core AI-powered business revenue RMB 13.6B (+49% YoY) is **>50% of Baidu General Business for the first time** — the formal inflection point flagged in the 4Q25 note ("if AI-powered share crosses 50% in 2026, that is the formal inflection point where the incumbent transformation thesis 'completes'"). This crossed in Q1'26, ahead of the implicit 4Q25 timeline. Product portfolio cited: ERNIE models, DuMate (AI agent), Digital Humans, Miaoda (vibe coding), Famou Agent — discrete products with discrete metrics (Miaoda MAU +70% QoQ; Digital Humans cost -80%). Monetization diversifying across subscription, token, and project-based models with future shift toward outcome-based pricing. Li (Yap Q&A) reiterated the "application-driven approach" framing from 4Q25; the strategy is consistent and now backed by revenue. (Prepared remarks; Yap Q&A; AI Applications sentiment paragraph)
- **Implication:** Confirm — and the thesis is materially de-risked. The 4Q25 read described BIDU as having "converted ~43% of its core revenue mix to AI-powered streams"; one quarter later that number is >50% with sequential acceleration in product-level metrics. The "incumbent transformation thesis" is no longer aspirational; it is operating. Trajectory: if AI-powered share continues to compound at 49% YoY pace, by exit-2026 it could be ~60% of BGB. The remaining open question is durability — are these revenues structurally recurring (subscription + token at scale) or one-off project bookings? The diverse monetization mix Li described suggests structural, but the operator should request product-level revenue disclosure on the next call to validate.

  - Reasoning: BIDU's competitive position has demonstrably strengthened on the model-innovation axis between Q4'25 and Q1'26 in a way that is hard to dismiss — ERNIE 5.1 rankings, Kunlunxin deployment scale, AI applications cost-curve velocity, and the AI-powered revenue inflection are mutually reinforcing. Not 4+ because (a) consumer-AI monetization remains unproven at the 2C revenue line, (b) the legacy search business decline is still a drag, and (c) the geopolitical overlay is still material. ## 9. Vocabulary candidates

- **"Results-oriented" or "outcome-based" AI pricing** (Li, Zhuang Q&A) — explicit framing that monetization will shift from token-based to outcome-based as agentic capability matures. Crosses `ai_agent_monetization` and `inference_compute_economics`. - **"Vibe coding" (Miaoda product)** — the consumer-facing label for an AI agent that generates code from natural language prompts. Cultural/marketing term that may become genre-defining if Miaoda scales. Worth tracking against parallel offerings (Cursor, Lovable, Replit Agents).
- **"Full-stack AI capabilities"** (reiterated, with additional specificity around Kunlunxin at 30K+ accelerators) — strengthening of the 4Q25 "full-stack end-to-end AI architecture" vocabulary. The 30K-accelerator deployment unit is itself novel and may become a competitive disclosure benchmark for other AI infra players.
- **"Token-based to results-oriented" monetization shift** — this is the most strategically interesting linguistic shift in the call. If picked up by other agentic-AI vendors in subsequent calls, this is the leading indicator that the AI revenue model is bifurcating away from compute-input pricing toward business-outcome pricing. Material implication for the inference_compute_economics theme.
