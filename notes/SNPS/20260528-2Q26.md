_Prepared by earnings-reviewer, 2026-05-28. Tier: T1 (BCTK). First observation — no prior earnings notes on file for SNPS._

Solid beat-and-raise quarter with thesis-confirming signals across the EDA core, even as the Design IP segment continues to work through its bottoming process. Management raised full-year FY26 guidance across revenue, op margin, EPS, and FCF; called out agentic AI / multi-physics fusion as the next leg of monetization; and reported early proof points on Ansys integration synergies. No discernible thesis breaks; the IP segment underperformance and China demand caveats are watch-items but consistent with the prior framing on china_export_controls and chip_design_competition.

## 3. Forward guidance

Raised FY26 across the board:
- Revenue: $9.625B–$9.705B (raised; magnitude of raise not quantified in summary text, but explicitly characterized as a raise)
- Non-GAAP op margin: 41% at midpoint (+50 bps vs prior guide)
- Non-GAAP EPS: $14.72–$14.80
- Free cash flow: ~$2.0B (+$100M vs prior guide)

## 4. Thesis read by theme

### foundry_capacity

- **Status:** Confirm
- **Evidence:** Pharr (BofA) Q&A on Intel 18A/14A pipeline — Ghazi noted that "new foundry technologies benefit Synopsys's IP business and that production commitments would drive revenue." Prepared remarks called out "advanced node solutions" strength in EDA and "first HBM4 IP test chip delivered."
- **Implication:** Foundry roadmap progression (Intel 18A/14A, TSM advanced nodes) directly pulls SNPS EDA and IP volumes higher. Every new process node is a content-uplift event for SNPS, and the 18A/14A specific call-out is a confirming data point that the foundry side of the thesis remains intact.

### silicon_architecture_competition

- **Status:** Confirm
- **Evidence:** Multiple references to "increased alignment with hyperscaler demand for custom AI silicon," ">150 lifetime UCIe wins (64-gig on 2nm)," PCIe 7.0 IP with ">90% win rate, 18 new licenses." DeGasperi Q&A: Ghazi "emphasized the need for customized IP" for hyperscaler silicon.
- **Implication:** Hyperscaler custom-silicon proliferation (a key driver for AVGO/MRVL/etc.) directly pulls demand for SNPS's advanced-node IP catalog. The 90%+ PCIe 7.0 win rate is a strong data point that SNPS retains IP-leadership in the interconnects that matter for AI accelerators. Architecture proliferation is good for tool/IP sales.

### china_export_controls

- **Status:** Drift (mildly negative)
- **Evidence:** Tilton (Wolfe) Q&A on geographic performance — Ghazi noted "no significant changes in regional performance," but the AI-summary regional commentary explicitly flagged China growth as "largely optical" — sequential growth driven by Ansys addition + easier comps, with "underlying design-start activity remains constrained due to ongoing restrictions."
- **Implication:** Export controls continue to suppress organic China design activity. Management is framing the reported China growth optically to avoid drawing attention, which suggests headwinds persist. Not a thesis break — SNPS is managing through it — but worth tracking whether the optical/structural gap widens. The "non-semi sectors (aerospace, defense, automotive)" strength callout in regions is interesting — Ansys is doing the heavy lifting on geographic diversification.

### ai_infrastructure_capex

- **Status:** Confirm
- **Evidence:** "Expansion of AI is driving semiconductor demand, increasing architectural diversity and complexity." 3DIC Compiler "production scale adoption by leading HPC providers." Agentic EDA being evaluated by 20 customers with >25 AI agents in use. Quatrochi (Wells) Q&A: Ghazi explicitly tied revenue outlook upside to "AI semiconductor opportunities and the need for EDA software."
- **Implication:** SNPS is a direct beneficiary of the AI infrastructure capex cycle, but through a higher-margin software layer than the silicon/system names. The agentic EDA messaging suggests management thinks they can expand EDA's share of customer R&D budgets — that would be a thesis-extension rather than just thesis-confirmation. Watch for hard $ framing at the September Investor Day.

### lithography_roadmap

- **Status:** Confirm
- **Evidence:** Strength in "advanced node solutions" in EDA. 64-gig UCIe on 2nm, HBM4 IP test chip delivery, 3DIC Compiler production adoption. Implicitly aligned with ASML/TSM/INTC advanced-node ramps.
- **Implication:** SNPS rides the leading-edge roadmap — each new node generation increases EDA complexity and IP licensing value. The 3D-IC and advanced packaging emphasis is the natural extension as classical scaling slows. Confirms the lithography thesis read-through to SNPS.

### chip_design_competition

- **Status:** Confirm
- **Evidence:** Pharr Q&A flagged "new investors and board members" — Ghazi noted alignment "on value creation and monetization opportunities." Management explicitly described new business models with hyperscalers for IP, and >30 full-flow technical wins in EDA. Multi-physics fusion claims 3x faster design closure and 2x faster analog turnaround.
- **Implication:** SNPS holds the picks-and-shovels position in chip design competition — the more architectures compete, the more EDA and IP they need. The new-business-model commentary for IP (capturing more value from custom/high-demand IP) suggests management is repositioning IP from a volume-licensed commodity toward a value-priced strategic input. If executed, this is a margin-positive thesis extension. The "new investors and board members" reference (likely Elliott or similar) is a meaningful governance signal worth tracking — activists driving monetization discipline is bullish at the margin.

## 8. Management Q&A flags

1. **IP segment — repeated skepticism but no breakthrough push-back.** Mizuho, Wells, KeyBanc, BofA, and Wolfe all probed the IP recovery in some form. Ghazi consistently pivoted to "new business models with hyperscalers" as the recovery driver rather than reaffirming the legacy model. This is a meaningful narrative shift — management is implicitly conceding the legacy IP licensing model is structurally challenged and replacing the bull case with a hyperscaler-custom-IP narrative. Watch whether sell-side accepts this re-framing. Prior call (Q1'26) had challenging exchanges from Charles Shi (Needham) and Kelsey Chia (Citi) on the same topic — IP is the open wound.

2. **Multi-physics fusion — careful expectation management.** Vruwink (Baird) Q&A: Ghazi discussed sign-off and multi-physics integration; Glaser explicitly noted "expected ebb and flow of backlog due to renewal timing" — a hedge. Mobley (Loop) Q&A: Ghazi projected revenue synergies "starting in 2027" — pushing the hard numbers to next fiscal year. Management is deliberately not letting analysts anchor on 2026 multi-physics revenue. Sensible, but the 2027 anchor is doing a lot of work.

3. **New investors / board members.** Pharr (BofA) Q&A — Ghazi's answer ("alignment with new board members on value creation and monetization opportunities") is the kind of measured language used when management is being pushed harder by an activist/large new shareholder than they want to admit publicly. The "potential for improved efficiency and profitability" language is the operative tell. Worth watching for 13D filings or board composition disclosures.

4. **Headcount reductions.** Vleeschhouwer (Griffin) Q&A — Glaser confirmed "ongoing headcount reductions" toward a targeted 10% reduction (majority already completed). Balanced with strategic hires. This is consistent with synergy-realization narrative but worth noting the absolute scale — 10% headcount reduction at a company of SNPS's size is a meaningful productivity step.

5. **China — optical vs. organic dodge.** Tilton (Wolfe) Q&A — Ghazi gave the "no significant changes in regional performance" non-answer. The AI-summary independently flagged China growth as "largely optical" from Ansys inclusion. This is a soft dodge worth tracking — if export controls tighten further, the optical vs. organic gap becomes harder to mask.
