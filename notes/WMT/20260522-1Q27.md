# WMT — 1Q27 earnings read

_Prepared by earnings-reviewer, 2026-05-22. Tier: T3._

## 1. Headline read

First observation for WMT in the notes system — no prior earnings note to anchor against. The Q1 FY27 print (call held 2026-05-21) is a clean **Confirm** at the headline level: revenue $177.8B (+7.3% reported, +5.9% cc) beat consensus by ~$3.3B (~1.9%), adjusted EPS $0.66 was in line with the $0.658 mean, and the four BCTK themes assigned to WMT (agentic_commerce, local_commerce_delivery, ai_re_architected_incumbent, ad_market_strength) all received reinforcing data points — most notably global advertising +37% (Walmart Connect +44% ex-VIZIO), Marketplace sales up ~50% (best in 10 quarters), and expedited deliveries (under 3 hours) at ~36% of store-fulfilled orders. The notable softness is a 250 bps drag on op income from higher fuel costs in distribution and fulfillment, which kept reported op income growth (+5.0%) below the multi-year aspiration of "op income grows faster than sales."

## 3. Forward guidance

**Q2 FY27 (consolidated, cc):**
- Net sales: +4.0% to +5.0%
- Adjusted operating income: +7.0% to +10.0% cc
- Adjusted EPS: $0.72 to $0.74 (vs. Q2 FY26 base of $0.68)

**FY27 (full year): unchanged from prior 2026-02-19 guidance:**
- Net sales (cc): no specific range cited in press release excerpt; the baseline reference figures are FY26 net sales $706.4B, adjusted op income $31.0B, adjusted EPS $2.64
- Adjusted EPS: $2.75 to $2.85 (unchanged)
- Effective tax rate: 23.5% to 24.5% (unchanged)
- CapEx: ~3.5% of net sales (unchanged)
- Guidance explicitly does **not** assume any impact from IEEPA tariff refunds

**Tone:** confident but disciplined. The 7-10% adjusted op income guide for Q2 vs. 5% reported for Q1 implies management expects fuel cost drag to abate and higher-margin streams (ad, marketplace, membership) to continue layering. The decision to reiterate (not raise) FY27 despite the Q1 beat is consistent with WMT's historical philosophy of beat-and-maintain early in the year — a sandbag posture rather than a confidence signal in either direction. Worth noting the explicit IEEPA tariff carve-out: management is signaling potential upside if refunds materialize but is not banking on them.

## 4. Thesis read by theme

### agentic_commerce

- **Status:** Confirm (with caveat — no direct narrative on agentic shopping, but the underlying digital commerce infrastructure is strengthening)
- **Evidence:** "Global eCommerce sales grew 26%, led by store-fulfilled pickup delivery and marketplace... Marketplace sales up nearly 50% (best performance in 10 quarters)... expedited deliveries (under 3 hours) represented ~36% of store-fulfilled orders" (Q1 highlights, 8-K iacc 43344379). The only literal mention of "agentic platforms" is in the risk-factor boilerplate forward-looking statements section — not in management commentary.
- **Implication:** Marketplace acceleration to +50% and a 26% global eCom growth rate are the infrastructure prerequisites for agentic commerce — broader assortment + faster fulfillment + structured product data. WMT has the rails but is not yet narrating an agentic-shopping product story in the press release. Should track whether transcript Q&A (once ingested) elevates Sparky / agentic-commerce integrations with Perplexity, OpenAI, or others; absence of such narrative in the prepared remarks is a slight Drift signal vs. the most aggressive agentic-commerce thesis framing.

### local_commerce_delivery

- **Status:** Confirm
- **Evidence:** "Using stores, achieved faster delivery speeds on broader assortment; expedited deliveries (under 3 hours) represented ~36% of store-fulfilled orders" (Walmart U.S. segment commentary, 8-K iacc 43344379). eCommerce contribution to Walmart U.S. comp was ~530 bps in Q1 FY27, up from ~350 bps in Q1 FY26 — a 180 bps Y/Y increase in digital comp contribution.
- **Implication:** WMT's store-as-fulfillment-node model is working. The under-3-hours expedited share of 36% is a hard operational data point — they're approaching DASH/UBER speed on a fundamentally different cost structure (stores already exist, no marginal real estate). The 180 bps Y/Y step-up in eCom contribution to comp is the most direct quantification of the local commerce shift inflecting. Read-through to DASH (T2) is mixed: WMT is a competitive threat to last-mile pure-plays but also a customer (still uses third-party delivery networks). Read-through to AMZN local-delivery ambitions: WMT's store footprint advantage in non-coastal U.S. remains structurally hard to replicate.

### ai_re_architected_incumbent

- **Status:** Drift (slightly weak)
- **Evidence:** John Furner's CEO quote: "Our teams are adopting innovative technologies, driving productivity through automation, and growing higher-margin commerce solutions." (8-K iacc 43344379). The press release language on AI is generic — "innovative technologies" and "automation" rather than specific product or capability narrative. The only literal "artificial intelligence" reference in the 8-K is in the risk-factor boilerplate.
- **Implication:** WMT is doing the work (operating expense deleveraged only 56 bps in U.S. despite higher depreciation and healthcare — suggesting productivity offsets are real) but not narrating it in the AI-rearchitecture frame that the thesis assumes. This is a slight Drift: the financials say AI/automation is contributing, but the marketing language is still in the "omnichannel + tech-powered" framing of two years ago, not the "AI-native" or "Sparky agent" framing that would more aggressively support the ai_re_architected_incumbent classification. The transcript Q&A, once ingested, will likely have more specific AI commentary — analysts have been pushing the question every call for ~6 quarters.

### ad_market_strength

- **Status:** Confirm (strong)
- **Evidence:** "Global advertising business up 37%, with strength across segments. Walmart U.S. advertising up 36%... Walmart Connect (ex-VIZIO) +44%... Advertising business [International] grew 32%, driven by continued momentum at Flipkart" (8-K iacc 43344379). Walmart Connect ex-VIZIO at +44% indicates the core retail-media ad product is accelerating independent of the VIZIO acquisition contribution.
- **Implication:** This is the cleanest Confirm in the print. Ad growth at +37% global / +44% Walmart Connect core is meaningfully above what we'd expect from a pure-play retail-media comp, and is decoupled from comp-sales drag (4.1% U.S. comp does not constrain a 36% U.S. ad business). Material read-throughs: positive for TTD (T3) on ad inventory expansion and CTV via VIZIO; positive for AMZN advertising narrative validation (similar retail-media playbook); mildly cautionary for META/GOOGL on ad share migration to retail-media networks as the structural alternative. WMT advertising is now a high-growth, high-margin business with sufficient scale to materially influence consolidated operating margin trajectory.

## 6. Competitive advantage signal

- **Innovation rate** (current: _empty — schema gap_)
  - Evidence: Marketplace growth accelerating to +50%, expedited delivery share at 36%, advertising +37% — these are operational metrics showing the company is iterating quickly on the parts of the business it owns. But the language is conservative ("innovative technologies"); no breakthrough product announcement.
  - Recommendation: initialize at **3+** — strong execution on existing playbook (omni-retail) but not pushing the frontier on agentic/AI commerce architecture.
- **Distribution** (current: _empty — schema gap_)
  - Evidence: ~36% of U.S. households are Walmart+ members (implied from "record Q1 net adds"), 4,600+ U.S. stores serving as fulfillment nodes, Walmart Connect ad business at $billions-scale, International Flipkart momentum (eCom +27% International).
  - Recommendation: initialize at **5** — the distribution moat is on par with AMZN's. Stores + Walmart+ + ad surface area is a uniquely strong asset combination, especially in non-coastal U.S. demographics.
- **Overall** (current: _empty — schema gap_)
  - Recommendation: initialize at **4** — the distribution dominates the equation. Innovation is "good enough" because the distribution monetizes any reasonable execution. Same shape as AMZN's competitive advantage profile but with the inverse: AMZN's distribution is digital-first with physical extensions; WMT's is physical-first with digital extensions. For the businesses WMT is actually in (mass grocery, mass general merchandise, retail media, basic eCom), this is a 4-quality overall position. Would drop to 3+ if agentic-commerce thesis breaks WMT — but no evidence of that yet.
  - Reasoning: not a mechanical average. Innovation rate of 3+ × Distribution of 5 produces an Overall of 4, not 4+, because for retail-incumbent businesses the innovation tax compounds — competitors that beat WMT on AI-native discovery don't need WMT's distribution to take share at the discovery layer.
