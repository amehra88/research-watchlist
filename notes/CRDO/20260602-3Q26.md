## 4. Thesis read by theme

### networking_competitive_landscape

- **Status:** Confirm
- **Evidence:** Management defended AEC as "the de facto standard for intra-rack and rack-to-rack connectivity up to 7 meters," citing ~half the power of optical modules and superior reliability. On the most challenging exchange (Vivek Arya / BofA, re NVIDIA's investments in optical peers), Brennan positioned AEC and optics as complementary, with reliability, power efficiency and TCO as the differentiators rather than a head-to-head loss. CPO/NPO framed as deferred until they can match AEC/ZeroFlap reliability (Svanberg, Rakesh Q&A).
- **Implication:** The competitive frame holds — CRDO is winning on a system-level reliability/power argument, not pure price. The watchlist networking thesis is intact; the live risk to monitor is the optical/CPO substitution narrative that NVIDIA's optical-peer investments could accelerate, which management addressed but did not dismiss.

### ai_infrastructure_capex

- **Status:** Confirm
- **Evidence:** Revenue tripled YoY on "structural demand from hyperscalers"; FY27 guide of >50% YoY growth toward ~$2B; ZeroFlap Optics ramp pulled forward to Q1 FY27 (from 2H FY27) on direct customer pull, not just technical readiness. AEC fifth major hyperscaler win cited.
- **Implication:** CRDO is a direct, accelerating beneficiary of AI back-end network capex. The pull-forward of ZeroFlap is the strongest single data point — demand is outrunning the company's own prior timeline.

### ai_compute_topology

- **Status:** Confirm (with forward optionality)
- **Evidence:** Product roadmap explicitly targets topology evolution — AECs positioned for the 100G→200G-per-lane transition and 1.6T ports; OmniConnect gearbox ("Weaver," FY28) aimed at memory connectivity / composable XPUs in AI infrastructure; ALCs extending the system approach into mid-reach optical. Brennan tied OmniConnect to potential mitigation of industry memory-supply constraints.
- **Implication:** CRDO is positioning across the full intra-/inter-rack and scale-out topology, with credible roadmap extensions (gearbox, ALC, composable XPU). These are FY28 stories — optionality, not yet revenue — but they confirm the thesis that CRDO is a structural connectivity layer in AI compute topology rather than a single-product AEC vendor.

### hyperscaler_revenue_concentration

- **Status:** Drift
- **Evidence:** Top three end customers each >10% of Q3 revenue; the largest was 39%, second 32%, third 17% — i.e. ~88% of revenue in three accounts. Management expects 3–4 customers at the >10% level going forward and is adding neoclouds (TensorWave named for ZeroFlap) and other segments. Four domestic hyperscale customers drove the YoY growth; a fifth AEC win was cited.
- **Implication:** Concentration remains high and is the principal structural risk to the thesis. The drift is favorable in direction — customer count is broadening (neoclouds, fifth hyperscaler) and ZeroFlap brings a different customer set — but the magnitude (top-2 = 71% of revenue) means a single hyperscaler's capex digestion or design re-spin remains a material swing factor. Not a break; the diversification is real but early.
