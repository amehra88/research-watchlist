## 3. Forward guidance

Q3'26 guide: revenue $207–215M, operating margin 18–20%, non-GAAP diluted EPS $0.39–0.45. This is a clear haircut to Street EPS (midpoint -15.5% vs. consensus mean of $0.497) and a small revenue trim (-1.7% at midpoint). Management framed guidance as "current market conditions and the influence of elevated AI demand on fab utilization" — notable inversion of the typical AI-tailwind narrative: PLAB sells photomasks at design release, so when foundries run hot on existing AI/HPC product the customer base defers new tape-outs, *reducing* near-term mask demand. Full-year 2026 CapEx guidance maintained at $330M (US Allen + Korea 8nm builds on schedule). Visibility framed as "limited" with the typical 1–3 week backlog cited; management did not revise the medium-term setup. Tone shifted from point-estimate confidence last quarter to wider ranges and explicit acknowledgement of macro uncertainty.

## 8. Management Q&A flags

1. **Sriharan (Singular Research) — challenging exchange on design pipeline, margin compression, Allen expansion, regional differences.** Management acknowledged fixed-cost structure limits flexibility and that memory-supply constraints disproportionately impact Asia. This is the second consecutive quarter Sriharan has pressed PLAB on margin and capacity — Q1'26 challenging exchange covered margins, high-end supply, capacity, Asia, and local competitors. Repeated challenging exchanges from the same analyst on the same axis is a watch flag.
2. **Michaelis (Lake Street) on visibility and customer design timelines.** Management cited geopolitical tensions and post-CNY slowdown; deflected to customer mid-term optimism without committing to a recovery timeline. Mild dodge on timing.
3. **Schwab (Craig-Hallum) on capacity and advanced node aspirations.** Management used the question as a vehicle to re-emphasize Allen/Korea positioning and "advanced node aspirations" — unusual emphasis on US semiconductor market positioning, consistent with CHIPS Act / regionalization framing.
4. **Notable absence:** no analyst pressed on whether the AI-utilization-crowding-out dynamic is structural or transitory. This is the single most important interpretive question for the print and went unexplored on the call.
