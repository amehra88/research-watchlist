# MU — Q3 FY26 (3Q26) earnings read

_Prepared by earnings-reviewer, 2026-06-25. Tier: T1 (BCTK)._

## 1. Headline read

Decisive **Confirm** of the AI-memory super-cycle thesis, with a magnitude that again blew past both consensus and the company's own prior guide — Q3 FY26 revenue of **$41.46B** (+346% YoY, +74% QoQ) at **84.9% non-GAAP gross margin** and **$25.11 non-GAAP EPS**, versus a Q3 guide of only ~$33.5B given on the Q2 call ~3 months ago and Street consensus of $34.3B / $19.55. This is the second MU note in the corpus; against the prior 2Q26 read (which classified the cycle as a positive **Drift** toward "structurally advantaged"), the structural-margin narrative is now an actual — gross margin stepped from 74.9% to 84.9% in one quarter and the company guides FQ4 to ~86% on $50B revenue. The Strategic Customer Agreement (SCA) construct flagged as a watch-item last quarter has crystallized into ~40% of revenue under non-cancellable take-or-pay contracts with **$22B+ in upfront customer cash deposits**. The thesis has moved from "competitive cyclical-up HBM supplier" to "structurally advantaged AI-memory franchise with contracted multi-year demand" — strengthening the operator's 4→4+ trajectory on all three schema attributes, with potential_investor_interest now the clearest candidate to test 5.

## 3. Forward guidance

FQ4 FY26 (ending ~late Aug 2026) guided to **$50.0B ± $1.0B revenue** (another record, ~+21% sequential off the $41.46B base), **~86% gross margin** (up from 84.9% non-GAAP this quarter), and **non-GAAP diluted EPS of $31.00 ± $1.00** (GAAP $30.73 ± $1.00). Guidance philosophy remains ambitious and point-estimate-anchored (a hard revenue/EPS midpoint with a ±$1.0B band, plus a specific GM target), not the wide cyclical ranges memory analysts historically apply. On the analyst call, management framed the trajectory as "durable" and supply-constrained "beyond 2027 and even into 2028," with HBM booked out through 2027/2028 and HBM TAM projected to exceed $100B by 2027. FY26 capex raised to ~$27B; FY27 guided to "mid-$40 billion or higher," over half for greenfield construction (Idaho 1, Tongluo, Idaho 2) with wafer-outs in 2027-2028 and start-up costs of $100-200M/quarter in 2027. Capital return: 100% of FCF to shareholders, primarily buybacks, with intent to step up from December 2026 (CHIPS-agreement anniversary); the Q3 release declares a $0.15/share quarterly dividend (consistent with the 30% raise already logged at 2Q26 — likely the continuation of that increase, not fresh Q3 news), and buyback "could reach 10% of the company next year" if FCF persists (~$30B FCF guided next quarter). (Sources: press release Business Outlook; InsiderScore AI summary of the Q3'26 post-earnings analyst call, iacc 43457498.)

## 4. Thesis read by theme

### hbm_competitive_landscape

- **Status:** Confirm
- **Evidence:** "HBM4, built on 1-beta DRAM technology, is in high-volume shipments for our lead customer's platform, and qualification samples have been shipped to multiple end-customers"; HBM4E (1-gamma) volume production expected calendar 2027 (press release product highlights). On the call, HBM "booked out through 2027 and even into 2028," HBM TAM ">$100B by 2027," and SCAs explicitly include HBM with hyperscalers; management aims to keep "HBM share relative to DRAM share" consistent (Sur/JPM, Rakesh/Mizuho Q&A).
- **Implication:** Confirms MU shipping HBM4 in volume into the marquee lead-customer (NVIDIA) platform and broadening qualification samples to multiple end-customers — the qualification-share-gain thesis is intact and now multi-customer. The prior quarter's HBM4 share-target "correction" did not recur as a flag this call; management's framing is "match HBM share to DRAM share," a measured but constructive posture. The watchlist "follows SK Hynix (000660.KS) on HBM leadership" framing still holds (SK Hynix overall 5 vs MU 4), but the gap is narrowing on execution.

### semiconductor_cycle

- **Status:** Drift (positive — escalating toward thesis-defining)
- **Evidence:** Gross margin stepped 74.9% → 84.9% (non-GAAP) in a single quarter; FQ4 guided to ~86%; management frames the up-cycle as supply-constrained "beyond 2027/2028" with demand structurally exceeding the industry's ability to supply, so "supply growth will dictate shipment growth rather than demand." SCAs now ~40% of revenue (heading to ~half), non-cancellable take-or-pay through 2030 (call: O'Malley/Barclays, Rakesh/Mizuho).
- **Implication:** Stronger positive drift than the 2Q26 read. The cyclicality discount that caps memory multiples is being directly attacked by (a) a multi-year, contracted demand book, (b) industry-wide supply discipline, and (c) margins at levels with no historical memory precedent. This is the single most important swing variable for why investor interest is capped below 5 — and it is resolving favorably. Not yet a clean Break-of-the-cyclicality-bear-case (a true down-cycle has not been tested against the SCA protections), but the evidence base materially lengthens the assumed up-cycle duration.

### ai_infrastructure_capex

- **Status:** Confirm
- **Evidence:** Core Data Center BU revenue $11,524M (+653% YoY) at 87% GM and Cloud Memory BU $13,769M at 83% GM — data-center-tied units now ~61% of total revenue; LPDRAM/SOCAMM growth for CPU-based agentic-AI servers (SOCAMM2 in high-volume production); MU FY26 capex ~$27B rising to mid-$40B+ in FY27 to add capacity (press release + call).
- **Implication:** Confirms MU as a direct, high-margin beneficiary of the AI-infrastructure buildout; memory content per AI system is rising and data-center demand is now the dominant revenue driver. MU's own record capex is the supply-side mirror of the hyperscaler-capex theme — and the SCA cash deposits effectively have customers pre-funding that capacity.

### model_efficiency_evolution

- **Status:** Confirm (mechanism nuance)
- **Evidence:** Management ties rising memory demand to "agentic AI" driving CPU-based server growth and to higher LPDRAM/SOCAMM content per server; on the call, the supply/demand framing is that AI workloads (more memory-intensive inference) keep demand above supply "into 2028" (Weathers/DB, Schneider/GS Q&A).
- **Implication:** The bear mechanism here is that more-efficient models reduce memory intensity. Management's read remains the opposite — agentic/reasoning-heavy inference raises memory content (capacity and bandwidth), not lowers it. This call adds the SOCAMM/LPDRAM-in-data-center vector as concrete evidence that model evolution is currently a memory tailwind. Confirms; remains a swing variable worth monitoring (e.g., if model-efficiency gains ever outpace content growth).

### china_export_controls

- **Status:** Drift (minor, two-sided)
- **Evidence:** Capital-return step-up explicitly timed to the "CHIPS agreement anniversary" in December 2026 — i.e., CHIPS-linked restrictions still gate buyback magnitude/timing (call: Reitzes/Melius challenging exchange, Murphy). Competitive commentary: Chinese competitors (CXMT, YMTC) "acknowledged as having grown, but output mainly confined to China"; greenfield expansion concentrated in US/Idaho and Taiwan/Tongluo, not China (Rakers/Wells Fargo Q&A).
- **Implication:** Export-control / CHIPS exposure again surfaced as a capital-return *timing* constraint rather than a demand or revenue headwind. CXMT/YMTC growth is the structural watch-item (commodity-DRAM/NAND displacement risk over time) but management reports no marked shift in global competitive dynamics this quarter and frames Chinese output as China-contained. Net: the watchlist "China export-control exposure a watch-item" framing holds — surfaced, not escalated.

### nand_demand_cycle

- **Status:** Confirm
- **Evidence:** Enterprise SSD revenue hit $5B in Q3 (call); G9-based PCIe Gen6 SSD in high-volume production; 245TB QLC SSD shipments commenced; G9 NAND advancing across client/auto (press release). NAND is part of SCAs (particularly enterprise SSD); management notes NAND "constrained" though DRAM is "more critically so" (Rakers/Wells Fargo Q&A).
- **Implication:** Confirms NAND participating in the up-cycle with record enterprise-SSD demand, leading-edge node ramp (G9), and inclusion in long-dated SCAs. NAND is a clear second-order beneficiary of the AI-data-center storage buildout. Supports the constructive NAND-cycle thesis; DRAM/HBM remains the tighter, higher-margin leg.

## 5. AI positioning signal

- **Current score:** 4 (notes: "trending 4+ on HBM mix shift")
- **Evidence from this call:** HBM4 in high-volume shipment to lead customer (NVIDIA) + qualification samples to multiple end-customers; HBM4E (1-gamma) on track for CY2027; data-center-tied BUs now ~61% of revenue at 83-87% GM; SCAs include HBM with hyperscalers and enable multi-year co-engagement; record margins management attributes to "the strategic value of memory in the AI era" (Mehrotra, press release).
- **Recommendation:** drift to 4+ (under review for 5, pending sustained HBM4 multi-customer share confirmation and a second quarter of the structural-margin profile)
- **Reasoning:** The print and the SCA/HBM-co-design dynamic deepen AI-customer entanglement and push MU's AI exposure beyond "competitive #3 supplier." Held short of a clean 5 only by SK Hynix's retained HBM leadership (000660.KS at 5) and the still-commodity base-DRAM/NAND layer — but the operator-noted 4→4+ trajectory is now well-supported by actuals, and the path to 5 is live.

## 6. Competitive advantage signal

- **Innovation rate** (current: 4)
  - Evidence: HBM4 in volume on 1-beta, HBM4E on 1-gamma for CY2027; 256GB DDR5 RDIMMs on 1-gamma + advanced 3D die stacking; LP5X SOCAMM2 in high-volume production (first-mover claim); G9 PCIe Gen6 SSD and 245TB QLC SSD shipping; 1-gamma LPDDR5X ramping at a leading smartphone OEM; first 1-gamma DDR5 samples to a robotaxi customer; FY27 R&D to rise significantly (press release product highlights + call). 65,000+ patent portfolio cited as IP moat (Rakers Q&A).
  - Recommendation: hold at 4 (firm 4+ trajectory)
- **Distribution** (current: 4)
  - Evidence: SCAs now ~40% of revenue (heading to ~half), non-cancellable take-or-pay through 2030, 16 agreements with $22B+ deposits ($18B cash); customers across hyperscalers/HBM and enterprise-SSD NAND; demand exceeds supply such that customers are rationed; all four BUs at record revenue (Cloud Memory, Core Data Center, Mobile & Client, Auto & Embedded) (press release + call).
  - Recommendation: drift to 4+ (consider 5 as SCA share approaches half of revenue)
  - Note: The SCA build-out is a genuine, escalating distribution-moat enhancement. Last quarter it was a single five-year agreement; this quarter it is 16 agreements at ~40% of revenue with $18B of customer cash in hand. Multi-year take-or-pay supply commitments convert MU from a price-taking commodity vendor toward a contracted, visibility-rich franchise — the central mechanism that normally caps memory distribution scores is being directly mitigated.
- **Overall** (current: 4)
  - Recommendation: drift to 4+ (clearest of the three CA fields; 5 in view if SCAs reach ~half of revenue and survive the first cycle test)
  - Reasoning: Not a mechanical average. For a memory franchise, the differentiating axis is whether it can escape commodity price-taking — and innovation and distribution are now reinforcing exactly along that axis. Leading-edge nodes (1-beta HBM4, 1-gamma) earn the qualifications; the SCA construct monetizes that lead with contracted, deposit-backed, multi-year visibility. The overall holds at 4 only because (a) SK Hynix retains HBM leadership (overall 5), (b) base DRAM/NAND is still partly commodity, and (c) the SCA downside protections are untested through a real down-cycle. But the 4+ trajectory is firmer than at 2Q26, and this is the attribute closest to a structural re-rate to 5.

Offsets: buyback step-up gated to December 2026 by CHIPS-agreement timing; FY27 capex rising to mid-$40B+ (capital intensity, start-up cost drag $100-200M/qtr in 2027); CXMT/YMTC longer-term commodity-displacement watch-item.
- **Recommendation:** drift to 4+ (operator-locked 4+ confirmed; 4+→5 is now live — the "structural margin + contracted demand + record FCF + 100% capital return" combination is precisely the factor stack that supports a 5, with the principal remaining gates being (i) a second quarter sustaining the margin/SCA profile and (ii) the buyback actually stepping up post-December 2026)

## 8. Management Q&A flags

1. **Capital-return / buyback feasibility probe (Reitzes/Melius — flagged challenging exchange → Murphy, Sadana):** Reitzes pressed on the credibility of returning 100% of FCF and the feasibility of buying back up to 10% of the company. Murphy confirmed the commitment, leaning on SCA-driven cash visibility and structural supply tightness; the step-up is explicitly gated to the December 2026 CHIPS-agreement anniversary. The most pointed exchange of the call — the constraint is timing, not capacity.
2. **SCA cash-deposit return schedule (O'Malley/Barclays, Rakesh/Mizuho → Sadana):** On the $22B+ deposits, Sadana clarified deposits are non-prorated, returned on a predefined schedule weighted to the latter half of each agreement term, and serve as contingency + good-faith commitment. Constructive detail, but note: the deposits inflate near-term operating cash flow and receivables — a quality-of-cash-flow item to track (OCF $25.4B this quarter partly reflects SCA mechanics, not pure operating conversion).
3. **HBM inclusion in SCAs (Rakesh/Mizuho → Sadana):** Confirmed HBM is included in SCAs with hyperscalers — a meaningful escalation from generic "long-term agreement" language; ties the highest-margin product directly into the contracted demand book.
4. **Demand-fulfillment through 2028 (Schneider/GS → Sadana, Murphy, Bhatia):** Asked whether MU can meet forecast demand through 2028, management was candid that "complete supply-demand intercept is not foreseeable," i.e., supply remains the binding constraint even with greenfield ramps — bullish for pricing but a cap on shipment-volume upside.
5. **Greenfield cost drag (Weathers/DB → Bhatia, Murphy):** Start-up costs ($100-200M/qtr in 2027) and a mix shift to higher-performance/higher-cost bits will bias near-term DRAM bit costs upward — flagged honestly rather than buried; management argues scale mutes the margin impact. Worth monitoring against the ~86%+ GM guide.

## 9. Vocabulary candidates

- **Strategic Customer Agreement (SCA)** — already surfaced at 2Q26; this quarter it materially scaled (16 agreements, ~40% of revenue, $22B+ deposits, take-or-pay through 2030). Reiterated as a candidate cross-name theme for memory/components suppliers moving to long-dated, deposit-backed supply contracts (relevant to 000660.KS HBM, AMAT/LRCX equipment reads). Strong candidate for operator promotion.
- **SOCAMM / SOCAMM2 (LPDRAM in data center)** — MU's low-power compression-attached memory module form factor for CPU-based / agentic-AI servers; a specific product-vector linking LPDRAM to data-center demand. Conceptually adjacent to ai_infrastructure_capex and model_efficiency_evolution. Surfaced for operator review.
- **High Bandwidth Flash (HBF)** — carried over from 2Q26 (NAND-adjacent AI-memory tier); not raised prominently this call. Keep on watch for nand_demand_cycle / hbm_competitive_landscape relevance.
