## 3. Forward guidance

Management **raised** FY26 revenue guidance to $6.3B ±$100M (~32% y/y growth at midpoint) — described as another in a series of guidance raises this year. FY26 adjusted gross margin guided to 44.5%–45.0%; FY26 OpEx ~$1.61B ±$20M (nudged up to reflect higher performance-based comp and targeted investments to secure supply); operating margin ~19% ±50bps. Capex of $250M–$275M planned. Tone is confident and visibility-forward: backlog reached $7.7B (+$600M+ sequentially), ~80% of hardware backlog deliverable within 12 months, with management explicitly citing "strong visibility into 2027." RLS hyper-rail revenue is expected to begin in 2027, ramp linearly and multi-year ("hundreds of millions over multiple years," per call). Management flagged a note of caution on Q4 gross margin (supply-chain mix and inflationary pressure) and advised against over-interpreting current Q4 projections. Guidance philosophy remains range-based with a conservative framing on near-term margin despite repeated raises.

## 4. Thesis read by theme

### networking_competitive_landscape

- **Status:** Confirm
- **Evidence:** Optical networking grew 42% y/y and routing & switching grew 88% y/y (paraphrase, prepared remarks per call summary). On competitive dynamics, Scott McFeely (Q&A, O'Laughlin/TD Cowen) described consolidation in the systems space and the difficulty for component vendors trying to move up into systems; new win with a major switch OEM for WaveLogic 5/6 nano pluggables; pluggable revenue expected to more than double from 2025.
- **Implication:** CIEN is gaining share in optical systems and DCI as the industry consolidates, with vertical integration (coherent modems, WaveLogic) as the moat. Confirms the thesis that CIEN is a structural winner in the networking competitive landscape rather than a price-taker.

### ai_infrastructure_capex

- **Status:** Confirm
- **Evidence:** Management framed the addressable market as roughly doubling to ~$50B by 2029, driven by AI network infrastructure (paraphrase, prepared remarks per call summary). Direct cloud customer revenue +70% y/y; industry-first multi-rail order from a leading hyperscaler for the RLS hyper-rail platform; backlog $7.7B with orders "deployed, not warehoused."
- **Implication:** CIEN's revenue is a direct leverage point on hyperscaler AI capex, and the demand signal is order-backed (backlog) rather than purely guidance-based. Confirms the AI-infrastructure-capex thesis with tangible bookings evidence.

### ai_compute_topology

- **Status:** Confirm
- **Evidence:** RLS hyper-rail (multi-rail DCI) addresses data center interconnect and scale-out AI fabric capacity/efficiency (paraphrase, prepared remarks). DCOM (data center out-of-band management, routing+switching + PON) drove the 88% R&S growth, anchored by Meta, with a 2nd hyperscaler ordering and a 3rd in lab qualification; DCOM TAM cited at $1–3B by 2029. Nubis acquisition (active copper cable / optical engine tech) on track, accretive in 2027–2028; coherent-light technology expected to impact the market in late 2027 (Q&A, Katta).
- **Implication:** CIEN is positioning across multiple layers of AI compute topology — DCI (hyper-rail), out-of-band management (DCOM), and emerging short-reach interconnect (Nubis active copper, coherent light). Confirms the thesis that CIEN sells into the evolving AI fabric/topology, not just long-haul transport.

### hyperscaler_revenue_concentration

- **Status:** Confirm (with two-sided implication)
- **Evidence:** Two cloud customers each contributed >10% of revenue in Q2 (paraphrase, prepared remarks). Direct cloud revenue +70% y/y vs. service provider +28% y/y; in the Citi exchange (Q&A, Colby), Graff explained that hyperscaler growth is overshadowing other customers and diluting the relative contribution of the legacy 10%-customer base. DCOM anchored by Meta; multi-rail anchored by a single lead hyperscaler with others "in advanced discussions."
- **Implication:** Concentration is intensifying — the growth engine and the principal source of customer-concentration risk are the same hyperscaler cohort. This is the lever that drives the upside (AI capex pass-through, multi-rail TAM) but also raises sensitivity to any single hyperscaler's capex digestion or vendor-diversification decision. Confirms the thesis dynamic; the risk side warrants monitoring as the 2027 ramp concentrates further into a small number of anchor accounts.

## 8. Management Q&A flags

- **Multi-rail revenue timing/profitability (Chatterjee/JPM; Roy/Stifel; Long/Barclays):** Management consistently anchored hyper-rail revenue to a 2027 start, "linear and multi-year," and margin-accretive — repeated emphasis across multiple analysts suggests management is deliberately tempering near-term expectations while underscoring the multi-year size ("hundreds of millions over multiple years"). Emphasis, not a dodge.
- **Q4 gross margin (O'Laughlin/TD Cowen):** Graff attributed potential Q4 GM pressure to supply-chain mix and inflation and explicitly "advised not to overinterpret current projections." Mild sandbag / expectation management on the only soft spot in an otherwise upbeat call — worth watching whether Q4 GM lands at the conservative or better end.
- **OpEx increase (Chatterjee/JPM; Roy/Stifel):** Two analysts pressed on rising OpEx; Graff framed it as variable/performance comp tied to outperformance plus targeted supply investments, stressing revenue will grow faster than OpEx (operating leverage). Reasonable, but recurring analyst attention signals the Street is watching cost discipline.
- **10%-customer metric / service provider dilution (Colby/Citi):** Graff acknowledged hyperscaler growth is diluting other customers' relative contribution. Transparent answer that doubles as the clearest articulation of the intensifying concentration dynamic.
- **Supply constraints (Marshall/Morgan Stanley):** Graff acknowledged modem, pump-laser and CDM constraints but leaned on vertical integration as mitigation. Candid on the binding constraint to growth ("customers would take more if supply allowed").
