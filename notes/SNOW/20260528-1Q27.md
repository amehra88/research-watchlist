## 3. Forward guidance

- **Q2 FY27 product revenue:** $1.415B–$1.420B, ~30% YoY. FactSet consensus_fixed for the same period (as of 2026-05-01) was $1.777B SALES, but that figure includes total revenue and looked stale relative to the print pace; the more telling fact is that management is now guiding Q2 to a higher absolute level than Q1's beat with growth still at 30% on a much larger base.
- **FY27 product revenue growth:** raised from 27% to 31% YoY. Management attributed the raise to (a) core data platform strength and (b) "meaningful uplift" from CoCo and Snowflake Intelligence — the first time SNOW has explicitly attributed a guidance raise to AI product monetization, not just AI-driven core consumption.
- **FY27 non-GAAP operating margin:** raised from 12.5% to 13.5%, despite a 150bps headwind from the Observe acquisition. Implies underlying margin lift closer to 250bps.
- **FY27 non-GAAP adjusted FCF margin:** held at 23%.
- **Tone:** confident but not promotional. Guidance philosophy reiterated as unchanged (per Robins to Lenschow); the beat is attributed to CoCo + core acceleration rather than philosophical reset, which preserves credibility on future raises.

## 4. Thesis read by theme

### enterprise_ai_adoption

- **Status:** Confirm (with positive drift)
- **Evidence:** CoCo reached 7,100+ accounts since February 2026 GA — described as "fastest [adoption] in company history." Global 2000 customer adds were 13 in Q1 vs. 4 in PY. Customers >$1M ARR rose to 79 (46 net adds in Q vs. 26 PY). Use cases per account executive +86% YoY, projects deployed +114% YoY. (InsiderScore summary, customer metrics + AI product adoption sections.)
- **Implication:** Enterprise AI adoption is not just being talked about — it's showing up in customer count, deployment density, and large-account expansion simultaneously. The combination of CoCo penetration into 51% of the customer base and step-function project-deployment growth supports a thesis that SNOW is becoming a primary platform for production enterprise AI workloads, not just an experimentation surface.

### ai_infrastructure_software

- **Status:** Confirm
- **Evidence:** $6B / five-year AWS contract (doubling prior agreement), fully baked into guidance. $200M OpenAI partnership expansion. SAP integration GA. AWS marketplace lifetime sales >$7B. (InsiderScore summary, partnerships section.)
- **Implication:** SNOW is anchoring itself in the AI infrastructure stack via committed hyperscaler economics and named model-provider partnerships, while leveraging AWS pricing concessions to defend gross margin against lower-margin AI revenue mix. The doubled AWS commitment is the more important read: it signals SNOW expects compute consumption to scale substantially, and it secures unit economics on that path.

### observability_competitive_landscape

- **Status:** Drift (constructive)
- **Evidence:** Observe acquisition contributed <1pp to Q1 product revenue growth; full-year contribution still expected at ~1pp. Observe is flagged as a 150bps margin headwind for FY27 but is being absorbed within a raised overall margin guide. (InsiderScore summary, acquisitions + margin outlook sections.)
- **Implication:** Observe is on track but is not yet the swing factor — the AI products (CoCo, Snowflake Intelligence) are. The thesis that SNOW can credibly extend into observability adjacent to DDOG/SPLK remains intact but unproven on revenue contribution; the more material competitive read is that SNOW's data-gravity argument is strengthening regardless of whether Observe scales independently.

### ai_agent_monetization

- **Status:** Confirm (material upgrade vs. prior posture)
- **Evidence:** Pending Natoma acquisition framed as extending SNOW's "agentic control plane into everyday applications" in a governed environment. Snowflake Intelligence positioned alongside CoCo as a revenue-contributing agentic product. Management explicitly attributed a portion of the guidance raise to AI products, not just AI-driven consumption — a notable shift in language. (InsiderScore summary, AI and product innovation section; Materne and Zukin Q&A exchanges.)
- **Implication:** SNOW is now articulating a multi-product agentic monetization story (CoCo for code/migration, Snowflake Intelligence for analytics, Natoma for application orchestration), and tying it to a discrete guide raise. This is the first quarter where ai_agent_monetization moves from "narrative" to "in the print," which is what the thesis required.

### ai_re_architected_incumbent

- **Status:** Confirm
- **Evidence:** Internal AI deployment: 25% increase in support case throughput per engineer, 25% reduction in case resolution time, 30% reduction in cloud-deployment resolution time, 40% less engineering time per ticket, developer productivity doubled (PRs and LOC per engineer), >100 workflows automated across finance/marketing/sales/HR in weeks. 20% more product capabilities delivered YoY. Hiring constrained (17 organic adds in Q1 ex-acquisition). (InsiderScore summary, productivity & internal use section.)
- **Implication:** SNOW is using its own AI products on itself to drive operating leverage, and the leverage is showing up in both gross hiring discipline and capability delivery velocity. This is the cleanest "AI re-architected incumbent" signal in the print and meaningfully supports the margin trajectory — the 300bps YoY operating margin expansion with limited S&M hiring would not be possible without the productivity gains.

Ramaswamy addressed via cost-governance features in the product; Robins acknowledged AI products carry lower GM but argued AWS contract economics offset. **Flag:** this is the one place management acknowledged structural margin pressure — worth tracking each subsequent quarter for whether AWS offset proves durable.
- **Raimo Lenschow (Barclays) — sandbag check:** asked whether the guidance raise philosophy had changed. Robins held the line — philosophy unchanged, beat attributed to CoCo launch + core acceleration. **Flag:** this is the right answer if SNOW wants credibility on future raises, but it sets up scrutiny if Q2 prints meaningfully above the new guide.
- **Koji Ikeda (BofA) — long-term positioning:** asked how SNOW maintains its trusted position amid AI velocity. Ramaswamy/Kleinerman emphasized infrastructure governance and security. **Flag:** management has internalized that the trust/governance pitch is the structural defense against hyperscaler AI offerings — consistent with the moat thesis.
- **Brent Thill (Jefferies) — emphasis:** Ramaswamy unprompted emphasized the balance between AI efficiency gains and continued sales/marketing investment. **Flag:** suggests management is preempting a "are you under-investing in S&M?" concern — worth watching whether sales productivity gains hold in Q2 with the limited hiring (17 organic adds).
- **Kirk Materne (Evercore) — emphasis:** Ramaswamy described CoCo as transformative to sales team efficiency, framing it as a customer story and an internal story simultaneously. **Flag:** the dogfooding narrative is becoming a deliberate part of the pitch; investor day risk is that the productivity numbers normalize.
