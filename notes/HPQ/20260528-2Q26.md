## 1. Headline read

Q2'26 was a **Confirm** print on the single assigned theme (`pc_demand`): Personal Systems revenue grew 13% YoY with both Commercial (+14%) and Consumer (+10%) double-digit, AI PCs reached 44% of unit mix (vs. 35% prior quarter), and HP raised full-year diluted EPS guidance to $2.90–$3.10 (toward the upper end of prior range) — all consistent with the bull-case of a Windows 11 refresh-driven, AI-PC mix-shift-supported PC tailwind. Offset: management flagged 2Q–3Q–4Q sequential memory/storage cost inflation pressuring PS margins, with Q4'26 cited as the trough before FY27 recovery. The print was strong; the forward margin shape is the friction point.

## 3. Forward guidance

- **Q3'26 diluted EPS:** $0.61 to $0.71 (transcript). The Q3 EPS range sits below Q2'26 actual ($0.77 GAAP / management's non-GAAP EPS reference implicit) — explicit sequential deceleration tied to memory/storage cost pass-through to PS COGS.
- **FY26 diluted EPS:** raised to $2.90–$3.10 (previously the company had guided toward the lower end of an unspecified prior range — implies a meaningful upward revision to the implied mid-point).
- **FY26 free cash flow:** $2.8–$3.0 billion (transcript).
- **PS unit TAM:** management expects unit TAM "down high teens" in 2H'26, but expects revenue offsets from pricing and premium-mix shift — a deliberately ASP-led model rather than volume-led.
- **PS margins:** below long-term range for remainder of FY26; Q4'26 explicitly called as the **margin trough**; sequential improvement expected entering FY27.
- **Cost program:** $1B gross annualized run-rate savings target by end of FY28 reaffirmed (multi-year, includes voluntary early retirement expense recognized in Q2).
- **Guidance philosophy:** point-range remains. The upward FY26 EPS revision while simultaneously flagging margin pressure ahead suggests management has high confidence in mitigation actions and pricing offsets — a moderately confident tone, not a sandbag.

## 4. Thesis read by theme

### pc_demand

- **Status:** Confirm
- **Evidence:** Personal Systems revenue +13% YoY (Commercial +14%, Consumer +10%) per prepared remarks; AI PCs 44% of shipment mix in Q2 vs. 35% prior quarter, with explicit guide to 60–70% of shipments in FY27; geographic strength outside Americas (APJ +18%, EMEA +6% in constant currency) driven by Windows 11 refresh; 30% of installed base still on Windows 10 — i.e., refresh runway intact. Management acknowledged 2–3% revenue pull-forward in Commercial due to cost-anticipation buying, so underlying organic growth is closer to +10–11% YoY. Eighth consecutive quarter of top-line growth (per transcript prepared remarks).
- **Implication:** The thesis that PC demand is benefiting from a multi-quarter Windows 11 refresh layered with AI-PC mix uplift remains intact and is being executed with above-expectation profitability (PS operating profit +30% YoY at 5.2% margin). The qualifier: a measurable share of Q2 commercial demand was pulled forward, the unit TAM is expected down high-teens in 2H'26, and PS margins compress into a Q4 trough. So the "demand strength" leg of pc_demand is confirmed, but the "margin leverage on that demand" leg is on hold until memory/storage cost normalization in FY27. Net: Confirm, with magnitude intact and timing of margin payoff pushed right by ~2–3 quarters.

## 8. Management Q&A flags

1. **EPS guidance for FY27 — Parkhill declined to guide (Daryanani / Evercore).** When pressed on FY27 EPS shape, CFO said it was "too early for guidance" but expected margin improvements post-Q4'26. Standard dodge for a one-quarter-ahead read, but worth flagging — this is the question the bulls need answered, and management is not yet willing to put a number on the FY27 recovery they're verbally promising.
2. **Tariff refund optionality — Parkhill flagged as out-of-model upside (Wamsi Mohan / BofA).** CFO stated tariff refunds are "not factored into the current financial outlook" because the refund process "currently does not favor complex multinational companies." This is a deliberate sandbag — if the refund process changes, it's pure upside. Worth tracking as a non-modeled catalyst.
3. **Memory cost sequencing — challenging exchange flagged by InsiderScore from Q1'26 call carried forward (Aaron Rakers / Wells Fargo; Krish Sankar / Cowen) — recurring tension.** The summary calls out continued probing on memory cost guidance and seasonality. Q2 confirmed Q2 cost pressure as previously flagged and extended it through Q3 and Q4 — analysts are pushing because the cost curve is still moving against them. No current-quarter "challenging exchanges" tagged by the InsiderScore sentiment model (challenging_analyze_exchanges = 0), suggesting management handled the cost narrative more confidently this quarter than last.
4. **AI-PC unit elasticity at higher prices — Woodring (Morgan Stanley) probed, Parkhill conceded soft low-end demand.** CFO acknowledged "lower demand in low-end units but strength in premium segments." Useful framing — HPQ's AI-PC mix is a deliberate price-up strategy and management is willing to cede unit-share at the low end to defend ASP and gross margin. This is a strategic choice with thesis implications: if low-end share-loss accelerates, the unit-TAM-down-high-teens guide could prove too optimistic for HPQ specifically.
5. **APJ acceleration and Americas softness — Cadiz (Citi).** APJ +18% vs. Americas flat was attributed to timing (Americas pulled forward Win 11 refresh into 2H'25). Plausible explanation but worth watching — if Americas remains flat in Q3, the "timing" narrative gets weaker.
