### ...busy week ending July 17, 2026

- Anthropic & OpenAI now up against Kimi K3 from China:[](https://substack.com/redirect/e41b145b-bfe9-4bf2-bc28-6a1968891035?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA)[Moonshot AI](https://substack.com/redirect/e41b145b-bfe9-4bf2-bc28-6a1968891035?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA) took the[world’s open source frontier AI model crown this week](https://substack.com/redirect/a3255f74-6b5a-4df0-9b8b-7fd9ddab8292?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA) with the release of the world’s largest open source, 2.8 trillion parameter[frontier class AI model, Kimi K3](https://substack.com/redirect/783ec67a-8121-4f4a-a47f-5a15727604cc?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA). China now has the dibs on the world’s largest open source AI model, just in time for its[World AI conference this week, where President Xi](https://substack.com/redirect/74c578f5-56e1-4697-b8ba-4d0011a41e21?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA) delivered a keynote highlighting China’s AI leadership. As I discussed on[ARD #120](https://substack.com/redirect/3c5901de-e4f9-4176-bfaa-66aa20be6598?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA), Kimi K3 lands close to Anthropic’s Opus 4.8 — but crucially it’s open-weights (the world can inspect, host and run it independent of China) at perhaps a third of the price or less. By[Friday’s ARD #121](https://substack.com/redirect/0b680264-05d2-42fa-8569-30f58c9a6c58?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA) the point got sharper: Kimi K3 has effectively upset the global lead the two closed frontier labs assumed — Anthropic and OpenAI would likely have been down 10%+ on the week if they were already public (not stock advice). My take: this is less a binary “race” than a global undertaking, where US firms increasingly lean into Chinese open models as portfolio supplements — balancing cost, privacy and capability — even as geopolitics tries to pull the two apart. The perceived[US–China gap](https://substack.com/redirect/4ceeb27e-f152-4f43-87f2-cf78e92a0cf3?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA) keeps looking narrower than assumed. More[here](https://substack.com/redirect/3c5901de-e4f9-4176-bfaa-66aa20be6598?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA) and[here](https://substack.com/redirect/0b680264-05d2-42fa-8569-30f58c9a6c58?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA). - Google Gemini now behind frontier AI leaders: Google[](https://substack.com/redirect/df0c1b16-b178-4b21-848d-5b214f2901e1?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA)[Gemini 3.5 Pro now](https://substack.com/redirect/df0c1b16-b178-4b21-848d-5b214f2901e1?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA) is lagging the US Frontier AI model companies Anthropic and OpenAI. It’s flagship[Gemini 3.5 Pro is now behind](https://substack.com/redirect/f499f8c8-5dff-4ddb-888a-220e04dacd58?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA) in schedule by months. As I covered on[ARD #121](https://substack.com/redirect/0b680264-05d2-42fa-8569-30f58c9a6c58?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA), the slip lands specifically in the AI coding race — the agents/coding/productivity layer where Anthropic’s Fable 5 and OpenAI’s GPT-5.6 are surging — with Gemini 3.5 Pro reportedly held back for falling short of internal goals. It’s the latest twist in Google’s whipsaw Gemini arc: a “Code Red” when ChatGPT first launched, then a resurgent Gemini that handed the “Code Red” back to OpenAI, and now another turn back. My take: this is a stumble, not a structural break — Google still has a similar opportunity to turn it around, it’ll just take more time, and it remains among the best-positioned[globally on](https://substack.com/redirect/657d4a60-dfcf-4652-a8b2-ecbf5f063f28?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA)[consumer AI](https://substack.com/redirect/657d4a60-dfcf-4652-a8b2-ecbf5f063f28?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA), side by side with Apple. The lawsuit — Apple’s “First Blood” against OpenAI over AI-device trade secrets (400+ hires under Jony Ive) — lands pointedly ahead of OpenAI’s expected mega-IPO, as I covered around[ARD #117](https://substack.com/redirect/5753ad93-30ff-41fd-a550-a1c88e310564?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA). The chip roadmap (M6, M7 Pro/Max/Ultra into M8, touch MacBook Pro) underlines Apple’s under-appreciated silicon strength for running Apple Intelligence locally and on its privacy-protected servers — the Srouji/Ternus thread from[ARD #118](https://substack.com/redirect/4f9d53ab-33a6-4dce-ad68-7c05223a9fa0?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA). My take: Apple keeps executing on its AI opportunities with alacrity, worldwide. - Nvidia backed Thinking Machine’s US open source AI model: Thinking Machines founded only three years ago by ex-OpenAI senior talent,[launched its first open source AI model Inkling](https://substack.com/redirect/d169d353-d538-4ace-814a-8763d353c3df?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA) this week. Nvidia is a key investor and backer, providing the company with lots of its latest Vera Rubin chips to train and develop the model. It’s part of Nvidia’s ‘[Kingmaker](https://substack.com/redirect/dc56f25b-e404-4e08-96f6-a706319020b8?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA)‘ strategy in[US open source](https://substack.com/redirect/4ceeb27e-f152-4f43-87f2-cf78e92a0cf3?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA) AI companies. The last one[being Reflection AI](https://substack.com/redirect/067b86ba-458c-4ac8-a885-2e7e83ceed13?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA) that I’ve discussed. As I noted on[ARD #120](https://substack.com/redirect/3c5901de-e4f9-4176-bfaa-66aa20be6598?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA), Inkling — from the team led by ex-OpenAI CTO Mira Murati — isn’t trying to go toe-to-toe with the frontier flagships; it’s a flexible, quirky open-weight tool aimed at builders. The bigger signal is the pattern: Nvidia increasingly playing kingmaker, seeding a stable of US open-source champions — Reflection AI, now Thinking Machines — with capital and its latest Vera Rubin silicon. My take: it’s the US answer to China’s open-source surge (Kimi K3, Qwen, DeepSeek), and it fits the argument I’ve made that[Nvidia and Apple can anchor a US open-source counterweight](https://substack.com/redirect/4ceeb27e-f152-4f43-87f2-cf78e92a0cf3?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA) rather than cede that ground. More[here](https://substack.com/redirect/035d91e9-fb9b-4c50-8389-70144b577a47?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA).
