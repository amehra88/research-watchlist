### ...busy AI weekly roundup for week ending Friday June 26, 2026

- Apple recognizes RAMageddon: The [memory price hikes officially arrived](https://substack.com/redirect/74c06ffd-2138-4c25-987c-f378be89ceac?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA)this week, and it’s Apple that made them [feel real](https://substack.com/redirect/369c4e2a-0c40-418b-8b8d-b5eb4620a1a4?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA). Media is [dubbing it Apple’s ‘RAMageddon’](https://substack.com/redirect/366de3f7-71ac-4e85-955c-985ad82fb4c3?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA)— raising Macs, iPads, Apple TVs, Vision Pros and displays 15-25%+ to counter the memory shortage (the value MacBook Neo quietly went from $599 to $699). Even as [it held off](https://substack.com/redirect/9a969800-5c95-4939-ad19-32983e139024?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA)on base iPhones for now. The market [took note](https://substack.com/redirect/77c9db3c-5990-4746-bd3c-2f6f25dbc7aa?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA). As outgoing [CEO Tim Cook put it, it’s a “100-year flood”](https://substack.com/redirect/2c43da30-0015-4347-a755-d34fd171c88a?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA) he hasn’t seen in four decades in the business. Apple is also [adjusting their AI-ready chip release schedule](https://substack.com/redirect/84310196-4767-475a-801b-d17f876549ec?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA) for this year and next. My take: on a relative basis Apple still has the [most resilient supply chain at scale](https://substack.com/redirect/a566de88-d758-4f7e-8dfe-af46251ffe50?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA) — the best seat in the pouring rain versus peers without its wide, deep, global hardware/software supply chain. But it’s a relative win, not an absolute one: even Apple is passing the costs through, with little near-term relief for anyone. And because it’s Apple’s prices moving — not a Windows laptop or [an Xbox](https://substack.com/redirect/6fdd8e59-cb17-4c09-a0cc-08d07559bed8?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA) — regulators and economists might actually notice.. More [here](https://substack.com/redirect/9ca4d023-0dd1-4182-9033-b2cd5f40a8f8?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA) and [here.](https://substack.com/redirect/a566de88-d758-4f7e-8dfe-af46251ffe50?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA) [](https://substack.com/redirect/8c4753f1-7e91-4581-8586-2406eb4c8aad?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA)

- US’s Memory Supply Option in China: There’s [no easy respite](https://substack.com/redirect/7178a08a-22da-45f9-816d-145974474680?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA)from the global memory supply bottleneck and price hikes. There may be a somewhat difficult relief valve for the[memory crunch](https://substack.com/redirect/c9f86ed5-5094-4a5b-bc66-494148f3e074?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA) — it just runs through China, and US companies like [Apple haven’t been able](https://substack.com/redirect/4c030db8-0940-4af2-80d7-777752394eaa?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA) to tap it. As the [WSJ frames it,](https://substack.com/redirect/7178a08a-22da-45f9-816d-145974474680?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA) the crunch is “almost impossible to solve” quickly: the three memory titans (SK Hynix, Samsung,[Micron](https://substack.com/redirect/51ff0280-3208-4d63-9459-78402c831f05?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA)) have swung wafer capacity to high-margin HBM for AI data centers, Micron’s new Idaho and New York fabs won’t open until mid-2027 and 2030, and national-security rules block American firms from working with China’s fast-rising[CXMT (DRAM) and YMTC (NAND)](https://substack.com/redirect/d538ee6f-8466-47e5-8593-a6cdaf92788a?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA) — with Micron itself lobbying to keep that door shut. Apple’s Cook says “everything needs to be on the table,” and HP is reportedly exploring [CXMT chips](https://substack.com/redirect/a54b2cbb-29f5-428e-a787-6acad61020d8?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA)for Asia-bound products. My take: water, water everywhere, and not a drop to drink — the one near-term supply valve is capped by geopolitics, and it may take[Xi’s White House visit this September](https://substack.com/redirect/da71fa70-4110-4e26-986f-ff4f8f90cf98?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA) and a broader tech deal to change that. Until then, the world just has to wait for the chip giants to make more, like regular potato chips. More [here](https://substack.com/redirect/e1134e5a-f4cf-4234-ab54-c7b7c20ef830?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA). [](https://substack.com/redirect/9a30d750-2415-4682-b34c-1750e4725dbd?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA)

- Microsoft sides against Frontier AI Models: Picking up the thread from[ARD #98](https://substack.com/redirect/ed512b01-1d9a-4de1-b3ab-e75849cfad7d?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA), Microsoft [CEO Satya Nadella has now drawn](https://substack.com/redirect/d697220a-670f-45dd-b81a-991c91853ce0?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA) the line in ink — “We can’t let AI giants eat the economy” — putting [Microsoft firmly on the side of](https://substack.com/redirect/18f9e72d-e1d1-4724-94a5-27efb6c895f1?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA)[enterprise customers](https://substack.com/redirect/18f9e72d-e1d1-4724-94a5-27efb6c895f1?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA) rather than the frontier model-builders. In a matter of weeks Microsoft rolled out a suite of low-cost models and Copilot Cowork (an agent that lets users pick cheaper models for long-running tasks), and is even[weighing hosting China’s open-source DeepSeek](https://substack.com/redirect/2eab342b-3e55-41d1-badf-dbae48de40a3?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA) — a striking move from one of OpenAI’s oldest backers. Nadella reframes AI as job reorganization over elimination, and says the industry still has to “earn the social permission.” My take: this is the[Frenemies](https://substack.com/redirect/4bd3eff9-57d8-4b86-93ca-5b19152d5ce5?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA) dynamic in full color — Microsoft wants to be far less dependent on a handful of frontier model companies (beyond Nvidia too), and it’s carving out an ‘us vs them’ position with the customers right[ahead of the OpenAI and Anthropic mega-IPOs](https://substack.com/redirect/f29887c7-cb86-45f1-983a-c9ec7f15ff5f?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA). A mechanism that’s worked in power politics for millennia. More [here.](https://substack.com/redirect/4af0a8ed-e170-475f-b084-3906a60ee374?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA) [](https://substack.com/redirect/515ca6fb-3b53-4911-a1f8-f32fc0a1967e?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA)
