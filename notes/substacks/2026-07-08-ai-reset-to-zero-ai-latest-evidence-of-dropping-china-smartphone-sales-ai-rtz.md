# [AI: Latest evidence of dropping China smartphone sales. AI-RTZ #1141](https://substack.com/app-link/post?publication_id=684161&post_id=205654651&utm_source=post-email-title&utm_campaign=email-post-title&isFreemail=true&r=8ne7oq&token=eyJ1c2VyX2lkIjo1MjMwMjM3MjIsInBvc3RfaWQiOjIwNTY1NDY1MSwiaWF0IjoxNzgzNDg3MjYyLCJleHAiOjE3ODYwNzkyNjIsImlzcyI6InB1Yi02ODQxNjEiLCJzdWIiOiJwb3N0LXJlYWN0aW9uIn0.E93eUASTnN7kGbqS1XbMH_HaK7_074DFkn9FJjUHc7M)

### ...global RAMAgeddon is accelerating its toll on global sales, starting in Asia

I’ve been [relentlessly discussing](https://substack.com/redirect/087c903f-967c-487e-b238-8934d27e2999?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA)the accelerating ‘[RAMageddon’](https://substack.com/redirect/9a1d9eb7-1dcc-43c8-8644-b88b139e7a3d?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA) where [global memory supply](https://substack.com/redirect/df836299-5a49-431c-ba81-c82d3a8ecccc?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA)constaints and [unprecedented price increases,](https://substack.com/redirect/3d36526e-e34f-4c15-a6cd-15cfe45b2289?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA) would be a multi-year feature of this [AI Tech Wave](https://substack.com/redirect/a24ad428-2fb6-4d08-b00e-7d5fc4382073?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA).

And not a quarterly boom and bust cycle for memory chip semiconductor companies, as we’ve seen through decades of tech waves. Specifically a few days ago on AI Ramblings Daily ([ARD) podcast #108,](https://substack.com/redirect/46875e25-3c46-40f1-84f0-53ef67293dac?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA) I specifically underlined again that we would see [dramatic drops](https://substack.com/redirect/a55d6f18-cf93-4c2b-9e2a-8beb2ac68f66?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA)in [unit sales of computers, laptops and smartphones](https://substack.com/redirect/17beb04b-78af-49a8-ab8e-cb4903bf097e?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA), especially by [Asian vendors](https://substack.com/redirect/c5fb9e89-69d8-4d21-a145-aa560265bcaa?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA). That’s because that’s where a majority of that unit market comes from and resides in the ‘[Global South’.](https://substack.com/redirect/0f09d079-6598-476b-a702-c24c2d42a41b?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA)

That now makes up over 85% of the world’s 8+ billion people, and over 40% of global GDP. Equal to the developed G7 countries in terms of share of global GDP.

So all this is important as we see the rising metrics of market share drops for the world’s Asian (read Chinese) smartphone makers.

A Reuters lays out in [“](https://substack.com/redirect/dfad36b1-7b9f-4d9f-a82c-279df06e797b?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA)[China smartphone sales drop 13% during 618 festival as memory costs limit discounts”:](https://substack.com/redirect/dfad36b1-7b9f-4d9f-a82c-279df06e797b?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA)

> “Huawei was only major brand with sales growth over month-long event, Counterpoint says”“Apple ranked second after discounts on iPhone 17 Pro series”“Brands limited incentives amid soaring global memory chip prices”“Counterpoint projects China smartphone shipments to post ​double-digit decline this year” [](https://substack.com/redirect/36f3b2e5-1555-4308-a122-30d9c096c937?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA)“Smartphone sales in China fell 13% ‌year-on-year during the month-long 618 shopping festival, as brands raised prices to offset higher memory costs, according to data from Counterpoint Research.”

The data, while over a short period, offers some dramatic drops in sales metrics.

> “Sales declined from May 26 to June 21, with all major Chinese brands except Huawei posting double-digit drops as fewer promotions compared ​to last year weighed on demand. Honor sales dropped 33%, while Xiaomi’s [(1810.HK) , opens new tab](https://substack.com/redirect/602e0b44-f52b-4e87-b09f-88a761952746?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA) fell 24%.”“Higher memory prices ​amid a rapid build-out of AI infrastructure have pushed up handset costs this year, leaving ⁠brands with less room to offer steep discounts during the 618 festival, seen as a barometer for ​the country’s booming e-commerce sector.” [](https://substack.com/redirect/621b4117-6954-4a5f-878e-00adf81cf128?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA)

And a lot of shifting around in product portfolios of the companies in question.

> “Some older and newer models from Chinese smartphone brands were priced higher than comparable models ​a year earlier, while discounts during this year’s 618 festival were generally less aggressive, both in terms of the size of price cuts and the range of products covered,” said Ivan Lam, senior analyst at Counterpoint Research.”

[Apple](https://substack.com/redirect/1b2345f2-4f05-49f2-811a-664be3006e63?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA)maintained it relative advantage given its global brand and [supply chain moat.](https://substack.com/redirect/fa6cf546-f0ff-4799-af1f-32a5eb3b884b?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA)

> “Apple’s prices were broadly unchanged, but its ​discounts were also smaller.”“Huawei Technologies [RIC:RIC:HWT.UL] led the market with a 21% share, and was the only major brand ​to record year-on-year growth during the 618 period, with sales rising 19%.”“Its Enjoy 90 Pro Max was its best-selling model. The ‌Mate 80 ⁠also performed well, supported by promotions.”

Apple wasn’t unscathed of course. But did better on a [relative basis](https://substack.com/redirect/d6fb1886-dbe9-46a3-92b2-916c55f4933a?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA).

> “Apple’s sales fell 9% from a year earlier, although the U.S. tech giant climbed to the No. 2 spot after rolling out incentives about a month ahead of June 18.”“The discounts offered savings of up to 2,000 yuan ($295) on the iPhone 17 Pro series through a mix of official price cuts, ​platform subsidies and trade-in deals.”“Still, ​Apple’s sales remained lower ⁠than a year earlier, partly because promotions for the iPhone 16 series were more aggressive during the same period last year.”“[The 618 festival](https://substack.com/redirect/a32dd8cf-659e-45d9-86ec-8332f4ceeae4?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA), which began as a one-day ​event marking JD.com’s founding on June 18, 1998, has since grown into a ​month-long sales campaign, ⁠with major e-commerce platforms competing for consumer spending.”

The [whole piece](https://substack.com/redirect/dfad36b1-7b9f-4d9f-a82c-279df06e797b?j=eyJ1IjoiOG5lN29xIn0.bqmJ9TjykdtTXCviJ3jD2X5vxhprRGd5tpCaC89FQIA) is worth a read on the full scale of the diminishing metrics.
