Fable 5 came back yesterday after over two weeks offline, this time with different terms: usage caps, a credit model, and a filter that reroutes some work to a weaker model. The larger point is not whether it’s up or down or different than it was when it first launched. The last few weeks have made it very clear that we cannot depend on intelligence being available in a particular model format or through a particular pipe. We rent the intelligence. We do not own it.

That changes the skill that actually matters. When the model you want can be throttled, repriced, or pulled behind a policy line overnight, the useful move is no longer picking a favorite. It is figuring out which intelligence to point at which job, so that when one pipe closes you already know where the work goes next.

Most of us are not doing that. We open the same model out of habit, pay frontier prices for work a cheaper model would crush, and freeze when that model disappears. Meanwhile you have a deck to build, a call to summarize, a spreadsheet to clean, a bug to fix, and thirty minutes before the next meeting. You do not need a geopolitical thesis before breakfast. You need to know which AI to open first.

Here’s what’s inside:

- The routing move companies already made. Why Coinbase and Cursor stopped sending every task to the frontier, and what that tells you about your own bill.

- Start with the job, not the model. The questions that decide where work goes before a single model name enters the conversation.

- Daily driver, workhorse, frontier. When to pay for breadth, when a cheap model wins, and how to tell the difference in advance.

- Specialists for senses, sources, and actions. Why the model matters less than the harness once a job needs eyes, live data, or hands.

- Grab the model-picker prompt. Describe any task and it tells you which AI to open, how to check the output, and what you can safely move off the expensive model this week.

The model market is getting wider, cheaper, and more confusing at the same time. Describe the work before you pick the model.
