## 3. Forward guidance

Management raised and extended guidance materially:

- **FY26:** non-GAAP EPS $3.35–$3.45 (raised >40% from prior); revenue growth 29–33%; free cash flow at least $3.5B (up from a prior ~$2B target). (transcript summary, Guidance and Outlook)
- **FY27 (new):** consolidated revenue growth 8–12%; operating margin 12–16%; EPS growth 12–16%; free cash flow at least $4.5B. (transcript summary)
- **Tone / philosophy:** ranges rather than point estimates, framed conservatively against a "record backlog" and supply-gated conversion. CEO Antonio Neri repeatedly anchored the outlook on "durable" AI-deployment and data-center-modernization demand, explicitly stating no evidence of order pull-ins or cancellations (Q&A, Simon Leopold / Raymond James). Management notably did **not** assume supply improvement in FY27 — framing supply relief as upside, not base case (Q&A, Amit Daryanani / Evercore).

Direction: up and to the right, with the unusual feature of issuing a confident next-fiscal-year frame a full year early — a high-conviction signal.

## 4. Thesis read by theme

### ai_infrastructure_capex

- **Status:** Confirm
- **Evidence:** Cloud & AI revenue +23% to $7.7B; $1.8B in new AI orders in the quarter and a $5.9B AI backlog; FY26 Cloud & AI revenue growth outlook raised to low-20% range (from prior mid-to-high single digits). (transcript summary, Cloud & AI section)
- **Implication:** HPE is converting the AI-infrastructure capex wave into both orders and a multi-quarter backlog, validating the thesis. The lifting of the Cloud & AI growth band is the single largest positive revision in the print.

### networking_competitive_landscape

- **Status:** Confirm
- **Evidence:** Networking revenue $2.7B, +10% normalized, with orders growing far faster than revenue — Campus & Branch orders up in the upper-20% range, Data Center Networking orders ~+20%, security orders +18%, service-provider routing orders ~+30%. Juniper integration running ahead of synergy milestones; FY26 "networks for AI" order target raised to at least $2B; management guided mid-to-high-20% networking margins in FY27. (transcript summary, Networking section; Q&A David Vogt / UBS, Matthew Niknam / Truist)
- **Implication:** The Juniper acquisition is tracking ahead of plan on both revenue synergies and margin uplift, and networking+security convergence is being pursued "at the silicon level" — strengthening HPE's competitive position in enterprise AI networking. Strong confirm.

### enterprise_ai_adoption

- **Status:** Confirm
- **Evidence:** GreenLake customer count reached ~50,000 with net retention near 110%; management highlighted "early success in embedding agentic AI across offerings," cross-portfolio deals (Servers/Storage/Networking), and named enterprise/sovereign wins (Lowe's, Dallas Cowboys, UK Ministry of Justice). Neri pointed to enterprise and sovereign markets as both growth and higher-margin vs service providers. (transcript summary; Q&A Katherine Murphy / Goldman Sachs, Erik Woodring / Morgan Stanley)
- **Implication:** Enterprise AI adoption is showing up as consumption growth (GreenLake NRR ~110%) and a mix shift toward higher-margin enterprise/sovereign inferencing — supportive of both the demand and margin sides of the thesis.

### data_center_deployment_constraints

- **Status:** Confirm (with nuance — supply is the binding constraint)
- **Evidence:** Management repeatedly flagged component/supply constraints — memory (DRAM, NAND), DDR4/5, wafers, networking hardware — as the **primary gating factor for revenue conversion** in both FY26 and FY27, while demand and backlog remain robust. Neri expects AI-system conversion to accelerate "as memory becomes available later in 2027" and does not assume material supply improvement in FY27. (transcript summary, Supply Chain section; Q&A Daryanani / Evercore, Samik Chatterjee / JPMorgan)
- **Implication:** This is the thesis playing out exactly as named — deployment constraints are real and binding, but they constrain *timing/conversion*, not demand. The constraint is a revenue-recognition tailwind deferred, not lost, and represents explicit upside if supply eases. Confirm.

### hyperscaler_revenue_concentration

- **Status:** Drift (favorable)
- **Evidence:** Management explicitly framed HPE's AI strategy around **enterprise and sovereign** markets and inferencing, noting these are "typically more profitable compared to service providers." The opportunity mix was described as balanced across product segments. (Q&A Katherine Murphy / Goldman Sachs; Wamsi Mohan / BofA)
- **Implication:** Rather than leaning into lumpy, low-margin hyperscaler/service-provider AI cluster revenue, HPE is positioning away from hyperscaler concentration toward enterprise/sovereign mix. This is a favorable drift on the concentration thesis — the magnitude/mechanism of HPE's AI revenue is shifting toward more durable, higher-margin demand, reducing single-customer-cluster risk. Does not break the thesis; refines it positively.

## 8. Management Q&A flags

1. **Supply, not demand, is the constraint (challenging exchange).** Amit Daryanani (Evercore) pressed on whether demand or component availability is the binding growth constraint. Neri conceded supply availability is the key factor and explicitly did **not** assume material supply improvement in FY27 — framing any relief as upside. Unusual emphasis: management is sandbagging FY27 by baking in continued scarcity, which makes the guide more defensible.
2. **Abrupt Cloud & AI outlook change questioned (challenging exchange).** Erik Woodring (Morgan Stanley) challenged the sudden raise in Cloud & AI demand outlook (mid/high-single-digit → low-20s). Neri attributed it to broad-based acceleration (traditional servers, agentic AI, GreenLake pull-through) rather than one driver — a credible but notably large revision worth monitoring for durability.
3. **AI systems profitability.** Katherine Murphy (Goldman Sachs) probed AI-system margins; management steered to the enterprise/sovereign mix being structurally more profitable than service-provider AI — a deliberate framing that the AI revenue is higher quality than headline cluster deals.
4. **EPS-vs-consensus gap not directly addressed.** The reported $0.79 non-GAAP EPS sits well above the FactSet pre-print consensus high ($0.55); no analyst directly reconciled the magnitude on the call, leaving open whether part of the beat reflects normalization/tax/one-time items vs. clean operating outperformance. Flag for model refresh.
5. **Storage revenue-recognition optics.** Tim Long (Barclays) — Alletra MP growing triple digits in orders, but SaaS-based revenue recognition smooths reported storage revenue. Worth tracking that order strength leads reported revenue here.
