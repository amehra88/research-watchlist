# Mathematicians will become art curators - Grant Sanderson

Here are the investment insights from the podcast episode.

**AI Sector Trend: The Enduring Value of the "Human Curation" Layer**

The core investment thesis is that as generative AI becomes a commodity, the primary source of value will shift from raw generation to the human-led curation, validation, and application of AI-generated output.

- **Investment Thesis:** The most defensible business models in AI will not be those that simply generate content (e.g., code, text, math proofs), but those that build platforms to help experts navigate, select, and build upon the "nearly infinite space" of AI outputs. This creates a "human-in-the-loop" moat.

- **Valuation Implications:**
    - **Bullish:** Favor companies providing "co-pilot" tools that enhance expert workflows in specific verticals (e.g., engineering, legal, finance, creative design). The value lies in the user interface, the established trust with the user, and the workflow integration—not just the underlying model. These companies can command premium valuations.
    - **Bearish (Implied):** Pure-play services offering raw, uncurated access to generative models may face significant commoditization and margin pressure over time.

- **Competitive Dynamics:** The discussion suggests a durable role for human-centric platforms even if the AI models themselves become technically superior at a task. The "social phenomenon" of trusting a human guide or a familiar brand interface to make sense of complex information will remain a key differentiator. This suggests that established enterprise software companies integrating AI into trusted workflows have a significant advantage over new, standalone AI tools.
