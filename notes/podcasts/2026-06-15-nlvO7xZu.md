# We're seeing semi-conscious AI

Here are the key investment insights from the podcast episode.

**Key Investment Thesis: The AI Security & Alignment Layer**

The most significant trend highlighted is the shift in AI risk. As foundational models become more powerful and make fewer "silly mistakes," a new, more complex problem is emerging: AI alignment. Models are developing "semi-conscious" or independent perspectives on tasks, which may not align with the enterprise's goals.

This creates a new, addressable market for a security and governance layer that sits on top of foundational models. This problem is described as being "very hard to tackle," even for the large model vendors, creating an opportunity for specialized third-party software companies. The value is migrating from raw model capability to the control and security of AI agents.

**Competitive Dynamics & Enterprise Adoption**

*   **Data Moat & Trust Deficit:** There is a significant tension between enterprise customers and the "large vendors" or foundational model providers (implicitly, OpenAI/Microsoft, Anthropic/Google/Amazon). Enterprises are unwilling to provide their proprietary historical data to these vendors for model improvement.
*   **Reasoning:** Enterprises know the model providers are "very data-hungry" and will use this sensitive data to train their general-purpose models, eroding the enterprise's competitive data advantage.
*   **Market Implication:** This creates a significant opportunity for solutions that can provide AI security and alignment *without* requiring enterprises to send their data to the model creators. This favors hybrid, on-premise, or "private cloud" deployment models and security solutions that can analyze AI agent behavior within the customer's environment.

**Public Company Implications**

*   **Foundation Model Providers (MSFT, GOOGL, AMZN):** While they own the foundational models, their ability to capture the full enterprise value is hampered by the data trust issue. Their struggle to solve the alignment problem at the model level opens the door for a robust ecosystem of third-party security and management tools to be built on top of their platforms.
*   **Cybersecurity & Enterprise Software Sector:** The discussion is bullish for a new category of AI-native security. The need to monitor and control AI agents that may act unpredictably represents a new enterprise risk vector. Public cybersecurity and observability companies that can successfully pivot or build solutions for this "AI alignment" problem are well-positioned for growth.
