# Travel Through the Lens of AI with with Booking.com CEO Glenn Fogel

**Key Investor Insights**

The most significant insights from Booking.com's CEO Glenn Fogel concern the durability of Booking Holdings' (BKNG) business in the AI era, its competitive standing against Airbnb (ABNB), and its aggressive capital return strategy. He also offers a sober, dot-com-era perspective on the current AI hype cycle.

**Booking Holdings (BKNG) - Bullish Commentary**

*   **Competitive Dynamics vs. Airbnb (ABNB):** Booking's "alternative accommodations" business (e.g., home rentals) is approximately 75% the size of Airbnb's globally and has been growing at a faster rate than Airbnb for the past five years. This challenges the market perception of Airbnb's dominance in the category.
*   **Operational Moat:** Fogel argues that while a pure "technology moat" doesn't exist, Booking's competitive advantage lies in the immense complexity of its two-sided marketplace. This includes managing millions of partner relationships, navigating complex global payment and regulatory systems, and handling customer service at scale. He believes new AI-native entrants underestimate these real-world operational challenges.
*   **Capital Allocation (Shareholder Returns):** Booking has a strong focus on returning capital to shareholders. The company has repurchased approximately 40% of its outstanding shares over the last 12 years. In Q4 2023 alone, it bought back $3.6 billion in stock and paid out over $300 million in dividends. The company prioritizes reinvestment only when a high ROI is evident; otherwise, capital is returned to shareholders.

**AI/Tech Sector Trends & Valuations**

*   **Parallel to the Dot-com Bubble:** Fogel directly compares the current AI enthusiasm to the late 1990s internet boom, predicting a similar pattern of speculative boom, massive innovation, and an eventual washout with many failures. He cautions investors about the complexity of applying new technology to disrupt established, operationally intensive industries.
*   **Cost of AI ("Token Economics"):** The cost of using large language models is a critical factor. Fogel states that Booking is carefully analyzing the ROI and "token economics" of different AI models for various tasks, suggesting that the high cost of inference is a real business consideration that may favor more efficient or specialized models over time.
*   **AI for Efficiency:** AI is already a tangible driver of operating leverage. Fogel confirmed that on their Q1 earnings call, they reported that customer service costs per contact are decreasing while customer satisfaction scores are increasing, driven by AI implementation.

**Regulatory & Macro Factors**

*   **Regulation as a Barrier to Entry:** The global travel industry is highly regulated in diverse ways across different countries. This complex legal and compliance landscape serves as a significant barrier for new entrants, benefiting large, established global players like Booking Holdings.
*   **Risk of AI Backlash:** The rapid speed of AI-driven job displacement poses a societal challenge. Fogel expresses concern that if this transition isn't managed well (e.g., through retraining), it could lead to a political and social backlash against rapid technological adoption.
