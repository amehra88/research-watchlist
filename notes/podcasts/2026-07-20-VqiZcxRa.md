# Why Cheap Energy Always Creates Its Own Demand

**Core Investment Thesis: AI for Developer Productivity**

The primary investment insight is the significant economic value in using AI to automate the "low-leverage" tasks of highly-paid software engineers. The argument is that valuable engineering talent is currently wasted on tasks like fixing CI/CD pipelines, writing documentation, and administrative work.

This creates a major tailwind for tools and platforms that increase developer efficiency, freeing up engineers to focus on high-value problem-solving and innovation.

**Public Company Implications & Sector Trends**

*   **Bullish on AI-Powered DevTools:** The trend directly benefits public companies providing AI-native tools for the software development lifecycle. This thesis supports a bullish view on companies that can demonstrate clear ROI by automating developer work.
    *   **Microsoft (MSFT):** A primary beneficiary through its ownership of GitHub and the widespread adoption of GitHub Copilot, which directly addresses the coding and documentation tasks mentioned.
    *   **GitLab (GTLB):** As a comprehensive DevOps platform, its integration of AI features to automate testing, integration, and deployment workflows is a key catalyst.
    *   **Atlassian (TEAM):** Its dominance in project management (Jira) and documentation (Confluence) makes it a natural platform to embed AI assistants that streamline the non-coding aspects of engineering work.

*   **Productivity as a Margin Driver for Tech Incumbents:** For all large-cap tech companies (e.g., META, GOOGL, AMZN), the adoption of these AI tools internally is a key lever for improving R&D efficiency and operating margins. An engineering organization that becomes 10-20% more productive without increasing headcount translates directly to the bottom line.
