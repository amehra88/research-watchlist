# Why Inventing General Relativity Is the Final Test for AI - Adam Brown

**AI/Tech Sector Trends**

*   **Bullish Long-Term AI Capability Thesis:** The speaker posits that even if Large Language Models (LLMs) are fundamentally "interpolators," their capabilities are rapidly advancing up a "chain of abstractions." The core investment insight is that at a sufficiently high level of abstraction, this process can produce outputs equivalent to groundbreaking human invention (the example used is deriving general relativity from Newtonian physics). This implies an extremely high ceiling for the value creation potential of AI, supporting a long-term bullish outlook on the entire sector.
*   **AGI Timeline Signal:** A potential 10-year timeline is floated for AI to achieve the capability of making a leap equivalent to Einstein's discovery of general relativity. This suggests that transformative, AGI-level scientific and economic impact is a medium-to-long-term investment horizon to consider, rather than an immediate event or a distant fantasy.

**Specific Public Company Mentions**

*   Logos for OpenAI (ties to Microsoft, MSFT), Google (GOOGL), and Anthropic are shown as examples of current LLM developers. However, there is no specific bullish or bearish commentary on any individual company's strategy or competitive position. The discussion treats them as a group representing the current state of the art.

**Valuation and Market Commentary**

*   No specific price targets, valuation multiples, or market calls were made. The commentary is a high-level, qualitative assessment of the ultimate potential of the technology, which underpins the long-term valuation case for leaders in the AI space.
