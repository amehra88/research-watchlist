# Cooking with OpenAI’s Research Chief: AGI, o1, Evals, and Scaling Laws — Mark Chen

Here are the key investment insights from the podcast episode with OpenAI's Mark Chen.

### Top AI/Tech Sector Trends

*   **Bullish on Scaling Laws:** OpenAI's research chief firmly believes in the continued effectiveness of scaling laws. He strongly disagrees with "bear takes" that "pre-training is dead." He views the path to AGI as more of the same: "more careful research engineering, more careful data engineering, more careful scaling." This is a strong signal that the core thesis of bigger models, more data, and more compute will continue to drive progress. This outlook is bullish for the entire compute supply chain (e.g., NVDA) and hyperscalers providing the infrastructure (e.g., MSFT, GOOGL, AMZN).
*   **The "Evals Crisis":** Existing benchmarks are becoming saturated as models get more capable. A key challenge for the field is developing new, difficult evaluations ("evals") to accurately measure progress toward superhuman intelligence. OpenAI's philosophy is to separate the teams building models from the teams creating evaluations to avoid "benchmark-maxing" or teaching to the test.
*   **Rise of Agentic AI:** The impact of AI is expanding from single-shot tasks to complex, "long horizon meaningful work." This shift towards AI agents that can perform multi-step tasks is a key trend, indicating a significant increase in the potential economic value and scope of AI applications, particularly in enterprise software and automation.
*   **Multimodality is Key:** Models need to be trained on more than just text. Integrating vision and other data types is crucial for providing models with the necessary context to understand and operate in the real world, a limitation of text-only models.

### Competitive Dynamics

*   **Intense Talent War:** Chen confirms the "soup wars" anecdote, where Meta's Mark Zuckerberg (META) was personally trying to poach AI researchers. He states OpenAI responded in kind and feels they "came out on top," highlighting the intense, high-stakes competition for elite AI talent between the leading labs.
*   **Breakthroughs as a Recruiting Tool:** Google's (GOOGL) AlphaGo moment is cited as a major turning point that inspired many, including Chen, to enter the field. This underscores the strategic importance of major research breakthroughs for attracting top-tier talent and mindshare.
*   **Broadening the Talent Pool:** Top AI talent is not limited to those with PhDs. OpenAI actively recruits from quantitative fields like high-frequency trading (HFT), valuing skills in creative problem-solving and "brutal hard optimization."

### Capital Allocation Signals

*   **Compute as the Core Bet:** OpenAI's primary mechanism for allocating resources to research is through compute. Their strategy is to make a small number (3-5) of large, high-conviction compute bets per organization, rather than spreading resources thinly. This signals a continued focus on large-scale training runs as the primary driver of capability improvements.
