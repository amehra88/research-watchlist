# Don't trust OpenAI. Use your own models.

**Key Investment Thesis: Platform Risk in AI**

The primary investment risk highlighted is "platform risk." Large technology platform companies have a historical playbook of encouraging developers to build on their platform, only to later compete with and disintermediate the most successful applications. Investors should be highly cautious of any company, public or private, whose business is entirely dependent on a single major AI platform. The advice for builders is "use your own models," suggesting a potential bullish case for companies enabling model sovereignty and open-source solutions.

**Public Company Commentary**

*   **Microsoft (MSFT):** The speaker presents a bearish view on companies partnering with Microsoft or building on its platforms (including its proxy, OpenAI). He cites the historical example of MSFT replacing its partners, such as Lotus 1-2-3 and WordPerfect, with its own dominant products (Excel and Word). The warning is that this behavior will repeat in the AI era.
*   **Meta Platforms (META):** Mentioned alongside Microsoft as a company that has historically used the same platform playbook. Zuckerberg leveraged the Facebook platform to attract developers, observe their success, and then either copy their features or cut off their access.

**AI/Tech Sector Trends & Competitive Dynamics**

*   **The Inevitability of Platform Competition:** Foundational AI model providers (like OpenAI) are not just infrastructure players. To justify massive valuations, they are forced to move up the stack and capture the application layer. This means they will inevitably compete with companies building applications on top of their APIs.
*   **"Free" Credits as a Competitive Weapon:** The strategy of offering free credits or API tokens (as OpenAI is doing) is not a sign of partnership but an aggressive customer acquisition and market intelligence tactic. It allows the platform owner to see emerging use cases and innovations, which they can then incorporate into their own products. This stifles the long-term moat of application-layer companies.

**Valuation & Capital Allocation Commentary**

*   **High Valuations Drive Aggressive Behavior:** OpenAI's massive (private) valuation is cited as a key driver for its strategic necessity to win at the application layer. This pressure to grow into its valuation means it cannot afford to let a rich ecosystem of third-party applications flourish and capture most of the value. This implies a future of aggressive "build vs. buy" decisions from major AI players, leading to market consolidation.
