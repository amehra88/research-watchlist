# Senators John Fetterman and Dave McCormick: Bipartisanship, Money in DC, Datacenters, Graham Platner

**AI Infrastructure & Data Centers: The Core Investment Thesis**

-   The AI build-out requires a massive expansion of data centers, creating an enormous and potentially underestimated demand for power. One new data center complex in Pennsylvania will require 3.4 gigawatts of power, transitioning a former coal plant to natural gas to meet the demand. This is a strong bullish signal for utilities, power generation companies, natural gas producers, and the entire energy infrastructure supply chain.
-   The build-out is creating a "blue-collar boom." Data center construction creates thousands of jobs for trades (welders, electricians), with follow-on economic benefits for logistics, hotels, and restaurants in the surrounding areas.
-   Hardware refresh cycles are a key driver. The hardware (servers, chips) within data centers is expected to be upgraded every 3-4 years, indicating a sustained, multi-year demand cycle for semiconductor and hardware manufacturers.

**Regulatory & Geopolitical Tailwinds for AI**

-   There is strong bipartisan sentiment framing the AI race against China as a national security imperative. Senator Fetterman explicitly stated, "You want America to build that chassis, or do you want the Chinese to do that?" This suggests continued government support for domestic AI development and infrastructure.
-   A growing political backlash against moratoriums on data centers is forming. Fetterman called the idea of a moratorium a "China-first policy," arguing it would cede the AI race. This opposition to regulatory headwinds is a positive signal for investors in data center REITs (e.g., EQIX, DLR), power companies, and AI hardware suppliers.

**Macro AI Sector Trends**

-   AI is viewed as a foundational technology that is transforming all major sectors, including life sciences, defense, and energy. This reinforces the thesis that AI is a horizontal enabler, and its impact on public company valuations will be broad and not confined to the tech sector alone.
