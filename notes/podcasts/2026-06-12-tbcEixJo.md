# Fable Show & Tell + Goodfire's New Intentional Design Techniques

Here are the key public market investment insights from the podcast episode.

### Key Investment Theses & Sector Trends

*   **Autonomous Agents as the Next Platform:** The development of sophisticated AI agents (showcased by Goodfire) is presented as the next major computing paradigm, potentially disrupting traditional enterprise software-as-a-service (SaaS). The core investment thesis is that value will shift from applications with manual workflows to agentic platforms that can execute complex, multi-step tasks autonomously.
*   **Intense, Sustained Compute Demand:** The advanced capabilities in both generative video (Fable) and autonomous agents (Goodfire) require massive and ongoing computational power. This reinforces the high-demand thesis for the entire AI infrastructure stack, from semiconductors to cloud providers. The complexity of these new applications suggests that demand for training and, crucially, inference is not peaking.
*   **Value Accrual to the Infrastructure Layer:** The discussion highlighted that the cost of inference for these advanced applications remains a significant bottleneck. This suggests that in the near-to-medium term, the most durable value capture will remain with the "picks and shovels" providers—semiconductors and hyperscalers—who benefit directly from this high compute consumption.

### Competitive Dynamics

*   **Hyperscalers (MSFT, GOOGL, AMZN):** The episode reinforces their central role as the primary platforms for AI development and deployment. While private companies like Fable and Goodfire are innovating on top, they are ultimately customers of the hyperscalers' compute and model APIs. The race is on for hyperscalers to offer the best native agentic frameworks (e.g., Microsoft Copilot) to prevent value from leaking to third-party agent platforms.
*   **Semiconductors (NVDA):** The visual demonstration of generative video and complex agent tasks serves as a direct, tangible indicator of the unabated demand for high-performance GPUs. The capabilities shown are computationally expensive, supporting a bullish outlook on continued capital expenditure flowing to NVIDIA.
*   **Enterprise Software (CRM, NOW, etc.):** The vision for autonomous agents poses a significant long-term disruptive threat to incumbent enterprise SaaS companies. The core premise of a platform like Goodfire is to automate workflows that are currently performed manually within applications like Salesforce or ServiceNow. This could commoditize the value of the traditional SaaS interface over time.
*   **Creative Software (ADBE):** The rapid progress of generative video models from new entrants like Fable represents direct competitive pressure for Adobe. While Adobe is integrating its own Firefly models, the speed of innovation from focused startups indicates that its market leadership in creative tools is not guaranteed in the AI era.

### Valuation & Capital Allocation Commentary

*   **High Inference Costs Pressure Application Margins:** A key takeaway is the high cost and token consumption associated with running complex agents. This is a critical point for valuing any public application-layer AI company. High operating costs for inference could compress gross margins, making profitability a challenge even with strong customer adoption.
*   **Sustained Capex by Hyperscalers:** The computational requirements for agents and video generation directly validate the massive capital expenditure cycles at Microsoft (MSFT), Google (GOOGL), Amazon (AMZN), and Meta (META). This spending is not speculative; it is being directed toward enabling these next-generation workloads.
