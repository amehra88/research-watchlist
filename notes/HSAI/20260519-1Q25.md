## 3. Forward guidance

Management was unusually specific and held the line:

- **Q2'25 revenue:** CNY 680-720M (USD 93.7-99.2M), +48-57% YoY
- **Q2'25 shipments:** >300k units (~+250% YoY)
- **Full-year 2025:** revenue CNY 3.0-3.5B (unchanged), shipments 1.2-1.5M units (unchanged), gross margin ~40%, GAAP net profit CNY 200-350M
- **GAAP breakeven target:** Q2'25 (pulled in)
- **CapEx 2025:** USD 30-50M
- **OpEx:** targeting ~CNY 100M annual savings; R&D stable in absolute terms via platform-based development
- **Tariff assumption:** baked 45% U.S. tariff into the 2025 guide; describes impact as "immaterial"

Guidance philosophy is point-range, not point estimate, and the tone leaned ambitious-but-disciplined — they reaffirmed despite a "dynamic tariff environment" rather than widening ranges defensively. The pull-in of GAAP breakeven to Q2 (from prior implied later in the year) is the meaningful tonal change.

### autonomous_vehicle_competition (proposed)

- **Status:** Confirm
- **Evidence:** ATX LiDAR (~$200 ASP) hit mass production in Q1'25 with ~40,000 units shipped in its debut quarter; 12 OEM design wins including Li Auto's entire L Series, Zeekr, Great Wall, Chery, Geely. Leapmotor D10 — 15,000 orders in the first hour, 70% opting for LiDAR. Robotaxi LiDAR partnerships with Baidu Apollo Go and DiDi, with management noting a structural transition from mechanical to ADAS-style LiDAR for robotaxi fleets to enable scale. Robotics LiDAR shipments ~50k in Q1, >600% YoY.
- **Implication:** Hesai is establishing itself as the volume-share leader in China LiDAR with a clear product ladder (ATX mainstream / ATL customized / ATP+AT1440+ETX premium) and is converting the AV-adjacent ADAS opportunity into actual units shipped. The China AV competition theme is reading toward consolidation around Hesai on the sensor layer, with US-listed peers (LAZR, MBLY for ADAS, INDI tangentially) on the outside looking in for Chinese volume.

### automotive_semiconductor_demand (proposed)

- **Status:** Confirm
- **Evidence:** Stable LiDAR content per car at $500-$1,000 even as unit ASPs decline; 70% LiDAR attach rate on the Leapmotor D10's opening sales; full-year shipment guide of 1.2-1.5M units implies LiDAR is migrating from premium-only to mass-market China EV.
- **Implication:** The China auto semi demand picture is being pulled by ADAS feature attach as much as by unit volume. LiDAR content per car holding stable as ASP per unit falls means multi-LiDAR architectures are migrating downmarket — bullish for total LiDAR semi content even if per-unit pricing erodes.

### humanoid_robotics_competition (proposed)

- **Status:** Drift (positive)
- **Evidence:** Robotics LiDAR revenue scaling fast (>600% YoY, ~50k Q1 units); JT LiDAR with a 300k-unit order for robotic lawnmowers / smart home robotics, delivering over 12 months; ongoing logistics LiDAR for long-haul and last-mile.
- **Implication:** Robotics is becoming a real second leg, not just a side bet. Magnitude of contribution is small in dollars today but the order-book trajectory (single 300k-unit deals) suggests robotics LiDAR could meaningfully diversify HSAI's customer concentration risk away from China auto OEMs over the next 12-24 months. For the broader humanoid/robotics theme, this is a sensor-layer enabler signal — when humanoid programs scale, Hesai is one of very few cost-down vision/depth players able to meet automotive-grade reliability at consumer price points.

### china_us_tensions / china_export_controls (proposed)

- **Status:** Drift (still net-positive, but with explicit hedging)
- **Evidence:** U.S. is only 10% of projected 2025 revenue; only 5% is DDP (Hesai bears tariff cost); 45% tariff baked into the guide; Southeast Asia factory targeted for late 2026 / early 2027; legal — U.S. District Court dismissed a patent infringement case against Hesai in early May 2025 with no conditions; management explicitly addressed NASDAQ delisting rumors as having "no legal or factual basis" and declined to comment on Hong Kong listing speculation.
- **Implication:** The China-US friction premium that the market has historically applied to HSAI is being actively de-risked operationally (SEA factory, US revenue concentration capped) and legally (patent dismissal). However, the ADR delisting / Hong Kong listing question remains an unresolved overhang that management addressed only with reassurance, not with a concrete path. For names with similar China-listed-on-NASDAQ exposure (BIDU, BABA), HSAI's commentary is a useful read-across that the operational mitigations are tractable but the listing structure question is unresolved.

## 8. Management Q&A flags

1. **Tim Hsiao (Morgan Stanley) — challenging exchange on competition:** Hsiao raised concerns about peers reportedly winning projects at Xiaomi and Leapmotor accounts. Andrew Fan's response sidestepped the specific accounts and pivoted to "official orders and production, not rumors, matter" plus emphasizing data compatibility / vehicle design switching costs. **Flag:** the dodge on named accounts is notable. Worth tracking whether Xiaomi and Leapmotor's next vehicle programs continue to feature Hesai, or whether multi-sourcing creeps in.
2. **Tim Hsiao — Infinity Eye details:** Yifan Li gave a generic "adaptability and stable gross margins" answer. The platform was just launched April 2025, so concrete revenue contribution will be a future-quarter watchpoint.
3. **Jessie Lo (BofA) — ADAS ASP trend:** Fan acknowledged ASP shift toward lower price points but stressed content-per-car stability. The framing was disciplined; worth noting that "stable content per car" is a thesis-critical claim — if it slips, the whole "volume offsets ASP decline" argument breaks.
4. **Chunsheng Xie (Huatai) — global OEM timing:** Fan was forward-looking and cited the European exclusive design win and POCs, but did not pin down NRE income magnitude or specific delivery timeline. **Flag:** "rapidly gaining momentum" is doing a lot of work in the global OEM narrative; revenue contribution remains small.
5. **No analyst pressed on NASDAQ delisting / Hong Kong listing.** Management addressed it unprompted in prepared remarks. The unprompted address suggests management thinks it's an investor concern; that no analyst pushed further is itself notable — could be that the sell-side has been told not to engage, or that the topic is considered already-priced-in.
