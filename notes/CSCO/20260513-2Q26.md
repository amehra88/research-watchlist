## 3. Forward guidance

Three explicit forward statements:

1. **Q3'26 (April-quarter):** Revenue $15.4–15.6B; non-GAAP GM 65.5–66.5%; non-GAAP EPS $1.02–1.04. GM range is **a step down from Q2 actual (67.5%)** — explicitly attributed to continued memory cost flow-through, partially offset by mix and price actions taking effect.
2. **FY26 (full year):** Revenue $61.2–61.7B; non-GAAP EPS $4.13–4.17. Management characterized FY26 as "Cisco's strongest year yet."
3. **AI infrastructure orders FY26:** Now expected to exceed **$5B** (up from the prior $3B+ AI revenue framing); with **>$3B of AI infrastructure revenue from hyperscalers** flowing through in FY26 and the remainder anchoring FY27. Non-hyperscaler AI pipeline >$2.5B. Some current orders explicitly characterized as FY27 revenue events.

Guidance philosophy: management retained its preferred point-range format (no shift to point estimates). Tone is confident but not aggressive — Robbins/Patterson repeatedly framed AI orders as "nonlinear" and explicitly cautioned against extrapolating quarterly cadence. Tariff assumption: current tariffs/exemptions remain in place through end of FY26 — no incremental tariff degradation assumed.

## 4. Thesis read by theme

### networking_competitive_landscape

- **Status:** Confirm (strong)
- **Evidence:** Networking revenue +21% YoY; product orders +20%+ for sixth consecutive quarter; double-digit order growth in all major geographies and customer segments; Wi-Fi 7 +80% sequentially; data center switching had double-digit order growth in six of last eight quarters; new 102.4Tbps G300 chip launched, new Cisco 8000 and Nexus 9000 systems, plus two new pluggable optics. Robbins framed campus refresh as "top of the first inning." 60/40 systems/optics mix held steady in AI orders.
- **Implication:** This is the single strongest theme on the call. Networking is not a defensive cash cow here — it is reaccelerating on three independent vectors (campus refresh + AI infrastructure + Wi-Fi 7). The competitive-landscape framing in the watchlist implies CSCO is exposed to ANET/Arista, white-box, and hyperscaler in-housing pressure; this print is empirical evidence that CSCO is holding share AND growing in the AI silicon/optics layer specifically. Read-through positive for ANET (Tier 1, peer), CIEN (Tier 2), COHR/LITE (Tier 1 optics).

### ai_infrastructure_capex

- **Status:** Confirm (strong)
- **Evidence:** AI infrastructure orders $2.1B in Q2 alone (vs $1.3B Q1; matches full FY25 in a single quarter); FY26 AI order target raised to >$5B; >$3B AI infrastructure revenue from hyperscalers expected in FY26; non-hyperscaler AI pipeline >$2.5B; AMD + HUMAIN joint venture for 1GW AI infrastructure by 2030 (Phase 1: 100MW in Saudi Arabia); NVIDIA partnership engagement "early success"; management explicitly said the $5B AI target does NOT include P200 / G300 / Optics solutions revenue.
- **Implication:** CSCO has decisively transitioned from "AI-adjacent" framing to "AI-infrastructure-direct beneficiary." The fact that the $5B AI target excludes their newest silicon (P200, G300) and optics is a deliberate sandbag — suggests management has visibility on materially higher AI revenue in FY27. The AMD + HUMAIN JV signals sovereign-AI deployment exposure (Saudi-anchored) — a thesis vector not previously emphasized for CSCO. This drifts the thesis weight on CSCO **upward** within its tier.

### cybersecurity_competitive_landscape

- **Status:** Drift (negative tilt, near-term)
- **Evidence:** Security revenue **-4% YoY**, dragged by legacy product declines and Splunk's on-prem-to-cloud-subscription transition. Counter-narrative: new/refreshed security products (Secure Access, XDR, Hypershield, AI Defense) gaining traction with 1,000 new customers in Q2 (+100% sequential), 4,000+ since launch; refreshed firewalls posted third consecutive quarter of double-digit unit growth; 500 new Splunk logos in 1H, projecting 1,000 for the year. Management guided organic security to "approach double-digit revenue growth exiting Q4 2026" — explicitly behind prior expectations.
- **Implication:** Splunk integration is delivering customer traction but is currently a revenue headwind, not a tailwind. The "approaching double-digit exiting Q4" framing is a soft acknowledgment that the security narrative timeline has slipped. Thesis isn't broken — unit/customer metrics are positive — but the security business is not yet a growth contributor and won't be a clean story until FY27. Read-through cautious for PANW, CRWD, ZS exposure (security is consolidating faster than CSCO is monetizing the transition).

### observability_competitive_landscape

- **Status:** Drift (neutral, structural transition)
- **Evidence:** Splunk transition to cloud subscriptions is the dominant observability narrative on the call. Short-term revenue drag explicitly called out; long-term customer adoption framed as positive (1,000 new logo projection for FY26). 500 new logos in 1H. No direct read on competitive dynamics vs. DDOG, SNOW, NEW or open-source observability stack.
- **Implication:** Splunk is doing the right thing strategically (cloud-first) but the financial proof is deferred. CSCO is not yet differentiating in observability conversations on this call — the topic is treated as accounting timing rather than competitive positioning. For watchlist purposes, this is the weakest of CSCO's five themes — consider whether `observability_competitive_landscape` is genuinely a CSCO theme or whether it should be deprioritized vs. DDOG/SNOW as the cleaner expressions.

### enterprise_ai_adoption

- **Status:** Confirm (early-stage)
- **Evidence:** Use cases cited in financials (quant trading, fraud detection), manufacturing, pharma, retail (video analytics, mobile AI agents); over 90% of CSCO customer-support cases now involve AI; positive AI Summit feedback; management noted "strong investment in both cloud-based and on-prem architectures"; enterprise AI revenue contribution NOT expected to materially impact FY26 — meaningful ramp framed for FY27. Non-hyperscaler AI pipeline >$2.5B.
- **Implication:** Enterprise AI is real but not yet financially material for CSCO. This matches the broader watchlist read across enterprise-AI-exposed names (NOW, IOT, GWRE, PLTR, etc.) — adoption is broadening but revenue tail is FY27. CSCO's framing here is honest and consistent with peers. Confirms thesis without changing magnitude.

## 8. Management Q&A flags

InsiderScore flags **1 challenging analyst exchange** (Ben Reitzes / Melius). Five items worth surfacing:

1. **Reitzes (Melius) — gross margin / EPS guidance challenge.** Reitzes pressed that EPS guidance "might be higher than anticipated" given GM dynamics. Patterson explained GM pressure as "partly timing" and projected improvement with software growth into FY27; Robbins explicitly deferred to "follow-up after the call." The deferral is a tell — management is not yet ready to commit to a specific GM trajectory. Watch Q3'26 print for whether GM bottoms or steps down further.
2. **Rakers (Wells Fargo) — NVIDIA scale-up opportunity.** Robbins said CSCO "plans to participate in scale-up opportunities in the future" — read as: they're not there yet on scale-up (intra-rack / NVLink-equivalent) and are still anchored to scale-out (inter-rack Ethernet). This is a meaningful competitive gap vs. NVDA's NVLink ecosystem and ANET's recent scale-up positioning. Sandbag or genuine gap — unclear.
3. **Chatterjee (JPM) — CPO functionality in AI Optics.** Robbins affirmed belief in CPO (co-packaged optics) "eventual adoption" but said current customer preference is for "choice" (pluggables). This is consistent with COHR/LITE commentary that CPO adoption is slower than expected. CSCO is hedged on optics technology direction.
4. **Liani (BofA) — Q3-to-Q4 sequential growth seems low.** Patterson detailed networking strength; Robbins added that "nonlinear nature of the hyperscaler business adds some uncertainty to historical growth patterns." Translation: hyperscaler order lumpiness is real and management does not want to over-promise sequential cadence. Sandbag.
5. **Daryanani (Evercore) — Silicon One vs. Optics mix.** Robbins confirmed 60/40 systems/optics mix in AI orders is holding steady and clarified that the $5B AI target does NOT include P200, G300, or Optics solutions revenue — this is the most important sandbag of the call. Suggests FY27 AI revenue could surprise materially to the upside.
