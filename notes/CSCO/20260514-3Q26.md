# CSCO — 3Q26 earnings read

_Prepared by earnings-reviewer, 2026-05-14. Tier: T3._

## 1. Headline read

Q3'26 (April-quarter, reported 2026-05-13) is a clean and material **confirm/upgrade** of the 2Q26 read — every load-bearing thesis vector accelerated. Revenue beat consensus by ~1.6% on top of a 12% YoY print (record quarterly); EPS beat by ~2.5%; non-GAAP op margin held at 34.2%. The headline news is **AI infrastructure orders from hyperscalers running 4.5x prior-year totals** — FY26 hyperscaler AI order target raised from $5B (2Q26 call) to **$9B** in a single quarter, with FY27 AI revenue floor explicitly set at **$6B**. Networking product orders grew **>50%** and Acacia optics had its first >$1B order quarter. The margin drift flagged in 2Q26 (memory cost flow-through) is essentially resolved — GM came in at 66.0%, in line with the Q3'26 guide midpoint (65.5–66.5%) given in 2Q26, and management guided Q4'26 GM to roughly hold at ~66%. New nuance: a **$1B restructuring charge** (resource reallocation, not cost-out) and a much bigger AI design win footprint (5 new hyperscaler wins; Project Glasswing partnership). This print drifts the thesis materially upward in magnitude vs. the 2Q26 framing and provides explicit visibility into FY27.

## 3. Forward guidance

Three forward statements, each tighter and meaningfully higher than the 2Q26 versions:

1. **Q4'26 (July-quarter):** Revenue $16.7–16.9B; non-GAAP EPS $1.16–1.18; non-GAAP operating margin 34–35%. GM guide midpoint implied at ~66% (steady from Q3 actual). Revenue midpoint ($16.8B) is ~6.3% sequential growth from Q3, well above the +1.6% sequential implied in the 2Q26 framing.
2. **FY26 (full year):** Revenue raised to **$62.8–63.0B** (from $61.2–61.7B in 2Q26 — a ~+$1.5B raise at midpoint); non-GAAP EPS raised to **$4.27–4.29** (from $4.13–4.17 — +$0.13 at midpoint). FY26 now characterized again as "strongest year ever."
3. **AI infrastructure orders (hyperscalers, FY26):** Raised to **$9B** (from "exceed $5B" in 2Q26); **$4B** recognized as FY26 revenue, **>=$6B** explicitly guided as FY27 revenue. FY27 hyperscaler AI revenue floor is now in the public commentary set.
4. **Restructuring:** Up to $1B pretax charge announced — $450M in Q4 FY26, remainder in FY27. Management explicitly framed it as **resource reallocation toward silicon/optics/security/AI, not cost savings**. CFO declined to commit to operating margin improvement from it in FY27.

Guidance philosophy: management held the point-range format (no shift to point estimates). Tone went from "confident but not aggressive" (2Q26) to **confident-with-conviction** (3Q26). The explicit FY27 AI revenue floor of $6B is a notable departure — management does not typically guide out-quarter — and is the single most important guidance change on the call. Tariff assumption unchanged from 2Q26 (current tariffs/exemptions through end of FY26).
