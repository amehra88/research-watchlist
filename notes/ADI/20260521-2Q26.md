## 3. Forward guidance

Q3'26 (Aug-quarter) guide:
- Revenue: $3.9B (midpoint), ~8% above consensus mean of $3.61B and ~8% sequential.
- Operating margin: 49% at midpoint (flat sequentially — sustained at the elevated Q2 level).
- Tax rate: 12-14%.
- Adjusted EPS: $3.30 ± $0.15 — midpoint ~11% above $2.98 consensus.

Tone: confident, with explicit framing around being able to support "up to $20B in revenue" capacity by 2030 (vs. ~$14.5B run-rate implied by Q3 guide). Management called current gross margin (73%) a near-term ceiling — Q3 gross margin assumed to decline ~50bps due to non-recurrence of inventory repricing benefit, with mix a slight tailwind and utilization at peak. Pricing actions taken in 2026 contributing "a couple of points" to growth; volume — not further price — drove Q2 upside. Auto guided to mid- to high-single-digit sequential growth in Q3; Consumer guided to a single-digit sequential decline.

## 4. Thesis read by theme

### automotive_semiconductor_demand

- **Status:** Confirm
- **Evidence:** Auto +8% QoQ / +2% YoY (24% of mix). BMS for EVs returned to YoY growth for the first time in two years, with explicit strength in Europe and China. ADAS and infotainment (GMSL) contributing content + share gains. Customer inventory described as lean; Q2 saw record auto bookings. Guided to mid-/high-single-digit QoQ growth in Q3.
- **Implication:** Auto semis cycle has clearly inflected from inventory destock to content-led growth, with ADI's EV BMS franchise — a multi-quarter weak spot — finally turning. The lean-inventory + record-bookings combination is the cleanest setup for sustained sequential growth into 2H26.

### semiconductor_cycle

- **Status:** Confirm
- **Evidence:** Total revenue +15% sequential, +37% YoY, a record. Industrial +20% QoQ / +56% YoY with all sub-segments (automation, ATE, ETM, A&D, broad market) up sequentially and YoY. Aerospace/defense hit new revenue highs. Lead times stable, channel inventory at 6-7 weeks (normalized). Management characterized the environment as "reasonably calm" but with broad-based bookings strength.
- **Implication:** This is the strongest cycle confirmation from a broad-line analog name this earnings season. Industrial — the most cyclically-levered segment — printing +56% YoY with lean channel inventory points to a durable, content-and-cycle-driven upcycle rather than a sugar-high pull-in.

### data_center_deployment_constraints

- **Status:** Drift (positive — ADI is increasingly a beneficiary, not a constrained supplier)
- **Evidence:** Data center >75% of Communications revenue, +90% YoY, with optical and power portfolios each contributing. Empower Semiconductor acquisition explicitly framed as filling a "portfolio gap…in high-density power delivery for data centers," targeting AI accelerator infrastructure with material revenue impact in 2027. ADI's internal capacity has doubled since pre-COVID and supports up to $20B annual revenue. Supply situation called "calm" with optionality.
- **Implication:** The thesis frame for ADI under this theme was about end-customer constraints throttling demand for ADI's content. Reality is that ADI is now a meaningful, growing supplier into the AI accelerator power tree — not a constrained-supply story but a content-growth story. Worth considering whether ADI should be re-themed away from data_center_deployment_constraints (where it's not the bottleneck) toward a clearer ai_infrastructure_capex / inference_compute_economics framing.

### ai_infrastructure_capex

- **Status:** Confirm (and strengthening)
- **Evidence:** Data center +90% YoY explicitly attributed to AI infrastructure spending. Empower acquisition rationale is entirely about AI accelerator power delivery ("addresses immediate technology needs to accelerate time-to-market…expand TAM, especially in AI accelerator infrastructure"). Management cited "strong demand signals in Industrial AI-related applications."
- **Implication:** ADI is now a more direct AI capex play than it has been historically. The optical + power portfolios into hyperscaler infrastructure, augmented by Empower's IVR and silicon capacitor IP, give ADI a credible seat at the AI power-delivery table beginning 2027. This is a non-obvious second-order AI infrastructure beneficiary that the watchlist tagging already correctly anticipated.

### china_us_tensions

- **Status:** Drift (modest, with caveat)
- **Evidence:** No specific China export-control or tariff disruption called out as a Q2 headwind. BMS for EVs strength explicitly noted in China alongside Europe. Some "tariff-related pull-ins in 2025" referenced as affecting Auto seasonality (i.e., a 2025 effect now in the rearview). Manufacturing capacity expansion includes "geographical diversification" — a quiet hedge.
- **Implication:** China-US tension is not currently a binding constraint for ADI's reported results, and China demand (especially auto BMS) is a tailwind this quarter. The geographic-diversification capacity language is the standard hedge — no operational change signaled. Tension theme remains a tail-risk overlay rather than an active headwind.

## 8. Management Q&A flags

- **Rasgon (Bernstein) — gross margin ceiling** [CHALLENGING EXCHANGE]: Rasgon pressed on whether 73% is a ceiling and on outsourcing implications. CFO Puccio confirmed current guidance level as "a likely ceiling" — notable that management did not push back or leave room for upside. This is the cleanest single data point for modelers: don't underwrite further margin expansion beyond the Q3 49% OM / ~72.5% GM bar, even on positive mix.
- **Stein (Truist) — Empower rationale**: CEO Roche framed Empower as filling a gap that ADI needed to close on an urgent timeline — an honest concession that organic R&D could not have delivered the IVR + silicon capacitor capability fast enough for the AI accelerator window. Worth noting as a real (modest) innovation-rate concession.
- **Caso (Wolfe) — Empower revenue and integration timeline**: Roche/Puccio confirmed minimal 2026 revenue from Empower, with "substantial demand" in 2027. Sets the timing expectation cleanly — anyone modeling Empower contribution to FY26 should pull it out.
- **Arya (BofA) — pricing sustainability**: Management split the answer into short-term inflation pass-through ("a couple of points" to 2026 growth) vs. long-term value capture via ASP. Useful for distinguishing transient vs. structural pricing in the model.
- **O'Malley (Barclays) — auto inventory dynamics**: Puccio explicitly stated "no significant inventory build-up observed" at auto customers — direct contradiction of the soft-landing-then-destock concern in auto. Supports the cycle-confirm read above.
