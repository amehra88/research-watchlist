## 3. Forward guidance

- **Shopee:** FY26 GMV ~+25% YoY (deliberately conservative given Q1 strength). FY26 Shopee adj. EBITDA at least at 2025 absolute level.
- **Garena:** FY26 sustained YoY bookings growth, with "Arena of Valor" expected to have a record 10th-anniversary year.
- **Monee:** No specific FY guide; management flagged margins will fluctuate by country/product mix as the book diversifies geographically.
- **Tone:** Confident on top-line trajectory, deliberately conservative on margin guide. Management explicitly chose not to raise full-year GMV despite stronger-than-expected Q1, citing macro caution (fuel costs, ASEAN/Brazil competitive intensity). This is a guidance-philosophy continuity signal — sandbag-and-beat playbook, not a change.

## 4. Thesis read by theme

### agentic_commerce

- **Status:** Drift (positive)
- **Evidence:** Management described a "Shopee assistant for personalized recommendations and a virtual business advisor for sellers" in early testing (prepared remarks, AI section). AI-powered search/recommendations cited as driving a 14% YoY purchase conversion uplift. Orders driven by YouTube doubled YoY; Meta affiliate network +30% QoQ to 4.5M affiliates.
- **Implication:** SE is moving from passive AI tooling to agentic buyer- and seller-side surfaces, but still in early testing — read this as SE entering the agentic-commerce conversation, not leading it. Affiliate/social-commerce funnel data is the more material near-term proof point that the discovery layer is shifting.

### ai_generated_content

- **Status:** Confirm
- **Evidence:** "AI-generated content tools boosted purchase conversion rates" (prepared remarks). Content-driven orders (live streaming, short video) rose 50%+ YoY, now >25% of total orders in SEA. Free Fire Jujutsu Kaisen collab generated 700M+ official content views; Ramadan campaign delivered 120B+ global impressions (+70% YoY).
- **Implication:** Both the commerce and gaming arms are leaning hard on AI/content flywheels to sustain engagement. The 25% content-driven order share in SEA is a meaningful milestone — a quarter of orders now originate from non-traditional search surfaces.

### platform_take_rate

- **Status:** Drift (positive)
- **Evidence:** Shopee ad take rate +90 bps YoY; ad revenue +80% YoY. ShopeeVIP subscribers >10M (+40% QoQ), driving ~20% of GMV in Asia with 30–40% spending uplift in some markets. Analyst Q&A (Kothiyal, MS) explicitly probed take rates and seller commissions; Feng framed the answer as "balance between take rates and market reinvestment" — i.e., management is consciously holding back on monetization pace to defend share.
- **Implication:** Monetization is rising organically through ads and VIP, not through commission hikes — a healthier, more durable take-rate trajectory than blunt fee increases. The deliberate restraint on commission hikes is a positive signal for long-term platform health but caps near-term take-rate beat potential.

### local_commerce_delivery

- **Status:** Confirm
- **Evidence:** Instant and same-day delivery expanded; cost per order for instant delivery -20% YoY in Indonesia, order volumes +35%. Fulfillment volumes +25% sequentially. Taiwan collection points +50% YoY. Brazil opened three new fulfillment centers (total now 5); delivery time improved by over a day YoY while staying profitable.
- **Implication:** Fulfillment is becoming a structural moat, not an opex line. The Brazil-profitable-while-scaling narrative is the most differentiated piece — peers (MELI, AMZN-LatAm) are spending into similar markets without the same profitability discipline. Worth tracking whether Brazil profitability holds as fulfillment capex compounds through 2026.

## 8. Management Q&A flags

- **Killa (UBS) on Shopee EBITDA decline + fintech margins:** Feng attributed Shopee EBITDA drop to "seasonal factors and new initiatives." This is a soft answer — the real story (per prepared remarks) is investment in delivery, fulfillment, and ShopeeVIP. The "seasonal" framing in Q&A is mildly evasive; the prepared remarks were more direct. Long-term margin target of 2-3% reiterated. Flag: watch whether Q2 EBITDA recovers or whether reinvestment intensifies further.
- **Sharma (JPM) on buyback strategy:** Li gave strategic-rationale framing but no fresh pace/size update. This is a place where more disclosure would help the investor narrative; the answer was directionally supportive but light on specifics.
- **Xiao (Barclays) on fuel prices:** Feng acknowledged but downplayed via "government subsidies and partnerships." Worth tracking — fuel is the single most concrete macro headwind mentioned all call and management is treating it as manageable. If Q2 commentary tightens, that's a Drift signal.
- **Kothiyal (MS) on Brazil take rates and seller commissions:** Feng explicitly framed restraint as a strategic choice. Useful signal that take-rate upside is being deliberately deferred to defend share.
- **Jiang (Macquarie) on ShopeeVIP and Monee:** Detailed answer on VIP economics and Monee user expansion — management leaning in on these as the durable growth narratives.
