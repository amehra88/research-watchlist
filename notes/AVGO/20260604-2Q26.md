## 3. Forward guidance

Aggressive and confidence-laden, consistent with AVGO's recent point-estimate guidance philosophy.

- **Q3'26 (ending Aug 2, 2026):** total revenue ~$29.4B (+84% y/y); semiconductor ~$20.5B (+124% y/y) with AI semi ~$16.0B (+>200% y/y); infrastructure software ~$8.9B (+31% y/y); non-GAAP operating margin ~67%; adjusted EBITDA ~68% of revenue; consolidated gross margin guided to ~74% (down from 77.1%) on AI mix (8-K Ex. 99.1 outlook; transcript prepared remarks).
- **FY26:** AI semiconductor revenue ~$56B (~+180% from FY25). H2'26 AI semi expected to roughly double vs H1'26 (transcript).
- **FY27+:** reiterated AI semi revenue exceeding $100B; CEO Hock Tan confirmed under questioning that FY27 is on track to ~2x FY26 — "2027 will exceed very easily $100 billion." Visibility cited into 2028, with supply planning into 2028–2029 (transcript Q&A — Sur, Rasgon, Moore exchanges).
- **Demand-anchor framing:** multi-year, multi-gigawatt agreements named — Google (multi-gen TPU + networking), Anthropic (>1 GW in 2026, +5 GW from 2027), OpenAI (1.3 GW in 2027 within a 10 GW-by-2029 deal), Meta (3 GW through 2028, initial orders H2'27), plus two unnamed customers contributing $6B of POs for late-2026/2027 (transcript prepared remarks & Q&A).

Guidance philosophy unchanged: single point estimates, ambitious framing, demand described as "insatiable." Sentiment was scored Slightly Positive (a step down from prior-call Positive) — driven by the vendor's recalibrated sentiment model being more skeptical of generic optimism, not by a change in management tone.

## 4. Thesis read by theme

### ai_infrastructure_capex

- **Status:** Confirm
- **Evidence:** AI semi revenue $10.8B (+143% y/y), >$30B AI bookings in Q2, FY26 AI guide $56B (~+180%), reiterated >$100B FY27, multi-GW customer agreements (Google/Anthropic/OpenAI/Meta) (8-K Ex. 99.1; transcript prepared remarks & Reitzes/Moore Q&A).
- **Implication:** AVGO is a direct, accelerating beneficiary of hyperscaler/frontier-lab capex. The bookings-to-shipments gap (>$30B booked vs $10.8B shipped) and stated visibility into 2028 strengthen the durability of the capex tailwind underlying the ai_positioning 5.

### silicon_architecture_competition

- **Status:** Confirm
- **Evidence:** Custom XPU/ASIC franchise (Google TPU multi-generation, Meta, plus Anthropic and OpenAI custom-accelerator commitments) is the core growth driver; Tan reiterated focus on large XPU platforms for LLM developers and described rising content per gigawatt via multi-die XPUs and embedded CPU cores (transcript Q&A — Muse, Buchalter).
- **Implication:** Captive-silicon co-design thesis is playing out at scale. The "integrator/co-design house, not frontier-architecture inventor" framing (innovation_rate 4) holds — content/GW gains come from packaging and integration advances, not a new compute paradigm.

### networking_competitive_landscape

- **Status:** Confirm
- **Evidence:** Networking was ~40% of Q2 AI revenue; management guided this toward a normalized ~30% as XPU shipments outgrow networking, while reaffirming leadership in 100T/200T Ethernet switching and CPO (transcript Q&A — Schneider).
- **Implication:** The 40%→30% step-down is a mix shift (XPU growing faster), not competitive erosion — networking dollars still grow and leadership is intact. Confirms the distribution-5 read; timing of optical/CPO revenue scaling remains the open variable to monitor.

### hyperscaler_revenue_concentration

- **Status:** Confirm (with a named live risk)
- **Evidence:** Revenue increasingly anchored on a small set of hyperscaler/frontier-lab captive-silicon customers (Google, Meta, Anthropic, OpenAI). In the Curtis (Jefferies) exchange on the Google long-term agreement, Tan emphasized the deal's strength but acknowledged Google may diversify sourcing (transcript Q&A — Curtis).
- **Implication:** Concentration is the thesis working (deep multi-year design wins) and the standing structural risk simultaneously. The Google second-sourcing concession is the canonical drift-down trigger to watch; not yet material given the breadth of the customer agreements and the $6B of incremental POs from two additional unnamed customers.

### inference_compute_economics

- **Status:** Confirm
- **Evidence:** Custom inference silicon (OpenAI inference commitment, Anthropic) plus Tan's framing of stable dollars-per-gigawatt with rapidly rising gigawatt requirements driven by inference demand from frontier labs; VMware Cloud Foundation 9.1 positioned to support AI inferencing workloads (transcript Q&A — Reitzes; prepared remarks).
- **Implication:** AVGO is positioned on the inference cost curve via custom accelerators optimized for specific customer workloads, supporting the thesis that captive inference silicon captures economics as inference scales.

### ai_compute_topology

- **Status:** Confirm
- **Evidence:** Combined XPU + AI networking (Tomahawk/Jericho high-radix switching, 100T/200T Ethernet, optical/CPO) gives AVGO a full scale-up/scale-out fabric position; content-per-GW rising via multi-die XPU configurations and embedded CPU cores (transcript Q&A — Schneider, Buchalter).
- **Implication:** AVGO sells across the AI cluster topology (accelerator + fabric), reinforcing the integrated-system value capture that underpins the overall competitive-advantage 5.

## 8. Management Q&A flags

1. **Harlan Sur (JPMorgan) — the one flagged challenging exchange (Q2'26).** Sur probed the discrepancy in AI revenue growth projections and the AI backlog's translation into future revenue, seeking to reconcile prior statements with current FY27 guidance. Tan confirmed the FY26 numbers and projected FY27 to ~2x ("exceed very easily $100 billion"). Unusual emphasis: management leaned hard into the >$100B FY27 number under direct pressure rather than hedging.
2. **Blayne Curtis (Jefferies) — Google second-sourcing concession.** On the Google long-term agreement, Tan acknowledged Google may diversify its sourcing while expressing confidence in the deal's value. Mild sandbag/risk-acknowledgment worth tracking — the clearest concentration-risk tell on the call (see §4).
3. **Ross Seymore (Deutsche Bank) — margin mix.** Spears attributed gross-margin compression to semiconductor (AI hardware) mix; Tan stressed semiconductor margins are structurally stable. Management was careful to frame the ~74% Q3 GM guide as mix, not erosion — a pre-emptive defense against a margin-bear narrative.
4. **Tom O'Malley (Barclays) — Anthropic deal structure / AI financing.** O'Malley pressed on whether AVGO is providing financing/backstop in the Anthropic arrangement. Tan was explicit that AVGO provides compute capacity via its chips, not a financial backstop — drawing a clean line around the chip business model amid investor concern about circular AI financing.
5. **Timothy Arcuri (UBS) — supply assurance.** On wafer/HBM supply amid competition for capacity, Tan asserted AVGO is well-positioned for 2026–2027 with planning into 2028–2029. Confident, but supply assurance into out-years is an assertion to verify against subsequent capacity data.
