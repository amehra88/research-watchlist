## 3. Forward guidance

- **FY26 revenue:** $3.0–3.4B. Most capacity comes online in 2H26, so quarterly ramp will be back-half-weighted.
- **FY26 ARR exit (Dec'26):** $7–9B — reiterated from three months prior, with management explicitly stating confidence has **strengthened** given Q4'25 beat.
- **FY26 group adj. EBITDA margin:** ~40%. EBIT to remain negative due to capacity/R&D investment; medium-term EBIT target 20–30%.
- **FY26 capex:** $16–20B, with ~60% already covered from balance sheet ($3.7B cash), operations, and long-term contract advances. Remainder via corporate debt, asset-backed financing, or potential equity (ATM unused; ClickHouse/AVride stakes flagged as optionality).
- **Contracted power:** raised to 3+ GW by YE26 (from 2.5 GW), already over 2 GW as of Feb. Available capacity 800 MW–1 GW by YE26.
- **Q1'26 pipeline:** flagged as tracking >$4B in deal value with continued growth in deal size and duration.
- **Tone:** Confident, with a deliberate emphasis on ARR as the meaningful metric "during this hyper-growth phase." Management is explicitly de-emphasizing in-quarter revenue as a measure of the business while highlighting forward contract economics. Guidance philosophy is now anchored on ARR + capacity milestones, not quarterly revenue ranges — a structural shift versus how the Street has historically modeled it.

## 4. Thesis read by theme

### ai_infrastructure_capex

- **Status:** Confirm
- **Evidence:** $16–20B 2026 capex commitment with 60% already funded; 2025 group revenue +547% YoY in Q4 alone; capacity sold out through 2026; nine new data centers announced globally; contracted power raised to 3+ GW from 2.5 GW (prepared remarks, Guidance and Outlook + Demand and Capacity Expansion sections).
- **Implication:** NBIS is now a meaningful incremental source of AI infra capex demand — $16–20B is in the AVGO/MU customer-funded co-design range. The capex is contractually backed (Microsoft, Meta tranches secured) rather than speculative, which validates the thesis that AI infra capex has migrated from hyperscaler-only to a neocloud + hyperscaler dual structure. Read-through positive for ANET, AVGO, COHR, LITE on networking/optical demand; positive for MPWR/MU on power and HBM; modestly negative for the hyperscaler-monopoly framing on CoreWeave/Nebius peers (NBIS is taking real share).

### ai_compute_topology

- **Status:** Confirm
- **Evidence:** "Average contract duration for new cloud customers grew by 50% in 2025." "Orders from AI-native customers have grown from hundreds to tens of thousands of GPUs." Token Factory launch + Tavily acquisition (agentic search) — explicit move up the stack toward a full-stack AI cloud (prepared remarks, Strategic Initiatives + Customer Demand). Software attach rates cited at 100% with new products driving "higher per-GPU pricing."
- **Implication:** Topology is shifting from "rent GPUs by the hour" to vertically integrated stacks where the GPU layer is bundled with managed inference (Token Factory) and developer tooling (Tavily, +700K developers). This validates the thesis that AI compute topology is consolidating into platform layers rather than pure IaaS. Per-GPU monetization is rising, which is a Drift-positive within Confirm — the unit economics inside compute are getting better, not getting commoditized.

### inference_compute_economics

- **Status:** Drift (positive)
- **Evidence:** "Pricing remains strong, with no decline even for previous-generation GPUs" (sentiment summary, Customer Demand). Core AI cloud adj. EBITDA margin expanded from 19% (Q3) to 24% (Q4). 2026 group adj. EBITDA margin targeted at 40%. Depreciation schedule extended from 4 to 5 years "reflecting longer useful lives," which by itself lifts margins by ~2–3pp absent volume changes.
- **Implication:** The inference compute economics thesis has historically priced in margin compression as supply grows. NBIS's print is a Drift-positive: prior-gen GPU pricing is holding, longer contract durations are reducing churn risk, and the depreciation extension reflects an industry-wide recalibration that AI hardware has a longer useful life than initially assumed. The 40% group EBITDA margin target for 2026 is aggressive and worth tracking — if delivered, it argues for inference economics being structurally better than the bear thesis assumed. Watch for Q1'26 to test whether margin expansion holds at scale. Caveat: the depreciation change is a non-cash margin tailwind that needs to be backed out for true cash margin comparison.

### data_center_deployment_constraints

- **Status:** Confirm (with one nuance)
- **Evidence:** Nine new data centers announced; 3+ GW contracted power target (raised). "Components and supply chain for these contracts were secured ahead of time, mitigating risk of delays." Management acknowledged headlines about equipment shortages but stated NBIS is well positioned via proactive contracting and site diversification (sentiment summary, Data Center Supply Chain — flagged Neutral).
- **Implication:** NBIS is articulating data center deployment as the binding constraint and is investing aggressively to *be the supply* rather than be constrained by it. The thesis that DC deployment is the bottleneck is confirmed by the volume of capacity NBIS is contracting — but NBIS's read on its own positioning is that proactive contracting has de-risked the 2026 build. The Neutral sentiment flag from the AI summary on supply chain is the only place management showed any hedging in the call. Read-through is positive for BE, GEV, PWR, SEI (data center power/build), modestly bearish for tickers whose thesis relies on DC scarcity persisting.

### hyperscaler_revenue_concentration

- **Status:** Drift (cautionary)
- **Evidence:** Microsoft first tranche delivered Nov 2025, remaining tranches through 2026 with majority in 2H. Meta's two tranches delivered early Feb 2026, fully in service, "nearly a full year of revenue in 2026." Microsoft expected to reach full annual run rate contribution in 2027 (prepared remarks, Major Contracts).
- **Implication:** NBIS's "diversified" customer base has, in fact, materially concentrated around two named hyperscaler customers (Microsoft + Meta) for major revenue contribution. While the prepared remarks emphasize a broader pipeline ($4B Q1'26 pipeline, AI startups growing to enterprise scale, average contract duration +50% YoY), the gating revenue figures are tied to two counterparties. This is a Drift on the *concentration* axis of the thesis — concentration is rising, not falling, as NBIS scales. Hyperscaler revenue concentration as a thesis dimension should now be tracked specifically: % of 2026 revenue from top 2 customers is the metric to extract from the 20-F (filed 4/30/2026, iacc 43254462) and future 6-Ks.

## 6. Competitive advantage signal

- **Innovation rate** (current: unpopulated)
  - Evidence: Token Factory launch + Tavily acquisition within the year. Contract duration up 50%, per-GPU pricing up via software attach. Capex execution ahead of plan (3 GW vs. 2.5 GW prior). 60% of $16–20B capex already secured — financial engineering is itself a competitive vector here.
  - Recommendation: initialize at **4** — strong execution velocity, full-stack expansion, but the underlying GPU layer is the same NVIDIA stack as every competitor; differentiation has to come from software/topology.
- **Distribution** (current: unpopulated)
  - Evidence: Two named hyperscaler anchors (Microsoft, Meta) + diversified AI startup base, but distribution is bottlenecked by physical capacity. "Everything we build, we sell." This is supply-constrained distribution, not demand-constrained.
  - Recommendation: initialize at **3+** — strong in the segments they reach, but no native enterprise sales channel of the AWS/Azure/GCP variety; distribution depends on the platform being technically attractive to AI-native customers and hyperscalers needing supplementary capacity. If the supply constraint eases industry-wide in 2027, NBIS's distribution position is the weak link.
- **Overall** (current: unpopulated)
  - Recommendation: initialize at **4**
  - Reasoning: Innovation rate (4) and distribution (3+) don't average to 4 mechanically — for an AI infrastructure provider in a supply-constrained market, capacity execution and contract economics dominate the equation. NBIS is winning on the dimensions that matter *now* (build velocity, contract duration, anchor customer wins). The competitive advantage assessment will become harder once supply constraints ease, which is why the overall sits at 4 rather than the 4+/5 the operating momentum might suggest. The competitive moat is currently *execution and balance-sheet willingness*, not technology, and execution moats compress as competitors catch up.

## 8. Management Q&A flags

- **Revenue vs. ARR framing (analyst pushback):** Multiple analysts asked about the gap between $7–9B ARR exit and $3.0–3.4B reported revenue guide. Management's response was clean — "ARR is a more meaningful metric in the current hyper-growth phase" — and offered detailed bridges (capacity ramp in 2H, Meta full-year contribution, Microsoft 2027 full-run-rate). Flag: management is actively training the Street to value the company on ARR exit, not in-quarter revenue. Buy-side should follow but verify the ARR-to-revenue conversion in Q1'26 and Q2'26 prints.
- **Supply chain hedging (the only Neutral-flagged topic on the call):** Management addressed equipment shortage headlines and stated they're well-positioned via proactive contracting. Tone was confident but the topic was the *only* area where the AI sentiment summary did not classify as Positive. Worth watching whether Q1'26 disclosure surfaces specific shortages or delays.
- **Capital structure (analyst scrutiny):** Question on funding the $16–20B 2026 capex against $3.7B cash. Management: 60% covered (balance sheet + operations + contract advances); remainder via corporate debt, asset-backed financing, ATM (unused), or monetization of ClickHouse/AVride stakes. The ATM-unused emphasis is a deliberate signal to remove dilution overhang. Watch whether any of these capital tools get used in 2026 — the first one drawn will set the cost-of-capital benchmark for the model.
- **Microsoft contract economics (no dodge but careful framing):** When asked about contract terms, management confirmed delivery schedule and full-year-2027 run rate but did not disclose specific revenue per GW or margin per contract. Standard non-disclosure but worth flagging — the unit economics on the hyperscaler tranches are the single highest-leverage unknown in the model.
- **Q1'26 forward color:** Management volunteered that the Q1'26 pipeline tracks >$4B in deal value with continuing growth in deal size and duration. Unprompted forward color from management on the call before guiding Q1 is a confidence signal.
