## 4. Thesis read by theme

### ai_infrastructure_capex

- **Status:** Confirm (intensifying)
- **Evidence:** 2026 capex raised by $4B at midpoint to $20–25B, with the raise explicitly attributed to 2027 capacity needing to come online "early next year" against already-contracted customer commitments. Contracted power up to 3.5 GW (from 3+ GW Q4), targeting 4+ GW YE26. NVIDIA $2B equity investment + Exemplar Cloud status secures supply-chain priority (prepared remarks, Strategic Partnerships and Acquisitions; Capacity and Infrastructure). $6B capital raised YTD ($4B convertible + $2B NVIDIA equity), bringing cash to $9.3B.
- **Implication:** NBIS is now a top-tier source of AI infrastructure capex — $20–25B in a single year puts them within an order of magnitude of hyperscaler capex tiers and elevates them from "interesting neocloud" to a tracked driver of the AI infra theme. Read-through: incremental positive for ANET/AVGO/COHR/LITE on networking-and-optical pull-through; positive for MPWR on power; positive for VRT on data center build (NBIS now owns 75% of contracted power vs. lease-heavy historically — they are building, not renting). The $4B capex raise with no change in 2026 ARR/revenue is also a signal about pricing-power confidence — they're committing balance sheet to 2027 capacity at unchanged 2026 economics, implying they believe 2027 demand is firmer than the model would otherwise assume.

### ai_compute_topology

- **Status:** Confirm (intensifying)
- **Evidence:** Three platform acquisitions executed in the quarter — Eigen AI (inference optimization), Clarifai (inference + platform), Tavily (agentic search). Aether v3.5 launched as a proprietary AI development platform layer. Token Factory production wins cited at Revolut and 1X Technologies. NVIDIA Exemplar Cloud status — among the first across multiple GPU generations — secures roadmap alignment with NVIDIA's product cycles (prepared remarks, Strategic Partnerships and Acquisitions; Product). Full-stack platform from bare metal to AI services explicitly positioned vs. raw IaaS competitors.
- **Implication:** The topology thesis — that AI compute is consolidating into vertically integrated platform layers, not commoditizing into bare-IaaS — has its strongest validation yet. NBIS has now done three M&A deals in two quarters to climb the stack (Tavily in Q4, Eigen/Clarifai in Q1). The Aether platform + Token Factory + agentic search stack collectively differentiates the per-GPU monetization story. NVIDIA Exemplar Cloud status is the strongest signal of the quarter — it confirms NBIS is in the top tier of NVIDIA's preferred deployment partners, which materially raises the bar for any new entrant trying to win at NBIS's tier. Read-through is consistent with the bull thesis on CRWV and the bear thesis on pure-play bare-metal neoclouds.

### inference_compute_economics

- **Status:** Drift (strongly positive — approaching Break of the bear case)
- **Evidence:** Nebius AI segment adj. EBITDA margin expanded to 45% in Q1'26 from 24% in Q4'25 — an 21pp QoQ expansion. Group margin 32% with Q2'26 explicitly guided lower before Q3/Q4 acceleration. "Strong GPU pricing dynamics" called out: contract durations extending, average contract values increasing, prepayments increasing materially. Pipeline 3.5x QoQ. Multiple customers competing for each GPU brought online (prepared remarks, Market Demand and Pricing Dynamics).
- **Implication:** The bear case on inference compute economics — that supply growth drives per-GPU margin compression — is increasingly hard to sustain against NBIS's Q1 data points. Segment margin of 45% on a business that is still in supply-constrained build mode is well above the bear-case envelope, and the pricing-and-duration commentary suggests this is structural, not transient. The Drift-positive designation from Q4'25 has now moved closer to a "Break of the bear case" call: NBIS is showing that vertically integrated AI cloud at scale can earn hyperscaler-tier margins, not commoditized IaaS margins. Caveat to watch: Q2'26 is explicitly guided lower due to investment timing — if Q2 margin compresses meaningfully and Q3/Q4 don't recover as guided, the thesis read needs to revisit.

### data_center_deployment_constraints

- **Status:** Confirm
- **Evidence:** Pennsylvania 1.2 GW site announced — large, US-domiciled, attractive for hyperscaler co-location economics. Alabama and Missouri sites flagged for H2 2026 / early 2027. Contracted power now 3.5 GW (up from 3+ GW Q4), targeting 4+ GW YE26. "Vast majority of GPUs brought online are oversubscribed, with customers competing for access." Management acknowledges political/community opposition to US DC construction but says they're managing it via efficient transparent building methods and community investment (Nebius Academy). No specific permitting or regulatory issues reported (sentiment summary, Regulatory section — Neutral flag retained).
- **Implication:** Deployment constraint thesis remains binding industry-wide; NBIS continues to invest aggressively to *be the supply*. The shift to 75% owned (vs. leased) is meaningful — it converts the constraint from "find a hyperscale landlord" to "build it yourself," and NBIS has the balance sheet to do that now ($9.3B cash + $6B capital raised YTD). Read-through positive for BE/GEV/PWR (data center power), VRT (thermal/power infrastructure), AAON (cooling), modestly negative for any thesis predicated on a near-term DC supply easing. The political/community opposition flag is the one place management acknowledged a friction; worth tracking whether Pennsylvania or other US sites encounter permitting delays in 2026.

### hyperscaler_revenue_concentration

- **Status:** Drift (cautionary — concentration is rising, not falling)
- **Evidence:** New 5-year Meta contract of $27B announced ($12B dedicated commitment + $15B capacity option). Microsoft contract remains on schedule with major deliveries H2 2026. "Diversified" customer base emphasized in the segment commentary ("not relying on strategic hyperscaler deals") but the $27B Meta number alone — even on the $12B firm portion — dominates the multi-year revenue stack. Pipeline 3.5x QoQ across fintech, physical AI, life sciences, manufacturing flagged as the broader diversification narrative (prepared remarks, Customer Base; Contracts).
- **Implication:** Concentration is now structurally even higher than at Q4'25. The $27B Meta contract is the single largest customer commitment in the company's history; even framed as "$12B firm + $15B option," the optical and modeling weight tilts heavily to Meta + Microsoft as the dominant counterparties through at least 2028. Management's "broadening pipeline" narrative is plausible and partly evidenced by the 3.5x pipeline growth, but the contract economics are anchored on two hyperscaler names. This Drift cautionary persists from Q4'25 and gets one notch more cautionary. Specific metric to track: percentage of 2026 *and* 2027 contracted revenue from top 2 customers — should be extractable from the FY25 20-F (filed 4/30/2026, iacc 43254462) and forthcoming 6-Ks. The $15B option structure on the Meta contract is the most interesting modeling wrinkle: it gives NBIS flexibility to sell that capacity to other customers if market conditions remain strong, which functionally caps the downside of customer concentration *but* depends on NBIS exercising it well.

## 8. Management Q&A flags

The infilings summary surfaced no analyst Q&A transcript verbatim, so flags here are drawn from how management framed key topics in prepared remarks and what the AI summary called out as recurring themes. The Q4'25 call had explicit analyst pushback on ARR vs. revenue; this call's summary does not surface comparable pushback, suggesting the Street has accepted the ARR-led framing.

- **Capex raise framing:** Management framed the $4B capex raise as forward-investment for 2027 capacity with customer commitments "already in place" — i.e., not speculative. Worth verifying when the Q1 6-K / 10-Q-equivalent disclosures land: which specific customers underwrite the 2027 capacity, and what the contract terms look like. The credibility of this framing is the single most important diligence item.
- **Meta $27B contract structure:** $12B firm + $15B option is a deliberate structure that lets NBIS finance the buildout against contractual commitments while preserving flexibility to redirect capacity if Meta doesn't take it. Management cast this as a strength; it should be modeled as a hybrid take-or-pay where the $15B optional layer's economics are scenario-dependent. The unit economics on the firm $12B portion are the highest-leverage unknown — neither $/MW nor margin per contract was disclosed.
- **Q2'26 margin softness disclosure:** Management proactively guided Q2'26 margin "temporarily lower" due to investment timing before Q3/Q4 acceleration. This is forward-color the Street did not previously have. Reading: management is trying to inoculate Q2 expectations. If Q2 prints in line with the soft guide and Q3 ramps, this is a deliberate setup; if Q2 misses *its own* softer guide, the credibility erosion will be material. Track closely.
- **NVIDIA equity investment terms:** Management did not disclose the structure (common, preferred, conversion features) of the $2B NVIDIA investment in the summary. Standard non-disclosure pending the 6-K but a high-leverage unknown — terms determine whether this is dilutive equity or quasi-strategic preferred.
- **Pennsylvania community/regulatory opposition:** The Neutral-flagged regulatory section noted political/community opposition to US data center construction. Management deflected with "efficient transparent building methods" and "community engagement" language. This is the only area in the call where the AI sentiment summary did not classify as Positive — same pattern as Q4'25's supply-chain item. Worth watching whether site delays or permitting friction surface in Q2/Q3.

## 9. Vocabulary candidates

- **NVIDIA Exemplar Cloud status** — formal designation from NVIDIA for top-tier deployment partners across multiple GPU generations. Material competitive moat indicator. Worth tracking which other cloud/neocloud names hold this status (publicly: CRWV has been mentioned in similar context). Candidate sub-vocabulary item under `ai_compute_topology` or `silicon_architecture_competition`.

If multiple AI infra names start using this structure (option to allocate to other customers), it becomes a thesis-relevant pricing-dynamics item under `inference_compute_economics`.
- **Customer prepayments as financing** — material working-capital and capex-financing mechanism for NBIS; $2.3B operating cash flow Q1 was largely customer prepayments. If this pattern is common across neoclouds, it's a structural financing differentiator worth tracking as a meta-vocabulary item.
- **Owned vs. leased data center capacity** — NBIS shifted to 75% owned in Q1, from heavily leased historically. The own/lease ratio is now a competitive-positioning metric worth tracking. Owners get the capex burden but capture the operating margin; lessees stay capital-light but pay landlord margin.
- **Aether platform / full-stack AI cloud** — NBIS's proprietary platform layer. Token Factory + Aether + Eigen + Clarifai + Tavily collectively comprise the "full-stack" claim. Worth tracking as a NBIS-specific watch item, not a sector vocabulary candidate (each cloud has its own stack name).
- **NVIDIA equity-investment in cloud partners** — NVIDIA has now made significant equity investments in cloud partners (CRWV historically, NBIS now). This pattern is itself a thesis-relevant signal under NVDA's strategy (vertical investment in deployment partners). Candidate sub-vocabulary under `ai_infrastructure_capex` or new theme on NVDA's strategic-investment portfolio.
