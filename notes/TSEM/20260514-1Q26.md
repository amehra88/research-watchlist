# TSEM — 1Q26 earnings read

_Prepared by earnings-reviewer, 2026-05-14. Tier: T3._

## 1. Headline read

The Q1'26 print is **thesis-confirming and incrementally strengthening** vs. the 4Q25 / FY25 read. Revenue ($414M) landed in-line with the prior-quarter guide ($412M midpoint) and ~in-line with thin post-print consensus. The bigger signal is the **$1.3B contracted SiPho revenue for 2027 with $290M of prepayments already received**, plus Q2'26 guided to a record $455M (+22% YoY, +10% QoQ) — both are upward data points relative to the FY25-call construct, where the SiPho story was framed in capacity terms rather than booked 2027 revenue terms. Margin trajectory continues to improve: gross margin 27% (vs 20% in Q1'25), net margin 16% (vs 11% Q1'25). The 4Q25-call "guidance-by-backlog" framing now has its first hard $-denominated forward booking number behind it — that's a meaningful credibility upgrade on the 2028 model.

## 3. Forward guidance

- **Q2'26 revenue:** $455M ± 5%. Framed as the **"highest revenue in history."** +22% YoY vs Q2'25, +10% sequentially from Q1'26. Materially above the typical Q2 seasonality framing — implies the SiPho mix is now driving sequential growth in addition to YoY growth.
- **2027 revenue visibility:** $1.3B in **contracted** SiPho wafer shipments for 2027, with $290M of customer prepayments already in hand. Management explicitly stated this contracted number understates total 2027 SiPho demand — additional shipments to "over 50 SiPho customers" not included. This is the first time TSEM has put a specific dollar number on contracted SiPho 2027 revenue (vs. the prior call's "capacity reserved" framing).
- **2028 model status:** Prior call's 2028 model ($2.84B revenue / $750M net profit) was reiterated implicitly; Ellwanger told Acree (Benchmark) that "possible updates in the coming quarters" — a soft signal that the 2028 model may be revised upward as 2027 visibility firms.
- **SiPho capacity:** 5x Q4'25 base by end of 2026. Additional 300mm expansion in Uozu (Japan) targeted for 2027. METI grant approval pending for further buildout; interim plan uses existing Orai factory space to avoid capacity shortfalls before the new shell is ready (H1 2028).
- **CapEx:** $920M plan unchanged, now 40% deployed (up from 28% at FY25 call). On track.
- **Tax:** Q1'26 effective tax rate was 9% (benefited from a non-recurring item). Normalized rate going forward is 15-18% (Pillar 2). This is incremental color, not a change to prior framing.
- **Guidance philosophy:** Continued confidence with operationally specific framing. The shift from "reserved capacity" to "$1.3B contracted revenue + $290M prepayments" tightens the credibility of the 2028 model.
- **Tone:** Strongly positive; transcript notes "Slightly Positive" overall sentiment per InsiderScore but the sub-topic sentiment on Revenue/Profitability and Silicon Photonics is explicitly "Positive." S&P upgraded outlook to positive.

## 4. Thesis read by theme

### foundry_capacity

- **Status:** Confirm (strengthened)
- **Evidence:** SiPho capacity now 5x Q4'25 by end of 2026, with $1.3B in 2027 contracted revenue and $290M prepayments. Fab 7 (Japan, 300mm) "fully utilized and profitable"; transition to full ownership via Japan restructuring deal closed. Plans to scale Fab 7 up to 4x current levels; METI grant approval pending for the new shell (H1 2028). Long-term supply agreement with Nuvoton for Fab 5 Tonami protects 200mm manufacturing continuity. (Prepared remarks; Q&A with Richard Shannon, Craig-Hallum.)
- **Implication:** Foundry capacity thesis is being executed with increasing customer-funded de-risking. The Fab 7 full-ownership transition (Japan) is a positive structural change — TSEM controls its primary 300mm SiPho capacity outright. The capacity story is now backed by hard contracted 2027 revenue, which moves the thesis from "build it and they will come" to "they have already pre-paid for it." The Intel Fab 11X overhang remains in mediation but appears compartmentalized.

### semiconductor_cycle

- **Status:** Confirm (with the same caveat as last quarter — the "cycle" lens is becoming less informative for TSEM)
- **Evidence:** Q1'26 revenue +15% YoY ($414M vs $358M Q1'25). Q2'26 guide +22% YoY. Platform-level YoY growth: Imagers +9%, RF-SOI +12%, Power Management +10%, SiGe +24%, SiPho tripled. RF Mobile down ~36% sequentially (Q4'25 spike + 200mm→300mm transition); full-year 2026 expected to be down YoY for RF-SOI on the mobile side, but 300mm design wins setting up "record growth" in 2027-2028.
- **Implication:** Underlying upcycle continues to provide a tailwind, but the company-specific SiPho/SiGe content gain is now the dominant growth driver. RF Mobile weakness is a transitional drag that management explicitly framed as "short-term." The cycle lens is decreasingly useful for TSEM — the operative themes are increasingly AI infrastructure-adjacent. Recommendation to operator (echoing last quarter): consider adding `ai_infrastructure_capex` and/or `ai_compute_topology` to TSEM's theme set.

### china_us_tensions

- **Status:** Drift (still mostly absent from the call)
- **Evidence:** No direct discussion of China export controls, China revenue exposure, or US tariff exposure in the available transcript summary. The Fab 7 Japan restructuring (full ownership) is the most consequential geopolitical signal — it concentrates 300mm SiPho capacity in Japan and reinforces the Israel/Japan/Italy/Texas footprint without US-expansion catalysts. METI grant approval (Japanese government industrial policy) is now part of the capacity expansion plan — a positive jurisdictional alignment.
- **Implication:** As last quarter — china_us_tensions isn't the right primary lens. The deeper signal is positive: Japanese government engagement (METI) on capacity buildout suggests TSEM is positioned favorably in the Japan-US-Israel friendly-shoring axis. No incremental China exposure surfaced. Theme is essentially dormant for TSEM; consider replacing with `friendly_shoring_capacity` or `japan_capacity_concentration` framing in future watchlist refresh.

## 5. AI positioning signal

- **Current score:** unpopulated in watchlist (prior note recommended initiate at **4**)
- **Evidence from this call:** SiPho revenue tripled YoY in Q1'26. $1.3B contracted 2027 revenue with $290M prepayments — hard dollar evidence of customer commitment, not just capacity reservation. 400G-per-lane Mach-Zehnder modulator co-developed with Coherent reached high-volume manufacturing readiness (a real technology milestone, not a roadmap promise). Partnerships with OpenLight, Lightwave Logic, NLM Photonics, Salience Labs, Oriole Networks expand the ecosystem footprint. Active CPO development with hybrid bonding and indium phosphide laser integration. Management explicitly framed the SiPho position as defensible against GlobalFoundries and TSMC entries.
- **Recommendation:** **drift to 4+** from prior recommendation of 4
- **Reasoning:** The $1.3B contracted 2027 revenue with prepayments is qualitatively different from "we have customer reservations" — it is real, dollar-denominated, contractually-secured AI infrastructure exposure. The 400G-per-lane silicon modulator at high-volume manufacturing readiness with Coherent is a tangible technology lead. The breadth of optical-ecosystem partnerships (multiple named partners) suggests TSEM is becoming a central node in the SiPho supply chain rather than one vendor among many. Stops short of 5 because (a) TSEM is still one step removed from the end customer (NVDA, AVGO, GOOGL, etc. — Tower sells to module makers who supply hyperscalers), and (b) GFS and TSMC are publicly stated as active SiPho competitors — moat is real but not impregnable. PM to confirm before this is added to watchlist.

## 6. Competitive advantage signal

- **Innovation rate** (current: unpopulated; prior note recommended **4**)
  - Evidence: 400G-per-lane silicon modulator with Coherent at HVM readiness — a discrete technology milestone, not a roadmap claim. Fully qualified hybrid bonding for image sensors (a new platform feature). Gen3 power platform delivers 15% reduction in conversion losses. Active work on CPO with hybrid bonding and indium phosphide laser integration.
  - Recommendation: **hold at 4**. Innovation cadence is clearly strong but in the specialty-foundry lane; no reason to revise up from prior recommendation.
- **Distribution** (current: unpopulated; prior note recommended **3+**)
  - Evidence: $290M in prepayments now received (vs. "prepayments expected" framing last quarter). Customer base described as "over 50 SiPho customers" — narrower than mainstream foundries but broader than the implied last-quarter framing of a few large customers. Module-maker channel continues to be the connection to Nvidia and other AI infrastructure end customers.
  - Recommendation: **drift to 4** (from prior recommendation of 3+). The hard-cash prepayments and the disclosure of >50 SiPho customers tilts this up from the prior "narrow but deep" framing. Customer concentration risk remains real but is now diversified across a meaningfully larger set than implied last quarter.
- **Overall** (current: unpopulated; prior note recommended **4-**)
  - Recommendation: **drift to 4** (from prior recommendation of 4-)
  - Reasoning: Not a mechanical average. The qualitative shift this quarter is that distribution moved from "concentrated customer commitments" to "diversified-but-still-concentrated commitments with cash on the balance sheet." That cash-in-hand element materially de-risks the capacity plan. Innovation rate confirmed at the specialty-foundry-leader level (400G HVM milestone). Held below 4+ because GFS/TSMC competitive pressure is publicly acknowledged by management, and the relationship to end demand (hyperscalers) is still mediated by module makers. Notable that the prior-call concern about the Intel Fab 11X collapse appears compartmentalized — capacity is being executed in Japan instead, with government (METI) engagement.

## 8. Management Q&A flags

1. **$1.3B 2027 contracted revenue — Acree (Benchmark) explicitly probed whether this is "the full volume demand."** Ellwanger confirmed it is contractual but *understates* total demand — implying additional uncommitted shipments to 50+ SiPho customers on top. This is the most important quantitative nuance of the call: the headline $1.3B is a floor, not the expected outcome. Flag for upside framing.
2. **2028 model update timing — Acree pressed on when long-term models would be refreshed.** Ellwanger: "possible updates in the coming quarters." Soft, but a clear signal that an upward revision to the 2028 model is on the roadmap once visibility firms. PM should treat the existing $2.84B / $750M as a floor case from this point forward.
3. **Indium phosphide supply constraints — Thompson (Zacks).** Real constraint acknowledged, but Ellwanger expressed confidence in supply chain management. This is a watch item — InP laser supply is a discrete bottleneck for the integrated-laser SiPho story. Worth tracking via specialty publications (II-VI/Coherent, Lumentum, Sumitomo Electric).
4. **Market share vs GFS and TSMC — Sankar (Cowen).** Ellwanger asserted leadership without disclosing specific market share. This is a soft answer; management does not have or will not disclose a hard share number. Worth flagging — implies share is either not measurable cleanly (small enough niche that share data is unreliable) or is in the 30-50% range where disclosing it would invite scrutiny.
5. **Gross margin expansion with lower revenue — Thompson (Zacks) flagged the unusual combination.** Shirazi (CFO) attributed it to "selling higher-margin products." This is the cleanest evidence of the mix-shift thesis playing out in P&L — and a counter-cyclical margin signal worth watching as RF Mobile reaccelerates in 2027-2028 (lower-margin mix would normally compress gross).
