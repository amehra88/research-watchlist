## 3. Forward guidance

- **FY26 CapEx guide $175–185B** introduced (Pichai prepared, p.3; Ashkenazi, p.12). This is a step-function up from $91.4B FY25 actual — roughly a doubling. "Investments ramping over the course of the year." Capex framed as supporting frontier model development by Google DeepMind, Google Services user-experience/ad-ROI, "significant cloud customer demand," and Other Bets.
- **2026 CapEx allocation:** ~60% machines (servers), ~40% data centers + network + equipment, "fairly similar to 2025" mix (Ashkenazi, p.18). Just over half of 2026 ML compute allocated to Cloud business (p.18).
- **FY26 depreciation accelerates:** "We expect the growth rate in 2026 depreciation to accelerate in Q1 and meaningfully increase for the full year" (Ashkenazi, p.13). 2025 depreciation was $21.1B, up $5.8B / +38% from $15.3B in 2024.
- **Q1 2026 FX tailwind expected** at current spot rates, though "volatility in exchange rates could affect the impact of FX" (Ashkenazi, p.12). [Forward note: the 1Q26 actual print disclosed ~3pp FX tailwind, in line with this.]
- **Cloud supply environment:** "Tight supply environment we're operating in" (Ashkenazi, p.12). "Supply constrained even as we've been ramping up our capacity" (Pichai Q&A, p.15). Expected to "go through the year in a supply constrained way."
- **Continued hiring in AI and Cloud** (Ashkenazi, p.13).
- **Apple partnership announced:** GOOGL is Apple's "preferred cloud provider" and will develop "the next generation of Apple Foundation Models based on Gemini technology" (Pichai, p.5; Schindler, p.9). Material strategic and competitive moment.
- **Tone:** Pichai — "tremendous quarter," "really well positioned going into 2026"; Ashkenazi — "outstanding results" in Cloud. No sandbagging signals.
- **Next earnings:** Q1 2026 print confirmed 2026-04-29 (InsiderScore `future_earnings_dates`). [Forward note: 1Q26 print delivered $109.9B revenue +22%, raising FY26 capex to $180–190B.]

## 4. Thesis read by theme

### hyperscaler_capex_buildout

- **Status:** Confirm — and stepping up
- **Evidence:** $175–185B FY26 capex guide vs. $91.4B FY25 (~2x step-function up, Pichai p.3; Ashkenazi p.12). Q4 capex alone $27.9B. "Our 2026 CapEx investments are anticipated to be in the range of $175B to $185B" (Pichai, p.3). 60% machines / 40% long-duration. "Just over half of our ML compute is expected to go towards the Cloud business" (Ashkenazi, p.18) — meaning ~half stays in Google Services/DeepMind for internal AI workloads. Pichai called out compute capacity (power, land, supply chain) as what "keeps us up at night" (p.17).
- **Implication:** GOOGL is fully committed to the "spend-whatever-it-takes" cohort with META. The 2x capex step-up — telegraphed at year-end before 1Q — is the dominant macro signal of this print. Read-through strongly positive to NVDA, AVGO, TSM, ANET, COHR, LITE, VRT, MPWR, GEV, PWR, BE, and the broader AI-infrastructure-capex thesis. The "half of ML compute to Cloud" disclosure is the cleanest signal yet that internal Google Services demand for compute is now comparable to external Cloud customer demand — both halves are growing.

### ad_market_strength

- **Status:** Confirm (with the same Network-bucket nuance to track)
- **Evidence:** Search & other ads +17% y/y to $63.1B — Schindler called it "broad strength across all major verticals with retail particularly strong" (p.7). Sundar described an "expansionary moment" for Search and Ads (p.7). YouTube ads +9% to $11.4B, "driven by direct response," partially offset by "lapping the strong spend on US election in Q4 2024" (Ashkenazi, p.11). Network ads −2% to $7.8B — the open-web bucket continues to slowly fade. AI Max + PMax generated "nearly 70mm creative assets via text customization" in Q4 (Schindler, p.8); Aritzia case study +80% incremental uplift in conversion value; L'Oréal AI Max across 800 campaigns in 23 countries / 30 brands; NYX +23% revenue.
- **Implication:** Owned-and-operated surfaces (Search, YouTube) carry the print; programmatic Network slowly fades. The 9% YouTube growth (vs. Search's 17%) is the first hint of the YouTube-vs-Search divergence the MoffettNathanson conference (2026-05-14) would surface explicitly. Bullish for ad-tooling-driven ROI (read-through to META on direct-response performance ads); bullish for SMB+SMB-uplift narrative (Schindler, p.20: AI Max "already used by hundreds of thousands of advertisers"); read-through caution to open programmatic names (MGNI, PUBM). Retail vertical strength is read-through positive for SHOP, MELI, WMT digital ad businesses.

### search_disruption

- **Status:** Drift (favorable for GOOGL — Search reaccelerated to +17% with AI Mode + AI Overviews as drivers, not headwinds)
- **Evidence:** "Search saw more usage in Q4 than ever before as AI continues to drive an expansionary moment" (Pichai, p.4). 250+ product launches within AI Mode and AI Overviews "just last quarter" (Pichai, p.4). Gemini 3 integrated directly into AI Mode in Search. "Daily AI Mode queries per user double[d] since launch" in US (Pichai, p.4). AI Mode queries are "three times longer than traditional searches"; "nearly one in six AI Mode queries are now non-text using voice or images" (Pichai, p.4). Direct offers Google Ads pilot for AI Mode launched (Schindler, p.8). Pichai on AI-Mode cannibalization (Q&A p.20): "we haven't seen any evidence of cannibalization there."
- **Implication:** The bear case ("agentic search guts Google") is being actively dismantled quarter by quarter. AI Overviews + AI Mode are now Search growth drivers, not cannibalizers. Search reaccelerated by ~4–5pp y/y on a base this large is the operational proof point. Bearish for PPLX, ChatGPT-as-search, and thin-content publishers; vindicates the watchlist note that "search defense holding better than feared." For RDDT/PINS, the read-through is cautionary — AI Mode is capturing the long-tail commercial queries Reddit historically benefited from.

### ai_agent_monetization

- **Status:** Confirm — early but multiple rails firing
- **Evidence:** Gemini Enterprise: 8mm paid seats across 2,800 companies (BNY, Virgin Voyages, Wendy's, Kroger, Woolworths Group, USDOT); managed 5B customer interactions in Q4, +65% y/y (Pichai, p.6). "120,000 enterprises use Gemini, including AI unicorns like Lovable and OpenEvidence, and global enterprises like Airbus and Honeywell. 95% of the top 20 and over 80% of the top 100 SaaS companies use Gemini, including Salesforce and Shopify" (Pichai, p.5). "Revenue from products built on our generative AI models grew nearly 400% y-over-y" in Cloud (Pichai, p.5). First-party Gemini models process >10B tokens/min via direct API, up from 7B last quarter (Pichai, p.4). "Revenue from AI solutions built by our partners increased nearly 300% y-over-y, and commitments from our top 15 software partners grew more than 16x y-over-y" (Pichai, p.6). 325mm paid consumer subscriptions across Google One + YouTube Premium (Pichai, p.3).
- **Implication:** Multi-rail monetization is happening on schedule: (1) Cloud GenAI consumption (~400% y/y), (2) Gemini Enterprise seats (8mm), (3) consumer subscriptions (325mm), (4) ad-tooling uplift (70mm assets in Q4). Sets a clean baseline ahead of 1Q26 which would extend GenAI growth to ~800% y/y and seats to ~12mm. Read-through positive to MSFT, NOW, IBM, PLTR on enterprise agentic monetization patterns. On the consumer side, ad monetization in Gemini App still deferred (Schindler, p.21: "we're not rushing anything here") — same optionality posture as 1Q26.

## 8. Management Q&A flags

1. **TPU-outside-Cloud non-answer (Doug Anmuth Q2, p.15–16).** Anmuth asked directly about "the potential for TPUs to move outside of Google Cloud and into external data centers and develop as an incremental revenue stream." Pichai's answer never committed — he reframed TPU as a Cloud-attached choice for customers. **In hindsight (from the 1Q26 note already in our record), this question was prescient and Pichai was deliberately not pre-announcing.** The 1Q26 print formally launched TPU hardware sales to third parties. PM should treat conference dodges on this theme as setup for forward announcements.
2. **YouTube ad growth softness — first acknowledgment (Mark Mahaney Q, p.16–17).** Mahaney directly asked why YouTube ads only grew 9% given retail strength in Search. Schindler attributed it to lapping US election spend "and other brand-related verticals" and pivoted to the "YouTube ads + subs holistically" framing — the same framing that would surface again at the MoffettNathanson conference in May. **This is the first quarter the YouTube ad-deceleration narrative gets explicit air time.** Watch Q1 and Q2 for whether re-acceleration materializes.
3. **"What keeps you up at night" answer reveals compute as the binding constraint (Mark Shmulik Q2, p.17).** Pichai: "the top question is definitely around compute capacity. All the constraints, be it power, land, supply chain constraints, how do you ramp up to meet this extraordinary demand." Clean acknowledgment that the binding constraint is physical infrastructure, not capability or demand. Bullish read for power, DC, networking equipment suppliers (VRT, GEV, PWR, BE, MPWR, ANET, COHR, LITE).
4. **Cannibalization deflection on Gemini App vs. Search (Justin Post Q, p.20).** Pichai: "we haven't seen any evidence of cannibalization there." Combined with Schindler "not rushing anything here" on Gemini App monetization, the messaging is unified: free-tier Gemini App is being run as a top-of-funnel expansion play, deliberately not monetized yet. Optionality preservation. Bullish-deferred for ad-revenue rail in 2026/27.
5. **SaaS pricing power concern dodge (Mark Mahaney Q2, p.17).** Mahaney asked specifically whether Gemini adoption by SaaS customers undermines those customers' pricing power (the "DeepSeek moment" for SaaS framing). Pichai answered with "successful companies" framing — sidestepping the implied concern that some SaaS incumbents (including Salesforce, Shopify whom GOOGL named as customers) face seat-pricing compression. The dodge confirms a real overhang exists. Read-through caution to MSFT, NOW, TEAM, IBM legacy seat-priced businesses (the `software_seat_pricing_pressure` theme in master vocabulary).
