# GOOGL — Morgan Stanley Technology, Media & Telecom Conference read

_Prepared by earnings-reviewer-from-pdf (offline PDF processing), 2026-05-21. Tier: T1 (BCTK). Source: uploaded conference transcript (FactSet CallStreet PDF, 03-Mar-2026, 17 pages). Speaker: Anat Ashkenazi, CFO & SVP, Alphabet. Format: ~30-minute fireside chat at Morgan Stanley TMT Conference. Position in our record: sits between the 4Q25 print (2026-02-04) and the 1Q26 print (2026-04-29) — i.e., this is the first major external CFO appearance after the $175–185B FY26 capex guide was introduced._

## 1. Headline read

Ashkenazi delivered a disciplined, on-message CFO performance one month into the FY26 capex regime: confirms and defends the $175–185B FY26 capex (now stated as guided range with a midpoint reference of "$180 [billion]"), reinforces the AI-first / full-stack-driven thesis, and gives a clean ROIC framework. Conference **reinforces every standing GOOGL thesis** without surfacing new strategic disclosures. The most notable substantive disclosures are: (1) Nano Banana drove 20mm new Gemini subscribers in two weeks, (2) Cloud GCP growth rate was actually higher than the 48% headline Cloud rate, (3) ~half of FY26 ML compute allocated to Cloud (already in the 4Q25 note but re-stated here), (4) "ROIC, I think a year and a half ago, two years ago was a promise. Now... you see it in the numbers" — first time Ashkenazi has framed ROIC as already-realized rather than forward. Single-conference signal, weaker than an earnings print — but all weight is on the confirming side; no negative tape.

## 2. Strategic themes management emphasized

1. **Strategy unchanged; pace of execution accelerating.** "If you think about where we were a year ago versus where we are today or maybe where we were 10 years ago, the core strategy really hasn't changed... user first, consumer first, innovation based, and then, looking at being an AI-first company" (p.3). The framing: a stable long-arc AI-first strategy is now visibly compounding. Market-cap context she opens with: ~$2T a year ago, ~$4T today.

2. **Full-stack as the strategic moat.** "Within that strategy, we have and we have been talking about having the full-stack approach... the chips that we have, and we have our own TPUs... we're on generation 7. Access to GPUs from NVIDIA, as well as a set of data centers... Networking equipment that is, I believe, the largest, most comprehensive one in the world" + DeepMind/research + distribution (Search, YouTube, Cloud, Workspace) (p.3). This is the same full-stack architecture framing Pichai uses on earnings calls — Ashkenazi delivers it nearly verbatim, which is a tell that this is institutionalized GOOGL messaging.

3. **CapEx discipline framework articulated.** "Highly rigorous framework... start with aggregation of understanding the demand across the business, whether it's internal demand or external from customers like the cloud customers... we then ask what is that going to yield?" + financial metrics + operational metrics + frequent tracking (p.4–5). Explicit acknowledgment of 60/40 chips/data-center split (consistent with 4Q25 disclosure). Quantitative anchor she repeats from 4Q25: "$91 billion" FY25 actual; "$175 [billion] to $185 [billion]" FY26 guide.

4. **Cost discipline as continuous philosophy.** "It's like an Olympic athlete. You never stop training... You always want to beat your own record" (p.6). Three operational levers: (a) infrastructure efficiency / asset utilization / supply-chain matching (chips-to-DC), (b) organization — reduction in management layer, voluntary exit program in 2025, (c) internal AI deployment — treasury agent example, code-generated-by-AI percentage. Direct acknowledgment of depreciation P&L burden: "$21 billion in depreciation. The year before, it was about $15 billion. That's not a small number to offset" — explicitly framing the depreciation headwind margin defense has to overcome.

5. **Cloud growth driven by AI, with breadth across customer types.** "It's AI that's driving that growth" (p.12). GCP growth rate "actually higher than 48%" (p.11) — a small but meaningful disclosure beyond the headline. Customer mix: "across small AI labs, small companies to some of the largest enterprises... having that diversity within our customer base is encouraging" (p.12). Existing customers expanding (positive signal) AND new customers winning ("this win rate, new customer win rate going up").

6. **TPUs remain Cloud-tethered; merchant silicon question dodged again.** Direct question on "the media last week about potentially setting up essentially TPU neo clouds to sell those TPUs to third parties... can you talk to us about... whether it's tethered to GCP workloads or on a standalone basis?" (p.12). Ashkenazi answer: "we offer them both GPUs and TPUs, so they have the choice based on their specific needs or preferences... the customers lead from that perspective" (p.12–13). **Did not commit to merchant silicon distribution outside Cloud.** This is the third such dodge in our trajectory record (Anmuth in 4Q25, Sheridan in 3Q25, now here) before the 1Q26 print formalizes TPU sales to third parties. The pattern is now unmistakable: Pichai / Ashkenazi do not pre-announce strategic shifts at conferences.

## 3. Forward-looking commentary

- **FY26 capex range "$175 [billion] to $185 [billion]"** reaffirmed, with explicit acknowledgment that the year-over-year construction-and-chip timing could move the number up OR down within the guide (p.5–6). [Forward note: the 1Q26 print already in our record raised this to $180–190B.]
- **Active in-year management:** "We are actively managing it for this year as well and probably provide some updates on the quarterly calls on where we are" (p.6). Sets expectation that quarterly capex updates will continue — same posture as in 4Q25.
- **Cloud supply environment continues constrained:** "We always have now said multiple quarters in a row that we exited the quarter with more demand for our cloud services than we had supply" (p.5). Direct echo of the Pichai 4Q25 framing.
- **Depreciation growth will accelerate further in 2026:** "that number is only going to grow next year just given what we've done historically" (p.7). Same forward signal as 4Q25 ("growth rate in 2026 depreciation to accelerate in Q1 and meaningfully increase for the full year") — Ashkenazi reaffirms with conviction.
- **Agentic commerce as 2026 consumer surface:** "Soon, people can use a new checkout experience to buy directly in AI Mode and Gemini from select merchants... universal protocol [UCP] that allows them to connect in a consistent way with this agent. And we're now – we have a few customers on this" (p.9). Explicit framing of agentic commerce as "still in early days" but operational with select retail customers.
- **Waymo: ~5 cities last year + 5 more this year + "more to come, including potential for international cities"** (p.14). "Demand is so high for these Waymo Drivers that we're trying to launch as many markets as possible" (p.14). Ashkenazi: "we have shifted more to Waymo to be able to scale" within Other Bets investment allocation (p.15). [Anchor: 4Q25 note disclosed Miami launch + 400k weekly rides; 1Q26 note disclosed 500k weekly rides + 11 cities.]
- **Quantum, robotics, Isomorphic flagged as undertapped optionality:** "We haven't talked about quantum, which... will have potential implications in a diverse set of areas when we hope to have good use cases within five years or so" (p.16). Isomorphic for drug discovery; Intrinsic for robotics.
- **Next earnings:** Q2 2026 print confirmed for **2026-07-23** (InsiderScore `future_earnings_dates`, queried 2026-05-21).
- **Tone:** Disciplined, on-message, no sandbagging, no promotional excess. The closing "ROIC... a year and a half ago, two years ago was a promise. Now... you see it in the numbers" (p.15) is the cleanest single-sentence summary of the operative bull-case framing for GOOGL post-4Q25.

- **Implication:** No new information beyond 4Q25 dollar guide, but the CFO's defense conviction is unambiguous. The signal that GOOGL would invest MORE in-year if they could ("we are seeing greater demand than what we had anticipated beginning of the year, and we're trying to invest more") is read-through positive to NVDA, AVGO, TSM, VRT, GEV, PWR, BE, MPWR. - **Status:** Refines (favorable; Nano Banana data point and longer AI-Mode queries reinforce expansionary thesis)
- **Evidence:** Nano Banana within Gemini App "went viral, viral... in two weeks we had 20 million new Gemini subscribers" (p.8) — a discrete, quantitative customer-acquisition data point not previously surfaced. "We were incredibly pleased with AI Overview and AI Mode... people learn they can ask questions differently or ask questions they've never been able to ask before, never thought they'd be able to ask before. And what we're seeing is we're seeing longer queries now in AI Mode and AI Overview... It's driving growth in number of queries as well as commercial queries" (p.8). Google Lens called out as a high-growth feature, "younger users are using that more" (p.8–9). "We have invested in innovating what was core to Google's growth for several decades now... you have to kind of out-innovate yourself, disrupt your own business model" (p.9).
- **Implication:** Reinforces the standing Drift-favorable status. Nano Banana viral moment is a new operational proof point that consumer-AI subscription acquisition can happen in two-week bursts driven by feature launches. "Driving growth in... commercial queries" is the key phrase for `ad_market_strength` read-through — confirms what Schindler had in 4Q25 about commercial query growth via AI Mode. Younger-user adoption of Lens is the cleanest forward-durability signal. Bearish PPLX, ChatGPT-as-search, thin-content publishers; cautionary RDDT/PINS continues.

### ai_agent_monetization

- **Status:** Reinforces — agentic commerce now operational with "a few customers" via UCP
- **Evidence:** UCP described as "this universal protocol that allows them to connect in a consistent way with this agent. And we're now – we have a few customers on this" (p.9). Demo description: "you type in, I want a silk pillow or whatever it is... there are a couple of them where you can immediately click on the – there's a buy button and if you put your credit card, et cetera, you can immediately purchase through that" (p.9–10). Ashkenazi confirms personalized-agent shopping vision when prompted: "personalization, linking it to what you're looking for and it may be a preference or it may be criteria that you've provided ahead of time so that you have that personal shopper shop for you" (p.10). Treasury agent example from Ashkenazi's own finance org "goes across our balances of the cash we have on hand to maximize the return on that investment and is yielding actual real – real financial results" (p.7) — a discrete internal-deployment data point we haven't seen in our earnings notes.
- **Implication:** UCP has moved from "launched protocol at NRF" (4Q25) to "have a few customers on this" (here, 2026-03-03). Operational progression looks on-track for the agentic-commerce thesis. The Ashkenazi treasury-agent disclosure is the cleanest internal-Gemini-deployment data point in our record — it joins the 3Q25 sales-team productivity disclosure as evidence that GOOGL's internal AI deployment is producing measurable P&L. Read-through positive to NOW, PLTR, MSFT on the internal-productivity playbook pattern; positive to SHOP, MELI, DASH on UCP merchant-side adoption.
