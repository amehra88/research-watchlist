First-ever $100B quarter: consolidated revenue $102.3B (+16% y/y reported, +15% cc), Search & other ads +15% to $56.6B, YouTube ads +15% to $10.3B (accelerating from prior quarters), Cloud +34% to $15.2B with op margin 17.1% → 23.7%, Cloud backlog +46% q/q and +82% y/y to $155B, FY25 capex raised to $91–93B from prior $85B. Print **confirms the AI-capable-incumbent thesis on every primary dimension** — Search reaccelerating with AI Mode + AI Overviews as growth drivers (not cannibalizers), Cloud demand "tight supply environment" persisting into Q4 and 2026, and Anthropic's announcement of "up to 1mm TPUs" providing the most concrete external silicon validation to date. This print sets the trajectory the subsequent 4Q25 read (+18% revenue, Cloud +48%, $175–185B FY26 capex guide) and 1Q26 read (+22% revenue, $180–190B FY26 capex) extended.

## 3. Forward guidance

- **FY26 CapEx preview:** "Looking out to 2026, we expect a significant increase in CapEx, and we'll provide more detail on our fourth quarter earnings call" (Ashkenazi, p.12). [Forward note: the 4Q25 print disclosed $175–185B FY26 guide, then 1Q26 raised to $180–190B — so this Q3 framing of "significant increase" understated by 1Q vintage what would arrive.]

## 4. Thesis read by theme

### hyperscaler_capex_buildout

- **Status:** Confirm — and signaling the FY26 step-up
- **Evidence:** FY25 capex raised to $91–93B (from $85B prior); Q3 capex $24B; 60/40 servers/data-center mix (Ashkenazi, p.11–12). "Looking out to 2026, we expect a significant increase in CapEx" (Ashkenazi, p.12). Cloud backlog $155B, +46% q/q and +82% y/y — "we have more demand than we have supplied" (Ashkenazi Q&A, p.17). "Tight demand supply environment in Q4 and 2026" (Ashkenazi, p.12). Anthropic "recently shared plans to access up to 1mm TPUs" (Pichai, p.3) — concrete external silicon validation. Q3 depreciation +41% y/y to $5.6B.
- **Implication:** GOOGL is fully committed to the spend-whatever-it-takes cohort, telegraphing the FY26 step-up two quarters ahead of the formal $175–185B guide. Backlog +82% y/y is the cleanest forward demand signal; Anthropic 1mm TPU disclosure is the cleanest external custom-silicon validation. Read-through strongly positive to NVDA (GB300 A4X Max shipping), AVGO (TPU manufacturing partner), TSM (TPU + GPU foundry), ANET, COHR, LITE, VRT, MPWR, GEV, PWR, BE. The "depreciation accelerating" framing is the first concrete signal that 2026 P&L will absorb the cost of the capex build.

### ad_market_strength

- **Status:** Confirm — broad-based with retail and financial services leading
- **Evidence:** Search & other +15% to $56.6B, "growth across all major verticals with the largest contributions from retail and financial services" (Schindler, p.6; Ashkenazi, p.10). Healthcare also a contributor (Schindler Q&A, p.13). YouTube ads +15% to $10.3B — first y/y acceleration in several quarters — "driven by direct response advertising, followed by brand" (Ashkenazi, p.10). Paid clicks +7%, CPCs +7% (Schindler Q&A, p.14). AI Max "rolled out globally in September... already used by hundreds of thousands of advertisers... fastest-growing AI-powered Search Ads product"; "unlocked billions of net new queries" (Schindler, p.7). Kayak +12% conversion value early; PMax financial-services case +39% conversion volume y/y (Schindler, p.7). Network ads −3% to $7.4B — open-web bucket continues to fade.
- **Implication:** Owned-and-operated surfaces carry the print; programmatic Network keeps fading. YouTube +15% is the high-water mark for the trailing year — by 4Q25 it decelerates to +9% (lapping US election spend per management's own Q4 guidance preview), confirming the structural concern. The "paid clicks +7% / CPCs +7%" symmetry tells you AI Mode is not yet cannibalizing monetization. Bullish read-through to META (direct-response performance ads); bullish SMB-uplift narrative; cautionary for open programmatic names (MGNI, PUBM). Retail vertical strength read-through positive to SHOP, MELI, WMT digital ad businesses; financial services vertical strength is a new disclosed driver worth tracking.

### search_disruption

- **Status:** Drift (favorable for GOOGL — Search reaccelerated to +15% with AI Mode + AI Overviews as growth drivers, not headwinds)
- **Evidence:** "AI is driving an expansionary moment for Search" (Pichai, p.4; Schindler, p.6). "Overall queries and commercial queries continued to grow y-over-y... This growth rate increased in Q3, largely driven by our AI investments in Search, most notably AI Overviews and AI Mode" (Pichai, p.4). AI Mode scaled to 40 languages "in record time" — 75mm+ DAU in US; queries doubled over the quarter (Pichai, p.4). 100+ product improvements to AI Mode in Q3 (Pichai, p.4). "AI Mode is already driving incremental total query growth for Search" (Pichai, p.4). AI Overviews scaled to 2B+ users; "we see the monetization at approximately the same rate" as traditional Search baseline (Schindler Q&A, p.14). "Effect was more pronounced with younger people" (Pichai, p.4) — generational durability signal. Pichai on cannibalization (Q&A p.18): "we may have done text search, image search, video search, et cetera, and then we kind of brought it together as Universal Search. So, you will see evolutions like that" — framing AI Mode / Overviews as a Universal-Search-style consolidation that GOOGL controls end-to-end.
- **Implication:** Bear case ("agentic search guts Google") continues to weaken. AI Overviews monetizing "approximately the same rate" as classic Search is the cleanest data point on monetization parity for the print. AI Mode queries doubling and 75mm DAU is the consumer-engagement proof. Most importantly: AI Overviews and AI Mode are now confirmed total-query-growth drivers, not cannibalizers — combined with paid clicks +7% / CPCs +7%, the operational evidence is that AI integration is expansionary, not deflationary. Bearish for PPLX, ChatGPT-as-search, thin-content publishers; cautionary for RDDT/PINS as AI Mode captures long-tail commercial queries. Validates the watchlist note that "search defense holding better than feared."

3. **YouTube Q4 deceleration pre-flagged (Ashkenazi outlook, p.11).** "y-over-y comparisons in advertising will be negatively impacted by the strong spend on US elections in Q4 2024, particularly on YouTube." Management gave the explicit out before the 4Q25 print delivered YouTube +9% (down from +15% here). Worth tracking whether re-acceleration materializes in 1Q26 / 2Q26 — by the MoffettNathanson conference (May 2026), the YouTube CEO is taking explicit questions on this.
4. **"What gives you confidence" on ROIC dodge-and-redirect (Nathanson, p.17).** Nathanson asked Ashkenazi explicitly for ROIC framework with concrete early signs. 5. **Cannibalization framing — Universal Search precedent (Pichai, Gawrelski Q&A p.18).** Pichai's framing of AI Mode + AI Overviews + traditional Search as a Universal-Search-style consolidation is significant: it positions all three as features GOOGL controls and unifies over time, not as zero-sum competitors. Honest acknowledgment that absolute pace stays high but inter-release intervals may stretch.
