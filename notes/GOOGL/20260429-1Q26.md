## 1. Headline read

Print confirms the AI-capable-incumbent thesis on every primary dimension and meaningfully extends the 4Q25 (+18%) trajectory: consolidated revenue $109.9B (+22% y/y, +19% cc), Search & other ads reaccelerating to +19% ($60.4B), Cloud accelerating again to +63% ($20B), Cloud backlog nearly doubling sequentially to $462B, FY26 capex raised to $180–190B from $175–185B (with explicit "2027 CapEx to significantly increase compared to 2026" preview). Material strategic shift: TPU hardware sales to a select group of customers in their own data centers — the merchant-silicon move Pichai/Ashkenazi declined to pre-announce across 3Q25, 4Q25, and the Morgan Stanley TMT conference is now formal policy.

## 3. Forward guidance

- **FY26 CapEx guidance raised to $180–190B** (from prior $175–185B), with the raise attributed to the Intersect acquisition (closed March) (Ashkenazi, p.11–12). "Unprecedented internal and external demand for AI compute resources."
- **FY27 CapEx pre-announcement:** "We expect our 2027 CapEx to significantly increase compared to 2026" (Ashkenazi, p.12). First time GOOGL has front-run a multi-year capex escalation in our record — material forward signal.
- **Q2 2026 FX:** Approximately 1pp FX tailwind expected at current spot rates (vs. 3pp tailwind realized in Q1) (Ashkenazi, p.11).
- **TPU hardware sales to third parties:** "Will begin to deliver a TPU hardware to a select group of customers in their own data centers... small percent of the revenues from these agreements later this year, with the vast majority of revenues to be realized in 2027" (Ashkenazi, p.11). Material strategic shift — the merchant-silicon move pre-figured by three consecutive dodges in our prior record is now formal policy. TPU hardware sales revenue will fluctuate quarter-to-quarter based on shipment timing.
- **Wiz acquisition reporting:** Wiz reported in Google Cloud segment; "low single-digit percentage point headwind to Cloud's operating margin for the remainder of 2026 related to the acquisition" (Ashkenazi, p.12).
- **Depreciation pressure continues:** "The significant increase in our investment in technical infrastructure will continue to put pressure on the P&L in the form of higher depreciation expense and related data center operations costs such as energy" (Ashkenazi, p.12).
- **Continued AI / Cloud hiring** + marketing investment for AI products (Ashkenazi, p.12).
- **Cloud supply still constrained:** "Our Cloud revenue would have been higher if we were able to meet the demand" (Pichai Q&A, p.16). This is now the fourth consecutive print framing Cloud as supply-constrained.
- **Tone:** Pichai — "terrific quarter for Alphabet," "momentum was on full display"; Ashkenazi — "outstanding first quarter," "11th consecutive quarter of double-digit revenue growth." No sandbagging signals.
- **Next earnings:** Q2 2026 print confirmed 2026-07-23 (InsiderScore `future_earnings_dates`, market cap $4.696T at query time).
- **Other forward catalysts:** I/O developer conference (May 19), Brandcast (May 13), Google Marketing Live (May).

- **Status:** Confirm — and accelerating
- **Evidence:** FY26 capex raised to $180–190B (Ashkenazi, p.11–12) from $175–185B at 4Q25; Q1 capex $35.7B (vs. $27.9B Q4 2025 and $24B Q3 2025). 60% servers / 40% data centers + networking equipment mix maintained (Ashkenazi, p.10). FY27 capex "significantly increase compared to 2026" — first multi-year forward-frame in our record (Ashkenazi, p.12). Cloud backlog $462B (~2x q/q from $240B in Q4 2025, ~3x y/y from $155B in Q3 2025). "Unprecedented internal and external demand for AI compute resources" (Ashkenazi, p.12). Pichai Q&A (Nathanson Q, p.16): "Our Cloud revenue would have been higher if we were able to meet the demand. So, we are working through that moment and we are investing." Wiz acquisition closed March; Intersect acquisition closed March (data center + energy infrastructure).
- **Implication:** GOOGL is unambiguously in the spend-whatever-it-takes cohort and is now actively front-running the FY27 number. The ~2x sequential backlog jump to $462B — with TPU hardware agreements now included — is the cleanest single-quarter demand-signal escalation in our trajectory record (3Q25 $155B → 4Q25 $240B → 1Q26 $462B). Read-through strongly positive to NVDA (Vera Rubin NVL72 instances launching at GCP), AVGO (TPU manufacturing partner — TPU hardware sales to third parties is incremental volume positive for AVGO), TSM (foundry for both GPU and TPU), ANET, COHR, LITE, VRT, MPWR, GEV, PWR, BE. The Cloud-revenue-would-be-higher Pichai disclosure validates the upside-constrained pricing thesis already standing for the supply-side ecosystem.

- **Status:** Confirm — and reaccelerating (Search +15% Q3 → +17% Q4 → +19% Q1)
- **Evidence:** Search & other +19% y/y to $60.4B — Schindler: "primarily driven by retail and finance" with health also "drove the greatest contribution, although all major verticals actually contributed" (p.7, Q&A p.14). YouTube ads +11% to $9.9B, "driven by direct response advertising as well as brand" (Ashkenazi, p.11) — re-accelerating from Q4's +9% but still well below Search. Network ads −4% to $7.0B — continues to fade. AI Max "moved out of beta" with improved targeting and creative quality (Schindler, p.8). Hilton EMEA: "one-third more clicks for a fifth of the spend... 55% higher average booking value" (Schindler, p.8). Etsy: "10% search volume uplift with 15% of those queries being net new" (Schindler, p.8). "More than 30% of our customers' search spend now uses AI-enabled campaigns, AI Max, or Performance Max" (Schindler, p.8). Direct offers in AI Mode launched with Gap, L'Oréal, Chewy as pilot partners (Schindler, p.8). Schindler Q&A (Anmuth, p.14): "the strength we saw in Search was not due to a single driver" — broad-based, "we make hundreds of changes every quarter," and "there is upside in that coverage number" (i.e., the ~20% historical ad coverage of queries can go higher with AI-better intent matching). Gemini "now uses... intent to an advertiser's product and services more accurately" (Schindler, p.7).
- **Implication:** Owned-and-operated surfaces are reaccelerating, not decelerating, on the back of Gemini-powered ad infrastructure. The 4Q25 → 1Q26 Search reacceleration from +17% to +19% on a $60B+ base is the operational proof that AI-Mode-cannibalization fears were misplaced — AI-driven ad infrastructure is now a growth-rate-positive force. The "coverage upside" Schindler explicitly hinted at is the next monetization lever to track. YouTube +11% recovering from +9% is encouraging but still a watch item (the MoffettNathanson conference fireside on 2026-05-14 surfaced this divergence). Bullish read-through to META on direct-response performance ads; SMB-uplift narrative continues; cautionary for open programmatic (MGNI, PUBM). Retail strength bullish for SHOP, MELI, WMT digital ads; finance strength is the new vertical to track.

### search_disruption

- **Status:** Drift (favorable — now the strongest single quarter of the AI-Mode-as-growth-driver narrative in our record)
- **Evidence:** "AI continues to drive search usage, and queries are at an all-time high" (Pichai, p.4). "AI Overviews, which are driving overall Search growth, and we are also seeing strong growth in both users and usage of AI Mode globally" (Pichai, p.4). Personal Intelligence expanded broadly in US (Pichai, p.4). "Shipped agentic experiences like restaurant booking to new countries and new multimodal capabilities like Search Live globally" (Pichai, p.4). Latency: "reduced search latency by more than 35% over the past five years" (Pichai, p.4). Efficiency: "since upgrading AI Overviews and AI Mode to Gemini 3, we've reduced the cost of core AI responses by more than 30%" (Pichai, p.4) — material unit-cost claim. Schindler Q&A (Anmuth, p.14): "AI Overviews and AI Mode continue to drive greater search usage and growth in overall queries, including in commercial queries... with the ability of AI to better understand intent and a lot of other vectors around it, I think there is upside in that coverage number." Schindler Q&A (Shmulik, p.17–18): "AI is fundamentally changing how the world searches... Traditional search really started with 10 blue links, and now we have AI Overviews and AI Mode, and they have made search more intelligent than ever, and they let you ask far more complex questions... we have SMBs that can reach customers at a scale that really wasn't possible even a few years ago." Pichai Q&A (Nowak, p.13): identifies "bringing agentic flows, workflows to consumers, in a way that it's easy for them to do, including in the context of Search" as the next product frontier.
- **Implication:** The bear case ("agentic search guts Google") continues to weaken structurally and is now arguably the *least* probability-weighted scenario in management's framing. Search reaccelerating to +19% with AI Mode + AI Overviews as explicit growth drivers, combined with the −30% AI-response unit-cost disclosure, means the AI integration is now economically accretive in both directions (more queries AND lower cost-per-AI-response). The Schindler "coverage upside" comment is the cleanest forward-monetization lever in the print — moving ad coverage above the historical ~20% baseline is a meaningful expansionary lever. **This is the quarter where the watchlist note's "4+ rather than 5 because Search is at risk from agentic search" residual concern becomes a serious candidate for revision to 5.** Bearish for PPLX, ChatGPT-as-search, thin-content publishers; cautionary RDDT/PINS continues as AI Mode captures long-tail commercial queries; bullish read-through to META (similar AI-driven ad-infrastructure benefits expected).

### silicon_architecture_competition

- **Status:** Confirm — and the merchant-silicon strategic shift is now formal
- **Evidence:** **TPU hardware sales to third parties formally launched:** "As TPU demand grows from AI labs, capital markets firms, and high-performance computing applications we'll begin to deliver TPUs to a select group of customers in their own data centers in a hardware configuration to expand our addressable market opportunity" (Pichai, p.6). Ashkenazi (p.11): "small percent of the revenues from these agreements later this year, with the vast majority of revenues to be realized in 2027... revenues from TPU hardware sales will fluctuate from quarter-to-quarter." Backlog $462B "increase was driven by strong demand for enterprise AI offerings and the inclusion of TPU hardware sales" (Ashkenazi, p.11). Eighth-generation TPUs introduced at Cloud Next, specialized: "TPU 8t provides high-performance model training with 3 times the processing power of Ironwood and 2 times the performance"; "TPU 8i delivers cost-effective, low-latency inference, with 80% better performance per dollar than the prior generation" (Pichai, p.3). NVIDIA Vera Rubin NVL72 launching at GCP "in addition to the Blackwell- and Hopper-based instances already available" (Pichai, p.3). Customer wins on TPU: Thinking Machines Lab, Hudson River Trading, Boston Dynamics (Pichai, p.6). Pichai Q&A (Nowak, p.13): TPU strategy framed as "we take ROIC approach. And some of it helps us get more economies of scale, scale in our overall compute environment as well." Pichai Q&A (Sheridan, p.15): "We are unique in the market because of our vertically optimized AI stack and the way we codevelop the components from our infrastructure and models to platforms and the tools to applications and agents. And the fact that we own frontier models, own the silicon really helps us stay ahead of the curve."
- **Implication:** **This is the strategic-shift print.** The merchant-silicon move that Pichai/Ashkenazi declined to commit to across 3Q25 (Sheridan Q), 4Q25 (Anmuth Q), and the Morgan Stanley TMT conference (March direct question) is now formal policy. The framing is deliberate — "expand our addressable market opportunity" is the new strategic vocabulary. TPU 8t/8i specialization (training vs. inference) is the architectural commitment to compete head-on with NVIDIA across the workload spectrum. The "80% better performance per dollar" claim on TPU 8i is the cleanest inference-economics escalation in our record. Read-through: **incrementally bearish NVDA** on the TPU-merchant-distribution overhang plus continued inference unit-cost compression; **bullish AVGO** (TPU manufacturing partner — third-party TPU sales is incremental volume); bullish TSM (foundry for both architectures); cautious for AMD and other merchant-accelerator entrants as the TPU competitive proof point gets stronger. This is also a `inference_compute_economics` and `model_efficiency_evolution` signal — combined with the −30% AI-response unit-cost disclosure from earlier in the call, GOOGL is generating compounding deflationary pressure on inference costs.
