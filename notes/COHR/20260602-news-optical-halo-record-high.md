# Coherent — all-time high on Computex optical read-through (2026-06-02)

_Web-verified market event. COHR hit an all-time high on the Computex/Huang AI-optics narrative. Surfaced in the v1.5 digest as "Why is Coherent stock surging today?" (Google, MEDIUM)._

## Event (2026-06-02)
COHR hit an **all-time high, ~+16–17% on the day**, on the optical-connectivity read-through from **Jensen Huang's Computex keynote**, which emphasized accelerating demand for high-speed optical interconnect inside hyperscale AI data centers. The same catalyst sent MRVL ~+25% and lifted the broader optical complex (LITE/Lumentum, Corning).

## Why COHR specifically
- **NVDA holds a disclosed strategic equity stake in COHR**, validating Coherent's indium-phosphide optics as critical to next-gen AI infrastructure.
- Coherent builds the **actual optical hardware** (800G/1.6T transceivers, interconnects) — distinct from MRVL, which designs the connectivity silicon.
- At its **May 6 earnings**, management confirmed AI optical-transceiver demand is **booked out through 2028**.
- Context: COHR ~**+126% YTD**, ~**+455%** from its 52-week low of $76.88.
- **Multiple analysts raised targets** on 2026-06-02 amid the move (per cited sources).

## Analyst context (dated — NOT a June 2 event)
**BofA, 2026-05-13:** raised COHR price target to **$400 from $365**, rating **maintained at Neutral** (not an upgrade), on a thematic raise of its 2030 AI-datacenter systems TAM to **~$1.7T from ~$1.4T**, citing COHR's positioning in 800G/1.6T transceivers. The Neutral stance reflected the stock having already run hard. This is the analyst action behind the "BofA" association — it predates the June 2 surge by ~3 weeks.

## Thesis significance
Reinforces the LITE/COHR optical complex as a direct AI-infrastructure-capex beneficiary and a competitor-set anchor for the T3 optical/photonics names. The NVDA equity stake + demand-booked-to-2028 are the durable structural points; the June 2 move itself was a sentiment/halo event, not a fundamental change.
