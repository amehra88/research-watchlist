_Prepared by earnings-reviewer, 2026-05-29. Tier: T3 (watchlist). First earnings note on file for MDB._

## 1. Headline read

Clean beat-and-raise quarter: revenue $688M vs. ~$664M consensus (+3.6%), non-GAAP EPS $1.32 vs. ~$1.19 (+11%), with Atlas accelerating to +29% YoY (fourth consecutive quarter at >=29%) and full-year FY27 guide raised to $2.92-$2.96B (19-20% growth). Print confirms the AI-re-architected-incumbent / AI-infrastructure-software thesis on direction; the Frontier Lab + AI-native traction (Adobe, ElevenLabs, Endor Labs, Zomato) and LangChain integration are concrete proof points that MongoDB is being adopted as the memory/state layer for agentic applications, though management remains explicit that "the most transformative AI-driven impacts are still ahead."

## 3. Forward guidance

**Q2 FY27 (quarter ending 2026-07-31):**
- Revenue $729-$734M, +23-24% YoY
- Non-GAAP operating income $152-$156M, ~21% margin at high end

**Full-year FY27:**
- Revenue raised to $2.92-$2.96B, +19-20% YoY
- Non-GAAP operating income $571-$591M, ~20% margin at high end
- Targeting Rule of 40 (20% growth + 20% margin) at the high end

**Tone and philosophy.** Management remains in a "raise across the board" posture, with explicit confidence-language ("strong confidence in growth prospects"). Notable callouts on the guide construction:
- Atlas guide raised for FY27 reflecting "reduced sensitivity to individual customer consumption swings" — management framing Atlas as becoming more predictable, not just larger. This is a meaningful narrative shift if it holds.
- EA & Other expected flat in H2 FY27 due to difficult comps with a strong Q4 FY26. Management is forecasting "only deals with high closure probability" — prudent framing.
- Q2 is guided to +23-24% on the back of timing of several large multiyear EA deals; this is a flatter sequential trajectory implied for H2, consistent with the EA H2 caution.
- No change to go-to-market strategy signaled for FY27 (Berry directly confirmed in Q&A to Lenschow).

## 4. Thesis read by theme

### enterprise_ai_adoption

- **Status:** Confirm
- **Evidence:** Atlas customer count grew to 66,400 (vs. 55,800 PY); 45% of $100K+ ARR Atlas customers now use 2+ platform features (vs. 37% PY). Frontier Labs partnerships confirmed by Desai in response to Colville (Scotiabank). Specific enterprise AI proof points: Adobe using MongoDB as memory layer for AI agents; Endor Labs supporting 225% YoY revenue growth on Atlas; Zomato cut support costs 55% with Atlas-powered AI.
- **Implication:** Enterprise AI adoption is broadening from pilots into production workloads on MongoDB at named, recognizable customers. The 45% multi-feature attach rate (up from 37%) is the cleanest single metric supporting the thesis — customers are deepening, not just adding. Thesis intact; modest positive bias on pace.

### ai_infrastructure_software

- **Status:** Confirm
- **Evidence:** LangChain integration announced as a strategic partnership "to enhance development of agentic applications," positioning MongoDB as "preferred data layer for AI workloads." Vector Search + Voyage AI embeddings now embedded natively. Two new Chief Product Officers appointed (one for core/Atlas/EA, one for AI & emerging products) — explicit organizational signal that AI products are getting dedicated executive bandwidth.
- **Implication:** MongoDB is positioning explicitly as AI infrastructure software, not just a database vendor riding the wave. The LangChain tie-up and dedicated AI CPO are structural moves, not marketing. Thesis confirmed; the question is whether vector + memory-layer functionality is sufficiently differentiated vs. pgvector / Pinecone / native cloud vector services over the next 12-18 months.

### ai_re_architected_incumbent

- **Status:** Confirm with drift
- **Evidence:** Desai (in response to Lenschow on data consolidation) "emphasized modernization over consolidation" — i.e., MongoDB's pitch is that the AI wave forces app re-architecture and MongoDB benefits from greenfield rebuilds, not from consolidation plays. Singh (Morgan Stanley) Q on "AI stack value capture" got a reply emphasizing "MongoDB's architectural advantages for AI workloads." Management is positioning the document model + vector + real-time as inherently better-suited to agentic workloads than relational systems.
- **Implication:** The thesis works, but the framing has drifted from "incumbent re-architecture" to "greenfield AI-native architecture wins." That's a subtle but real shift — MDB is less of a re-architected-incumbent story (the company itself being modernized) and more of a beneficiary-of-app-re-architecture story (its customers modernize, and MDB picks up the workload). Thesis directionally intact, but the operator should consider whether the theme label still maps cleanly or if `ai_native_vertical` / `ai_infrastructure_software` are now the better-fitting tags.

### inference_compute_economics

- **Status:** Confirm (indirect)
- **Evidence:** Atlas consumption "stronger than expected, driven by established enterprise use cases in financial services, technology, media, as well as early adoption by AI-native and Frontier Lab customers." Net ARR expansion 121% (vs. 119% PY) — consumption-driven economics expanding. Desai (to Martino, Goldman) discussed agentic workloads' impact on consumption, framing MongoDB as well-positioned to capture incremental compute as agents proliferate.
- **Implication:** MDB benefits from the consumption side of inference economics — every additional agent query, memory write, vector retrieval flows through Atlas. Thesis confirmed indirectly: rising inference workloads = rising Atlas consumption. The risk symmetry is also live: if inference per-token cost compression accelerates, customer Atlas spend per workload could compress unless workload count scales faster. No evidence on the call of consumption pressure yet.

## 8. Management Q&A flags

1. **Atlas seasonality + AI-native (MacWilliams, Wells Fargo):** Desai gave a substantive answer citing ElevenLabs as a flagship AI-native customer. Notable that he used a specific named customer rather than generic "AI-native" language — small but credibility-positive signal.
2. **Data consolidation vs. modernization (Lenschow, Barclays):** Desai's response — "emphasized modernization over consolidation" — is a deliberate framing pivot. Worth tracking: this reframes the MDB story away from the data-platform consolidation narrative (where Snowflake/Databricks dominate the messaging) toward an app-re-architecture narrative where MDB has a cleaner story.
3. **Go-to-market changes for AI natives (Kidron, Oppenheimer):** Desai "acknowledged ongoing work in this area" — soft dodge. There is GTM work underway but management not yet ready to describe what's changing. Flag for next quarter.
4. **EA H2 commentary (Kidron follow-up):** Berry's clarification that H2 EA will be flat against tough comps was important — it sets expectations for a Q3/Q4 deceleration that's structural (comp-driven), not demand-driven. If the print misses this guide in H2, management will likely lean on this clarification. Pre-disclosed risk handled cleanly.
5. **Large deals impact on CRPO (Keirstead, UBS):** Desai/Berry described the strong bookings as "multiyear nature" and from "existing enterprise customers" — i.e., not new-logo driven and not pull-forward in a problematic way. Worth verifying in 10-Q deferred revenue / RPO disclosure when filed.
