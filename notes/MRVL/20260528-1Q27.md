## 1. Headline read

Confirm-with-acceleration print. MRVL beat its own Q1 FY27 guidance on revenue and EPS, raised FY27 to ~$11.5B (+40% y/y) and FY28 to ~$16.5B (+45% y/y, $1.5B above prior), now sees Q3 reaching $3B "a quarter ahead of previous outlook," and projected custom XPU revenue >$10B by FY29. Expanded NVIDIA partnership (NVLink Fusion, AI-RAN, Optics, with a direct NVIDIA investment in MRVL) and Celestial AI / Polariton acquisitions sharpen the optics + interconnect moat. The print confirms all six assigned themes; no theme is drifting or breaking.

## 3. Forward guidance

**Q2 FY27 guide:** Revenue $2.7B (+12% q/q, +35% y/y); non-GAAP EPS $0.88-$0.98; non-GAAP gross margin 58.25%-59.25%; non-GAAP opex ~$600M. Data center expected to accelerate to mid-40% y/y in Q2. Communications expected mid-single-digit sequential decline but continued y/y growth.

**Q3 FY27 commentary:** Now expected to reach $3B revenue - "a quarter ahead of previous outlook."

**FY27 (raised):** ~$11.5B revenue, +40% y/y; data center +50% y/y; interconnect +>70% y/y (up from prior +50% guide); switching to exceed $600M.

**FY28 (raised):** ~$16.5B revenue (+45% y/y), $1.5B above prior guidance, despite a moderation in cloud capex growth to "~30% range." Data center +55% y/y. Interconnect DCI doubling to $1B (from $500M FY26). Switching >$1B. Custom silicon to more than double y/y. Non-GAAP opex to grow mid-to-high teens % (well below 45% revenue growth) → targeting upper end of 38-40% operating margin model.

**FY29 line of sight:** Custom XPU revenue $10B+; $55B custom TAM cited; >50 active customer opportunities.

Tone: aggressive raises on a print that already exceeded guidance, with management citing "record bookings" and "strong customer signals extending into next year." Guidance philosophy has shifted from point-estimate quarterly guides to multi-year framing with explicit fiscal-year and FY+1 dollar targets - a pricing-power and visibility signal.

## 4. Thesis read by theme

### silicon_architecture_competition

- **Status:** Confirm
- **Evidence:** Custom XPU and XPU-attach business projected to "more than double y/y" in FY28 with >$10B revenue by FY29 across >50 customer opportunities and a $55B TAM. SRAM-based IP and high-speed I/O cited by mgmt (Sur Q&A, Murphy response) as the differentiation for memory-heavy AI architectures. New Tier-1 XPU wins beyond existing programs flagged.
- **Implication:** MRVL is consolidating a credible #2 custom-silicon position behind AVGO. The $10B FY29 number, if realized, lands MRVL meaningfully in the same conversation as AVGO's custom XPU franchise. Maintains the "custom silicon duopoly" investment frame against NVDA merchant GPUs - confirms the architecture-competition thesis as constructive, not break.

### ai_infrastructure_capex

- **Status:** Confirm (with nuanced positive)
- **Evidence:** FY28 +45% revenue growth maintained "despite a moderation in the rate of cloud CapEx growth, which is projected to be in the 30% range" (prepared remarks, Guidance). This decouples MRVL growth from absolute capex growth - implying share gain and rising content per box.
- **Implication:** Constructive read on the durability of MRVL's growth even if hyperscaler capex moderates. The "MRVL outgrows capex" framing is now explicit from management. Confirms thesis and strengthens it.

### networking_competitive_landscape

- **Status:** Confirm
- **Evidence:** Interconnect revised to +70% y/y FY27 (up from +50% prior). 1.6T ramp, PAM4 leadership, coherent light expansion, DCI doubling to $1B FY28. Switching to exceed $600M FY27 and >$1B FY28. Polariton (silicon photonics) and Celestial AI (scale-up optics) acquisitions integrated to extend connectivity portfolio. Murphy (Svanberg Q&A): "comprehensive technology portfolio... strategic advantage of offering end-to-end solutions."
- **Implication:** MRVL is moving from a connectivity-component supplier to a system-level interconnect platform. Direct competitive read-through: pressures AVGO on optics share, ANET on the switching-adjacent fabric, CRDO on PAM4/DSP, COHR/LITE on coherent optics. Confirms theme as one of the strongest drivers.

### hyperscaler_revenue_concentration

- **Status:** Confirm (with slight constructive drift toward diversification)
- **Evidence:** Murphy (Arcuri Q&A) detailed "engagements with US hyperscalers" with >50 active custom opportunities and "current growth projections based on existing design wins, with potential future contributions from new programs." New Tier-1 XPU programs flagged separately from existing programs. NVIDIA partnership + direct NVIDIA equity investment adds a new high-quality customer dimension.
- **Implication:** Concentration remains high, but the customer roster is broadening (multiple Tier-1 hyperscalers + NVIDIA as both partner and shareholder). Pipeline depth (>50 opportunities) lowers the single-customer single-program tail risk that was a prior overhang. Confirms with a positive nuance.

### ai_compute_topology

- **Status:** Confirm
- **Evidence:** Pajjuri Q&A on scale-out vs. scale-up: Murphy described MRVL as "emerging" in scale-out and pointed to "larger potential in scale-up networking, driven by new technologies and market dynamics." Celestial AI integration positioned for scale-up optics. NVLink Fusion partnership with NVIDIA explicitly cited as enabling MRVL components to interface with NVIDIA's rack-scale topology.
- **Implication:** MRVL is positioning at the scale-up frontier (intra-rack, GPU-to-GPU coherent connectivity), historically NVIDIA's proprietary moat. The Fusion partnership turns this from a competitive threat into a participation lane. Confirms thesis; topology trend is one of the highest-leverage TAM expansions in the call.

### inference_compute_economics

- **Status:** Confirm
- **Evidence:** Custom silicon growth (XPU + XPU attach) thesis explicitly grounded in inference economics - mgmt cited "memory cycle concerns and CXL-based design adoption" (Curtis Q&A) as drivers for CXL-attach opportunity alongside accelerators. SRAM-based IP positioning explicitly tied to "memory-heavy AI architectures."
- **Implication:** MRVL's product roadmap is aligned to the inference-compute pivot - memory bandwidth and inter-chip connectivity, rather than raw compute, are the bottlenecks management is solving for. Confirms thesis; constructive for MRVL's relative positioning vs. pure-FLOPS silicon stories.

## 9. Vocabulary candidates

- **scale-up optics** / **scale-up interconnect** - distinct from scale-out networking; intra-rack GPU-to-GPU coherent optical paths (NVLink-class). Could become its own theme if more names start framing around it (NVDA, AVGO, COHR, LITE, CRDO read-through).
- **XPU attach** - memory and networking attach revenue tied to a custom XPU design win; multiplier on the underlying XPU sockets. MRVL-specific framing; may merit theme if AVGO uses similar language.
- **NVLink Fusion** - NVIDIA's program for third-party silicon to interface with NVDA rack-scale interconnect. Notable shift in NVDA strategy and worth tracking as a category in subsequent NVDA / MRVL / AVGO calls.
- **CECL opportunity** (Curtis Q&A, paraphrase of CXL): if accurate, suggests CXL-attach memory expansion as a discrete TAM layer. Worth disambiguating whether the transcript meant "CXL" - InsiderScore summarizer may have transcribed phonetically.
- **DCI (data center interconnect) modules** - already a category in industry usage; MRVL's $500M FY26 -> $1B FY28 doubling makes this a distinct revenue line worth tagging.
