## 3. Forward guidance

Full-year 2026 revenue guidance raised to **$228M–$232M** (29–32% YoY growth), up from prior range. Plaque-specific revenue guide raised to **$19M–$21M**. Non-GAAP gross margin guide raised to **~81%** for 2026, with management reaffirming a midterm **85% target**. Plaque installed base target: **1,200 sites by year-end 2026** (vs 340 new accounts added in 2025).

Tone is confident, with explicit upside flagged for 2027 driven by (a) PCI Navigator broader rollout, (b) Plaque ASP step-up as initial low-priced contracts roll into renewal cycles, (c) RCT readouts beginning H2'26 and H1'27 expanding TAM by $6B toward an $11B opportunity. Guidance philosophy is point-anchored with range; management acknowledged the conservative-looking guide vs Q1 momentum (Marcus, Wise both probed) and defended on the basis of medical-education ramp timing for Plaque and need for clinical-community awareness build. Cash position $254.9M described as sufficient through profitability.

## 4. Thesis read by theme

### ai_native_vertical

- **Status:** Confirm
- **Evidence:** From the transcript summary: "The AI-powered platform now includes RoadMap, Plaque, FFRCT, and the newly launched PCI Navigator. The platform is presented as unique in providing a unified, integrated care pathway... The proprietary image database surpassed 200M annotated CCTA images, supporting rapid innovation, product launches, and gross margin efficiency." On Wise's question about annotated CCTA images, Farquhar explicitly framed the database as a competitive advantage driving innovation and efficiency. Gross margin expansion attributed in part to "AI automation."
- **Implication:** HTFL is operating as a textbook AI-native vertical: proprietary data asset (200M+ annotated CCTA images) compounding into both product innovation velocity (4 platform modules now shipping) and unit-economic leverage (gross margin up 520bps YoY with AI-enabled automation cited as a driver). The flywheel framing surfaced by Vazquez's question was endorsed by management. Thesis intact and arguably strengthening — data moat is being explicitly monetized through margin, not just product.

### vertical_ai_applications

- **Status:** Confirm
- **Evidence:** Plaque Analysis adoption "exceeding initial expectations" with 75% covered lives, 340 new accounts ramping from 2025 cohort and target of 1,200 sites by YE26. PCI Navigator launched April 2026 with "strong cardiologist feedback." DECIDE trial 90-day data showed 51% management change and 19-point LDL reduction — clinical-utility evidence that drives the vertical thesis. Two RCTs initiating H2'26 plus a third H1'27 designed to expand TAM by $6B to $11B total.
- **Implication:** The vertical (coronary artery disease diagnostic and procedural planning) is being expanded along every dimension that matters for a vertical-AI bull case: (1) deeper into the workflow via PCI Navigator, (2) earlier in the funnel via Plaque for asymptomatic populations, (3) broader payer coverage, (4) more clinical evidence to compound switching costs. The CT-first non-invasive testing shift management referenced is a multi-year structural tailwind. Thesis confirmed; trajectory is acceleration, not steady-state.

## 8. Management Q&A flags

- **Robert Marcus (JPMorgan, via Robbie) / Rick Wise (Stifel) — guidance conservatism probe:** Both analysts pushed on why guide raise was modest given Q1 outperformance, particularly on Plaque. Farquhar defended on coverage progress and adoption ramp timing; Verghese pointed to volume-growth-led upside with ASPs aligning to consensus. This is the cleanest flag on the call — management is explicitly choosing to keep guidance achievable rather than chase Q1 momentum. Read as conservatism, not sandbagging, but worth tracking whether the 2H'26 Plaque commentary stays consistent.
- **Matthew O'Brien (Piper, via Samantha) — clinical awareness gap on Plaque reimbursement:** O'Brien raised concerns that the clinical community is not fully aware of Plaque reimbursement availability. Farquhar acknowledged this as an ongoing challenge being addressed through education. This is the one genuine friction point surfaced — the 75% covered-lives figure is necessary but not sufficient for adoption if physicians don't know about it. Tracking item for next quarter.
- **Brandon Vazquez (William Blair) — flywheel framing:** Vazquez offered the "flywheel effect" framing for HTFL's portfolio adoption and Farquhar endorsed it, attributing it to the platform's expanding utility and the proprietary database. Notable that an analyst framed it this way and management leaned in rather than deflecting — supports the AI-native vertical thesis read.
- **Kallum Titchmarsh (Morgan Stanley) — DECIDE one-year outcomes:** Titchmarsh asked about expected impact of one-year DECIDE outcomes (due H2'26) on Plaque adoption. Farquhar highlighted importance for payer and physician engagement. This is a flagged near-term catalyst — H2'26 data readout could materially affect Plaque ramp trajectory and 2027 ASP commentary.
