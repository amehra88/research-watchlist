## 4. Thesis read by theme

### foundry_capacity

- **Status:** Confirm
- **Evidence:** Management cited "over 100 global factory projects" with "more than 10 added in the past quarter," and called out leading-edge foundry-logic as part of the segment expected to account for "over 80% of year-on-year wafer fab equipment spending growth in both 2026 and 2027." Customers are providing "rolling eight-quarter forecasts" — unusually long planning visibility for the WFE cycle. (Prepared remarks; Rasgon Q&A on WFE growth)
- **Implication:** The foundry capacity buildout — TSMC, Samsung, and to a lesser extent Intel Foundry — is delivering through-cycle visibility to mid-2027 with a step-up rather than the usual mid-cycle pause. AMAT is reading customer behavior as committed, not exploratory. Foundry capacity remains the dominant Tier-1 driver for AMAT's print.

### semiconductor_cycle

- **Status:** Drift (positive)
- **Evidence:** Semi equipment business raised to "more than 30%" growth for 2026, with similar trajectory expected in 2027. Management noted the company has "nearly doubled manufacturing capacity" and is aligning supply chain to the eight-quarter customer forecast — i.e. planning for sustained elevated run-rate, not a peak. (Prepared remarks; Dickerson response to Rasgon and Schneider)
- **Implication:** This call argues the conventional semicap cycle framing — peak, digest, trough — is being supplanted by an AI-driven secular elongation. Drift positive because the magnitude (30%+ two years running) is larger than typical cycle-peak language, and management is investing capex into the view. Worth watching for any sign of customer pull-ins distorting 2026 at 2027's expense.

### hbm_competitive_landscape

- **Status:** Confirm
- **Evidence:** DRAM revenue +18% YoY to $1.7B, with management calling out leading-edge DRAM as one of the segments expected to drive "over 80% of YoY WFE spending growth in 2026 and 2027." Advanced packaging revenue expected up "more than 50%" in 2026, with NEXX acquisition strengthening panel-level packaging. (Prepared remarks; Sankar Q&A on DRAM node intensity)
- **Implication:** HBM demand is flowing through to DRAM WFE and advanced packaging tools. AMAT's positioning is downstream of the SK Hynix / Samsung / Micron HBM capacity race — every HBM stack expansion drives multiple AMAT tool sales (deposition, etch, packaging). Confirms the read-through from MU/SK Hynix watchlist names.

### china_export_controls

- **Status:** Confirm (no drift — risk priced in)
- **Evidence:** China was 24% of Semi Systems plus AGS revenue. Management noted Huawei restrictions "have been factored into its guidance" and characterized China/ICAPS as flat to slightly higher in 2026. Arcuri (UBS) flagged Huawei risk as a challenging exchange; Dickerson acknowledged supply chain challenges but redirected to AI-driven growth. (Arcuri Q&A; ICAPS sentiment paragraph)
- **Implication:** The base case absorbs the export control regime as a known cost. The 24% China share is a structurally lower run-rate than the 40%+ levels seen in 2023-2024, suggesting most of the de-rating is behind us. Risk asymmetry now skews more to upside (relaxation/clarification) than downside, though tail risk on incremental restrictions remains.

### lithography_roadmap

- **Status:** Confirm
- **Evidence:** Management called out "transition to gate all-around nodes and capacity additions at leading-edge FinFET nodes" as driving Semi Systems growth. Trillium ALD integrated material solution and precision PECVD system flagged as new product introductions tied to GAA. EPIC Center launches fall 2026 with TSMC, Samsung, SK Hynix, Micron, Advantest as partners — strategic positioning for 2nm/A14 nodes. (Prepared remarks; EPIC Platform sentiment paragraph)
- **Implication:** The GAA node transition is the single largest WFE intensity step-up of the decade for non-lithography process steps (deposition, etch, materials engineering). AMAT's exposure is well-matched to where the dollar growth is. Confirms that the litho roadmap is pulling adjacent processes forward in unison rather than litho carrying disproportionate share.

### nand_demand_cycle

- **Status:** Drift (cautious — under-discussed)
- **Evidence:** NAND was not called out as a growth driver in the prepared remarks. Management emphasized "leading-edge foundry-logic, DRAM, and advanced packaging" as the >80% growth drivers, leaving NAND implicitly in the "other ~20%" bucket. No analyst pressed on NAND specifically. (Prepared remarks; absence in Q&A is itself the signal)
- **Implication:** NAND demand recovery is conspicuously absent from the bull narrative. The cycle remains capacity-constrained on the supply side and softer on demand than the AI-driven DRAM cycle. For NAND-levered watchlist names (MU, LRCX, ENTG), the read-through is neutral-to-slightly-negative — AMAT is not seeing the inflection here. Drift because prior conventional wisdom held NAND would join the upcycle by mid-2026; this call doesn't confirm that.

## 8. Management Q&A flags

- **Arcuri (UBS) — systems growth conservatism (challenging exchange):** Arcuri pressed on whether the implied second-half systems growth was being sandbagged given the eight-quarter forecast confidence. Hill defended with a "linear growth assumption" framing, which is itself a tell — flat sequential growth implies upside if customer forecasts hold. Worth watching as a setup-for-beat dynamic into Q3/Q4.
- **Brett (Morgan Stanley) — conductor etch share and process control (challenging exchange):** Brett directly questioned whether AMAT's conductor etch share gains were durable and was skeptical on process control market share. Dickerson defended with e-beam leadership but did not provide quantified share data — a soft answer relative to the rest of the call. Process control is the one segment where AMAT has historically underperformed KLAC, and management's defensiveness here matters more than the sunny framing.
- **Quatrochi (Wells Fargo) — AGS definition change:** Hill disclosed that the mid-teens AGS growth bracket now "excludes the 200-millimeter business." This is a re-baselining that flatters the comparable. Not a red flag but worth a note for the model — apples-to-apples AGS growth comparisons need adjustment.
- **Weathers (Deutsche) — ICAPS outlook:** Hill acknowledged ICAPS recovery is delayed pending excess capacity absorption. Confirms NAND-cycle/automotive softness; consistent with the under-discussion of NAND in prepared remarks.

## 9. Vocabulary candidates

- **"Eight-quarter rolling forecasts"** — a customer-planning visibility construct cited multiple times. If this becomes industry-standard language for WFE demand visibility, it merits inclusion adjacent to `foundry_capacity` / `semiconductor_cycle`.
- **"EPIC platform / EPIC Center"** — AMAT-specific R&D collaboration model. Not a watchlist theme per se, but a competitive-advantage construct worth tracking for any sell-side or industry coverage that adopts the framing.
- **"AIX software"** (>35,000 chambers connected) — AI-enabled service offering inside AGS. Sits at the intersection of `ai_re_architected_incumbent` and `enterprise_ai_adoption` for capital equipment vendors; potential thematic crossover candidate.
- **"Panel-level packaging"** (via NEXX acquisition) — advanced packaging extension. May warrant separation from the broader `advanced_materials_ai_infra` / packaging vocabulary if it becomes its own competitive arena.
