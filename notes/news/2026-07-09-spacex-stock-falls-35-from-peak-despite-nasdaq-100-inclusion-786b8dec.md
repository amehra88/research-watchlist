## SpaceX stock falls 35% from peak despite Nasdaq-100 inclusion

Index inclusion is usually a near-term demand catalyst; a 35% drawdown through it signals the market is not yet willing to hold the space economy premium at post-IPO valuations — a cautionary read for any position sized on inclusion-driven inflows.

- SPCX has retreated 35% from its peak price, per BeInCrypto
- The decline has occurred even after the stock was added to the Nasdaq-100 — an event typically associated with index-driven demand
- Index inclusion did not provide durable price support, suggesting selling pressure exceeded passive-flow buying
- Valuation reset dynamic post-inclusion is the implied read
