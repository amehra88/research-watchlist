## SK Hynix Raises $26.5 Billion in Second-Largest Share Sale Ever to Fund HBM Capacity Buildout

A $26.5B equity raise telegraphs that SK Hynix is making a long-duration bet on HBM demand, but the dilution and capacity addition are near-term headwinds for memory pricing and for peers including Micron — the key question is whether the market reads this as conviction or oversupply risk.

- SK Hynix closed a $26.5 billion share offering, described in coverage as the second-largest such transaction in history.
- The scale of the raise signals a major planned capacity investment commitment, consistent with aggressive HBM expansion.
- A transaction of this magnitude is a cross-asset capital-markets event, resetting near-term supply assumptions for memory.
- Management's willingness to accept significant dilution implies confidence in sustained HBM demand, though the market will weigh that against incremental supply risk.
