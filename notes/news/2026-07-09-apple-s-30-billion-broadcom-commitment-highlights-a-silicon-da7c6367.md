## Apple's $30 billion Broadcom commitment highlights a silicon dependency Apple cannot internalize

Confirms AVGO's structural position in the Apple supply chain is durable and not threatened by Apple's internalization trend; for a PM long AVGO, this is a positive thesis reinforcement on revenue visibility and switching-cost moat.

- Apple has committed $30 billion to Broadcom for a component it cannot design or manufacture internally, per the headline.
- The deal underscores a structural limit to Apple's otherwise aggressive silicon internalization strategy.
- Broadcom is the clear beneficiary: this is a locked-in, large-scale revenue stream from Apple's supply chain.
- The framing ('can't copy') implies Apple's custom silicon roadmap has a deliberate gap that favors AVGO for the foreseeable future.
