## New DRAM Price-Fixing Lawsuit Names Samsung, SK Hynix, and Micron; HBM Expansion in Collusion Spotlight

A successful antitrust action against all three memory leaders would structurally threaten the oligopoly pricing power underpinning AI memory margin expectations; the HBM angle is particularly pointed because it implicates the fastest-growing, highest-ASP product category rather than commodity DRAM.

- A new antitrust lawsuit simultaneously names all three dominant DRAM suppliers — Samsung, SK Hynix, and Micron.
- The case specifically tests whether the HBM market expansion can serve as evidence of collusion among the three.
- All three memory leaders now face formal antitrust tail risk at the same time.
- Coverage is from TrendForce, a specialist semiconductor trade publication, lending sourcing credibility to the filing.
