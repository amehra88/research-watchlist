## Chinese optical transceiver maker Innolight reportedly near $7B Hong Kong IPO approval

A ~$7B public listing for a leading Chinese optical transceiver player would materially raise the competitive bar for COHR and LITE; Innolight gaining public capital and profile could accelerate share pressure in datacom transceivers, a critical AI networking component market.

- Innolight, a Chinese optical transceiver manufacturer, is said to be near regulatory approval for a Hong Kong listing.
- The reported IPO size is approximately $7 billion, per both outlets covering the story.
- A listing at this scale would make Innolight a significant publicly traded competitor to Western optical peers.
- No IPO timeline or additional deal specifics are confirmed beyond the reported valuation.
