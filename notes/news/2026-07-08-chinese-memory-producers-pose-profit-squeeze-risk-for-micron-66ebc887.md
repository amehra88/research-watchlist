## Chinese Memory Producers Pose Profit-Squeeze Risk for Micron and SanDisk

Chinese memory expansion is the key overhang on the NAND recovery thesis — if domestic producers can undercut on price, MU's pricing power and cycle recovery timeline both compress. This runs counter to any investment case premised on a tight NAND supply environment.

- Chinese memory manufacturers are identified as a competitive threat capable of squeezing profits at Micron (MU) and SanDisk.
- The risk is framed as structural to the NAND demand cycle, not a one-off pricing episode.
- Geopolitical dynamics are cited as a driver of accelerating Chinese memory capacity.
- No specific margin figures or timelines are given in the headline; the story is directional.
