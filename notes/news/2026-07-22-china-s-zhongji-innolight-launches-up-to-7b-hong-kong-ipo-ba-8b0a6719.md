## China's Zhongji Innolight Launches Up to ~$7B Hong Kong IPO Backed by Alibaba, Tencent, and Temasek

A successful $7B IPO for a Chinese optical transceiver maker is a high-signal data point on the depth of AI infrastructure demand in China and the health of HK equity markets for deep-tech listings; BABA's cornerstone role reinforces its positioning as a key backer of domestic AI infrastructure, which is relevant to its cloud/AI thesis. If the deal prices well, it narrows the discount applied to China AI hardware names and could re-rate peers.

- Zhongji Innolight, China's leading optical transceiver and AI data-center component maker, is seeking up to approximately US$7 billion (HK$55 billion) in what headlines describe as Hong Kong's largest listing this year; one outlet cited $8B, but the majority converge on ~$7B.
- IPO priced at up to HK$1,010 per share across 54.5 million shares, with an expected July 30 debut.
- Alibaba (BABA), Tencent, and Temasek are among 33 named cornerstone investors, signaling strong institutional validation for the deal.
- FactSet sentiment is Positive; the scale and blue-chip anchor participation suggest strong demand for China AI infrastructure exposure via public markets.
- Innolight is described as Asia's second-largest player in its segment, positioning the IPO as a direct bet on AI data-center buildout in China.
