## Micron Commits $3 Billion to GlobalWafers to Secure AI-Era Silicon Wafer Supply

A $3B wafer supply commitment from MU is a bullish signal for AI memory demand durability and a strategic move to insulate against input-side bottlenecks — notably, it arrives on the same day Barron's frames MU as under existential competitive threat from Samsung and SK Hynix, making supply security a more urgent priority.

- MU is investing $3B in GlobalWafers to lock in silicon wafer supply for AI-era memory production.
- The move is framed as a proactive supply-chain de-risking action targeting upstream HBM and DRAM inputs.
- The $3B commitment signals MU's conviction in durable AI-driven memory demand requiring long-horizon capacity partnerships.
- This is a capital allocation decision to secure a critical input material, not an organic manufacturing expansion.
