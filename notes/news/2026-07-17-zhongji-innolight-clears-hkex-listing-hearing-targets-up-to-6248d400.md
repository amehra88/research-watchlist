## Zhongji Innolight clears HKEX listing hearing, targets up to $7B in Hong Kong's largest 2026 IPO

A $7B Chinese optical transceiver IPO is a structural competitive signal: a well-capitalized domestic champion could price-compress the global transceiver market over the medium term. Monitor whether the listing accelerates customer dual-sourcing conversations away from COHR and LITE.

- Chinese optical transceiver maker Zhongji Innolight cleared its HKEX listing hearing, advancing its Hong Kong IPO.
- The IPO target has been raised to approximately $7 billion, described in headlines as the largest Hong Kong debut of 2026.
- Zhongji Innolight manufactures optical transceivers — components critical to AI data center networking buildouts.
- The scale of the listing signals China is building domestic AI networking supply chain capacity materially, creating competitive pressure on Western incumbents COHR and LITE.
