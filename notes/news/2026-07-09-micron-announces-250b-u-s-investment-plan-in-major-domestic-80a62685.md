## Micron announces $250B U.S. investment plan in major domestic memory reshoring commitment

A $250B domestic commitment of this scale, if executed, would materially shift the U.S. memory supply chain and recalibrate Micron's competitive positioning versus SK Hynix and Samsung in HBM. For a tech PM, it raises both long-term capex execution risk and potential upside if HBM demand sustains.

- Micron disclosed a $250B U.S. investment plan, per the headline — scale suggests a multi-year domestic manufacturing and capacity buildout.
- Commitment signals Micron is repositioning as a domestic memory/HBM supplier, likely enabled by or aligned with federal incentives.
- Reshoring at this scale is a direct read on Micron's conviction in sustained HBM and advanced memory demand.
- Geopolitical context (export controls, China risk) appears to underpin the domestic orientation of the plan.
