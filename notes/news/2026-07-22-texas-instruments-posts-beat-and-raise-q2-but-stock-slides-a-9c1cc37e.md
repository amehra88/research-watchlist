## Texas Instruments Posts Beat-and-Raise Q2 but Stock Slides After Hours

When a beat-and-raise fails to hold the stock, it typically signals that expectations were already elevated or the market is skeptical of guidance durability. This is a caution flag for the broader semiconductor cycle and a read-through for other names where the bar may similarly be high.

- Texas Instruments reported Q2 results that beat estimates and raised forward guidance.
- Despite the positive outcome, the stock declined in after-hours trading.
- Two outlets (Investor's Business Daily, MSN) confirm both the beat-and-raise and the negative price reaction.
- A sell-the-news response to a beat-and-raise is a notable divergence between fundamentals and market behavior.
