## SK Hynix Raises Record $26.5B in ADR Issuance Targeting HBM Capex Gap

A record-scale ADR raise to fund HBM capex is a strong demand-durability signal, but the framing of a 'capex intensity gap' suggests returns-on-invested-capital risk is live; monitors for this thesis should track whether the capex translates to sustained HBM supply leadership or triggers an oversupply correction.

- SK Hynix completed what is described as a record ADR issuance of $26.5B.
- Proceeds are aimed at closing the company's capex intensity gap in HBM and memory infrastructure.
- The Futurum Group frames the raise as a question of whether it will be sufficient—execution risk is implicit.
- Scale of the equity raise simultaneously signals confidence in HBM demand durability and raises dilution considerations.
