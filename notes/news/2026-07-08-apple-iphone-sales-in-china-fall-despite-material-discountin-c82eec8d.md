## Apple iPhone Sales in China Fall Despite Material Discounting

If price elasticity is exhausted in China, AAPL faces a structural share-loss problem in its second-largest market — a more durable headwind than cyclical softness. This pressures both hardware revenue and the services monetization narrative built on an expanding installed base in China.

- Apple offered significant iPhone discounts in China, but sales volumes still declined, per Yahoo Finance.
- The inability of price cuts to recover volume suggests demand softness in China is structural rather than price-driven.
- Local competition or shifting consumer preferences are implied by the 'why sales still fell' framing, though no specific competitor is named.
- Discounting without volume response raises the prospect of simultaneous margin compression and revenue pressure in the China segment.
