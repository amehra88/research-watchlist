## Global Smartphone Sales Fall 11% in Q2; Apple and Samsung Retain Top Market Share Positions

An 11% YoY contraction in global smartphone volumes is a meaningful consumer demand signal; even with AAPL holding share, unit volume headwinds constrain iPhone revenue growth and dampen the handset upgrade cycle thesis for component suppliers downstream.

- Counterpoint Research data shows global smartphone unit sales fell 11% year-over-year in Q2.
- Apple and Samsung held their leading market share positions despite the aggregate demand contraction.
- The decline is a macro consumer-tech demand signal, not a company-specific share loss for either leader.
- Share retention in a contracting market points to competitive durability but does not offset volume headwinds.
