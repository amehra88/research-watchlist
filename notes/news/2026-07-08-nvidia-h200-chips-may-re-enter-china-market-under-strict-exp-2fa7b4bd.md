## Nvidia H200 chips may re-enter China market under strict export limits

China was a meaningful revenue contributor for NVDA before export controls; even a limited re-entry for H200 would be a material upside catalyst, but the 'strict limits' framing suggests incremental rather than structural relief — the durability of any revenue uplift depends on whether controls tighten again.

- Reports indicate NVDA's H200 GPU could be permitted to ship to China subject to strict licensing or usage constraints.
- The development is framed as a potential positive catalyst, with NVDA stock moving higher on the news.
- 'Strict limits' qualifier signals this is not an unrestricted reopening of the China market.
- Geopolitical and export control dynamics remain the governing constraint; no final regulatory ruling cited.
